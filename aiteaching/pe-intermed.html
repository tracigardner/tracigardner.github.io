As you venture into more sophisticated applications of AI in teaching and research, you will need more detailed and nuanced prompts. While simple prompts can suffice for basic tasks, complex academic endeavors often require a more comprehensive approach to prompt engineering. That’s where intermediate and advanced prompting come in.

In most cases, you will not need to create advanced prompting, unless your inquiry involves incredibly complex or sophisticated “asks.” The two types of prompting respond to very different needs.

The essential difference between intermediate and advanced prompting lies primarily in the complexity, sophistication, and level of control exercised in crafting the prompts. Here’s a brief comparison:

Intermediate Prompting

Intermediate prompting requires a good understanding of the AI’s capabilities. It involves incorporating more detail than you normally would in basic prompts and providing some context and specific instructions. The intermediate prompt often focuses on a single, moderately complex task and may include simple constraints.

Advanced Prompting

On the other hand, advanced prompting requires expert-level understanding of AI capabilities and limitations and employs highly sophisticated techniques, like chain-of-thought and role-based prompting. It is best used for multi-step or highly complex tasks. It could combine multiple prompting techniques; adds extra details, constraints, and precise specifications; and sometimes involves iterative refinement and careful calibration.

Despite the fact that these two styles seem very different, the line between them can be somewhat fluid. Advanced prompting often builds upon and combines intermediate techniques in more complex ways.

Here’s why detailed prompts are crucial:

  • Precision in Output: More detailed prompts allow you to specify exactly what you need, reducing ambiguity and increasing the likelihood of getting a response that meets your requirements.
  • Handling Complexity: Academic tasks often involve multiple steps, interdisciplinary concepts, or specific methodologies. Detailed prompts help break down these complex tasks into manageable components for the AI.
  • Contextual Understanding: By providing more context in your prompt, you enable the AI to better understand the academic framework within which it should operate, leading to more relevant and appropriate responses.
  • Quality Control: Specific instructions about the desired format, tone, or level of detail help ensure that the AI’s output aligns with academic standards and your pedagogical goals.
  • Encouraging Critical Thinking: Crafting detailed prompts requires educators to think critically about their objectives and the best way to achieve them, which can lead to improved lesson planning and assessment strategies.
  • Customization for Diverse Needs: Advanced prompts allow for tailoring AI responses to different learning styles, difficulty levels, or subject-specific requirements, enhancing the inclusivity and effectiveness of AI-assisted teaching.
  • Ethical Considerations: More detailed prompts can include guidelines on citing sources, avoiding bias, or addressing sensitive topics, helping to maintain academic integrity and ethical standards in AI-generated content.
  • Reproducibility and Consistency: Well-crafted, detailed prompts can be reused or slightly modified for different classes or semesters, ensuring consistency in the quality and relevance of AI-assisted materials over time.

By mastering the art of advanced prompting, educators can harness the full potential of AI as a powerful tool in their teaching arsenal, enabling more sophisticated and tailored educational experiences for their students.

Pro Prompting Tip: Since writing more complex prompts requires time, you should consider saving the ones you might need or revise in the future.
