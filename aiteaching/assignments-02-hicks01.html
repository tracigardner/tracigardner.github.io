AI in My World: A Personal Discovery

Assignment created by David Hicks (School of Education), using prompts with Claude AI

Rationale

This introductory assignment encourages students to explore generative AI in their daily lives, fostering curiosity and personal connection. By reflecting on their experiences, students begin to understand AI’s current capabilities, limitations, and societal impact.

Objectives

  1. Discover generative AI tools in personal or academic contexts.
  2. Critically analyze AI’s strengths and weaknesses.
  3. Reflect on ethical considerations and potential biases.

Assignment

  1. Choose two generative AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT, DALL-E, Midjourney) and interact with them in ways relevant to your life or studies.
  2. Document your interactions, noting tasks, prompts, and AI responses.
  3. Write a 500-word reflection addressing these questions:
    • What surprised you about the AI’s capabilities?
    • Where did the AI fall short or show limitations?
    • Did you notice any biases or ethical concerns?
    • How might this technology affect your field of study?

Expanded Task

Embark on a personal AI exploration journey. You’ll interact with two different AI tools, documenting your experience in a digital journal. Then, you’ll craft a reflective essay that not only describes your interactions but critically analyzes them through the lenses of capability, limitation, bias, and disciplinary impact.

Key Steps to Success:

  1. Tool Selection (Day 1)
    • Research 4–5 popular AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT, DALL-E, Jasper, Midjourney, GitHub Copilot).
    • Read reviews or academic papers on each tool’s strengths.
    • Choose two that intrigue you or relate to your field.
  2. Interaction Planning (Day 2–3)
    • For each tool, list 3–5 personally relevant tasks (e.g., essay brainstorming, code debugging).
    • Draft clear, specific prompts for each task.
    • Set aside uninterrupted time for AI interactions.
  3. Engaged Interaction (Day 4–6)
    • Use each tool for your chosen tasks.
    • Experiment with follow-up prompts based on initial responses.
    • Screenshot or copy all interactions into your digital journal.
    • Note immediate reactions: surprises, disappointments, questions.
  4. Critical Analysis (Day 7–9)
    • Review your journal entries.
    • For each tool, make a 4-column table: Capabilities, Limitations, Biases, Field Impact.
    • Fill in with specific examples from your interactions.
    • Consult AI ethics resources to deepen your bias analysis.
  5. Reflective Writing (Day 10–14)
    • Outline your 500-word essay using your table.
    • Draft, focusing on analytical depth over interaction summary.
    • Get peer feedback, especially from someone in your field.
    • Revise for clarity, critique, and discipline-specific insights.

500-Word Reflection

In my journey through the AI landscape, I’ve discovered tools with profound potential for music therapy—yet this potential is tempered by significant limitations and ethical considerations, particularly when working with vulnerable populations.

ChatGPT’s suggestions were insightful but initially lacked cultural nuance, which improved with specific prompting. Its adaptability—especially using GANs to generate culturally appropriate music—demonstrated interdisciplinary potential.

Midjourney showed similar promise. Generic visuals evolved into powerful therapeutic tools when guided by CBT-informed prompts, culminating in a manga-style score that resonated deeply with youth clients.

However, both tools revealed problematic biases—defaulting to white, able-bodied figures—a reminder of AI’s current limitations and the necessity of diverse datasets (Buolamwini & Gebru, 2018).

Overall, this experience highlighted AI’s potential as an ally in music therapy when critically and ethically engaged. We must lead the human-machine partnership with awareness and empathy.
