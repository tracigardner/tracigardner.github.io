Prompt Engineering 101: Learning to Talk to AI

Assignment Overview

In this unit, you will learn the fundamentals of prompt engineering—the practice of crafting clear, purposeful instructions for generative AI tools like Copilot. You'll explore how different types of prompts produce different results, practice writing effective prompts, and create your own prompt-driven AI project.

Learning Goals

  • Explain what prompts are and why they matter in AI writing tools.
  • Identify different types of prompts and their effects.
  • Apply a simple framework for writing effective prompts.
  • Write and refine your own AI prompts for specific goals and audiences.

Part 2: Prompt Framework: The CLEAR Model

Use the CLEAR framework to build better prompts:

  • Context: Set the scene or background for the task.
  • Language: Specify the tone or style.
  • End goal: Make your desired output or task clear.
  • Audience: Tell the AI who the output is for (optional but helpful).
  • Restrictions: Add any rules or constraints.

🧠 Example:

Prompt: “You are a technical writing tutor. Explain how to write effective instructions in plain language, using examples. Keep your explanation concise (under 150 words), and use bullet points.”

✍️ Exercise 2: Tone Shift

Write prompts that explain the same complex topic (e.g., quantum computing) to:

  • A middle school student
  • A college science major
  • A skeptical policymaker

Compare the differences.

✍️ Exercise 3: Creative Control

  • A short horror story (200 words)
  • A cover letter for a job in marketing
  • A list of 3 AI tools for students with pros/cons
