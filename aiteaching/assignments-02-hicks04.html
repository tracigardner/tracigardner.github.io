Assignment created by David Hicks (School of Education), using prompts with Claude AI

This assignment is the fourth week of a four-week series focused on learning about generative AI.

Rationale

This capstone assignment empowers students to become AI mentors within their disciplines. By designing workshops, students deeply engage with AI’s potential in their fields, preparing them to lead responsibly in AI-integrated workplaces.

Objectives

  1. Master prompt engineering in a discipline-specific context.
  2. Evaluate AI’s impact on professional practices.
  3. Design learning experiences to guide peers.

  5. Have students follow this schedule as they work:
    1. Discipline Foresight (Day 1–3)
      • Interview 2–3 professors or industry pros in your field.
      • Ask: “How might AI reshape our discipline in 5 years?”
      • Study 2–3 recent papers on AI in your field.
      • List 5–7 transformative AI applications, rank by impact.
    2. Application Deep Dives (Day 4–6)
      • Pick your top 3–5 applications.
      • For each, use AI to suggest implementation steps.
      • Example (Astronomy): “Detail steps to use AI for exoplanet atmosphere analysis.”
      • Refine each prompt 3–5 times, aiming for innovation.
      • Consult field experts to validate AI’s suggestions.
    3. Prompt Engineering Mastery (Day 7–9)
      • For each application, craft 3 prompt variants:
        • Basic: State the task simply.
        • Advanced: Add field-specific constraints and contexts.
        • Expert: Layer in latest research, edge cases, and ethical concerns.
      • A/B test each variant, noting differences.
      • Create a field-specific “Prompt Engineering Guide.”
    4. Rigorous Output Evaluation (Day 10–11)
      • For each AI-generated solution:
        • Fact-check against recent journals.
        • Run it by a subject expert.
        • Apply it to a real-world problem.
      • Create an “AI Output Evaluation Rubric” with criteria like accuracy, innovation, and ethical alignment.
      • Grade each output, reflect on what makes responses excel or fall short.
    5. Workshop Design (Day 12–13)
      • Structure your 30-minute workshop:
        • “AI Horizon” (5 min): Your field’s AI-driven future.
        • “Prompt Mastery” (15 min): Guide peers through crafting expert prompts in real-time collaborative exercises.
        • “Critique Corner” (5 min): Apply your rubric to assess outputs.
        • “Ethics Epilogue” (5 min): Discuss AI’s ethical dimensions in your profession.
      • Design an interactive handout or digital worksheet.
      • Plan a mini “AI Ethics Oath” drafting activity.
    6. Workshop Delivery & Reflection (Day 14)
      • Run your workshop for peers or a student club.
      • Gather feedback via a short survey.
      • Write your 500-word “AI’s Promise & Perils” essay, incorporating:
        • Workshop insights
        • Personal views on AI’s field impact
        • Call to action for responsible innovation
  6. Submit the following materials:
    • Workshop plan and materials
    • Prompt library with explanations
    • 500-word essay on AI’s promises and perils in your field

Biology Student’s Workshop Example

Task

Design gene-editing CRISPR experiments

Prompt

As a CRISPR expert, design a step-by-step experiment to edit the BCRA1 gene in human cell lines to study breast cancer resistance, including control groups and ethical considerations.

Workshop Intro

In our field, AI can design complex experiments and foresee ethical issues. However, it may miss cutting-edge techniques not in its training data. Today, we’ll co-design CRISPR experiments with AI, focusing on robust prompts that specify controls and ethics.
