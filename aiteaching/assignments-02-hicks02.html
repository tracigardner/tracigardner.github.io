Rationale

This assignment introduces students to prompt engineering and encourages them to view AI as a collaborative tool. By iteratively refining prompts, students learn to guide AI outputs effectively while maintaining their own voice and creativity.

Objectives

  1. Learn basic prompt engineering techniques.
  2. Use AI as a brainstorming and drafting partner.
  3. Distinguish between AI-generated content and personal contributions.

Assignment

  1. Choose a project in your discipline (e.g., research proposal, design concept, policy brief).
  2. Use an AI tool to brainstorm ideas, providing an initial prompt.
  3. Refine your prompt at least three times based on AI responses, aiming for better outputs.
  4. Create your project, incorporating AI suggestions but adding your own ideas as well.
  5. Submit the following materials:
    • Your project
    • A log of prompts and AI responses
    • A 300-word statement detailing how you guided the AI and where you added your own touch

Expanded Task

In this assignment, you’ll engage in a creative tango with AI. Choose a project that’s quintessentially “you” in your discipline. Then, use AI as your brainstorming partner, refining your prompts through several iterations. The goal is to guide the AI’s outputs while ensuring your unique voice shines through.

Key Steps to Success

  1. Project Conception (Day 1–2)
    • List 3 project ideas quintessential to your interests and discipline.
    • Note key components and your initial vision for each.
    • Choose the one that most excites you.
  2. AI Partner Selection (Day 3)
    • Choose the AI tool that best fits your project.
    • If unsure, experiment with 2–3 tools briefly and select the most responsive.
  3. First Dance (Day 4–5)
    • Write an initial prompt describing your project idea.
    • Example: “I’m creating an eco-friendly fashion line inspired by bioluminescent creatures.”
    • Note AI’s response and reflect on its understanding.
  4. Prompt Refinement I (Day 6–7)
    • Refine your prompt with more specifics and constraints.
    • Example: “Design a dress inspired by Aequorea victoria jellyfish. Focus on translating its green fluorescent protein into sustainable, light-emitting textiles.”
  5. Prompt Refinement II (Day 8–9)
    • Further refine by layering in emotional tone or deeper context.
    • Example: “The dress should embody ocean conservation. Include references to plastic waste. Aesthetic: haute couture meets environmental activism.”
  6. Final Collaboration (Day 10–11)
    • Perfect the AI’s contribution.
    • Merge AI’s ideas with your own. Decide what to modify, amplify, or contradict.
  7. Creation & Reflection (Day 12–14)
    • Create the final project.
    • Write your 300-word reflection using dance metaphors. When did you lead? When did AI?

300-Word Reflection:

In reimagining Detroit with ChatGPT, our collaboration evolved from a halting waltz to a soulful jazz duet, where structure met improvisation. At first, the AI played standard urban planning tunes—convert factories to lofts, add green spaces. These weren’t wrong notes, but they lacked Detroit’s unique timbre.

I then shared Detroit’s sheet music: its auto industry roots, Motown’s soul, its predominantly Black population. Suddenly, ChatGPT found the rhythm. Its suggestion to transform old assembly lines into maker spaces wasn’t just adaptive reuse; it was honoring the hands that built the city. Proposing Motown studios become music education hubs showed it grasped that Detroit’s arts aren’t relics but living inspirations.

As our duet intensified, I introduced Detroit’s "shrinking city" challenge—vast spaces, sparse population, rising heat. Many would see only urban decay, but the AI heard opportunity in the rests between notes. It riffed brilliantly: "Mobility Hubs" in old dealerships, wide roads becoming shaded "Cool Corridors." Most strikingly, it proposed monorails on factory rails—a transport solution that sings Detroit’s industrial heritage.

Our collaboration crescendoed when I pushed for social rebirth, emphasizing Detroit’s racial wealth gap and civil rights legacy. Here, ChatGPT’s response was a standing ovation. "Motown Microgrants" named for Black music icons wasn’t mere financial aid; it was cultural empowerment. Suggesting we convert rights landmarks into youth-managed museums showed deep respect for history and community. The "Own Your Note" housing idea, where payments build equity like a growing composition, wasn’t just innovative finance; it was poetry in policy form.

Throughout our jam session, the AI consistently surprised me. When I shared Detroit’s 80% Black population, it didn’t just note demographics; it saw a canvas for Black artists’ murals. Given the city’s vast spaces, it envisioned not abandonment but a stage for grand ideas like edge-city agriculture.

In urban planning, we’re taught that cities aren’t just built; they’re composed, with each element a note in a greater symphony. What struck me about our collaboration was how attuned the AI became to Detroit’s unique composition. It didn’t just solve problems; it found harmonies between past and future, struggle and strength.

This experience reshaped my view of AI in urban planning. It’s not merely a data processor or idea generator. At its best, when guided by human context and values, AI can be a co-composer—one that helps us hear the music in a city’s very streets. In Detroit’s case, that’s a phoenix’s song, rising from industrial ashes, powered by soulful, generative harmony.
