Rationale

This assignment invites students to reflect on their learning and to consider how they will engage with AI in their future academic or professional work. Through journaling, essay writing, and class discussion, students will articulate what they’ve learned, explore future possibilities for AI in their fields, and develop personal plans for ethical and informed AI use.

Objectives

  • Reflect on the knowledge and skills gained.
  • Consider future AI developments.
  • Develop a personal plan for ethical AI use.

Activities and Steps

  1. Readings
    • Bridle, James. 2023. “The Stupidity of AI.” The Guardian, March 16, 2023, sec. Technology. https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2023/mar/16/the-stupidity-of-ai-artificial-intelligence-dall-e-chatgpt.
    • Gawrylewski, Andrea. 2025. “The AI Future Is Here.” Scientific American. March 1, 2025. https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/the-ai-future-is-here/.
    • Piper, Kelsey. 2025. “AI 2027 Forecasts a World Utterly Changed by Artificial Intelligence.” Vox. May 23, 2025. https://www.vox.com/future-perfect/414087/artificial-intelligence-openai-ai-2027-china.
    • Mucci, Tim. 2024. ’The Future of Artificial Intelligence.’ October 11, 2024. https://www.ibm.com/think/insights/artificial-intelligence-future.
    • Romero, Alberto. 2025. “Fear Wins.” The Algorithmic Bridge (blog). April 10, 2025. https://www.thealgorithmicbridge.com/p/fear-wins.
  2. Discussion Questions
    Use these questions in small group discussions or online forums to explore the readings before students begin their projects.

    • How has your understanding of AI evolved since the start of the course? What surprised you the most?
    • Where do you see AI having the most immediate impact in your field of study or future career? Are those changes promising or concerning to you? Why?
    • Think about an AI tool you used this semester. How did it influence your writing, thinking, or decision-making process? Would you use it differently now?
    • What ethical challenges (e.g., data privacy, bias, misinformation, labor displacement) seem most urgent to you in the context of your major or profession?
    • What responsibilities do individuals, especially students and early-career professionals, have when it comes to using AI tools ethically and effectively?
    • In your view, what role should colleges or instructors play in guiding students’ understanding and use of AI technologies? Are we doing enough?
    • How do you plan to stay informed about developments in AI? What kind of boundaries or guidelines do you think you’ll need to set for yourself as a user?
  3. Reflective Journal
    1. Keep a journal throughout the course, documenting your learning experiences, challenges, and reflections.
    2. Reflect on how your understanding of AI has evolved.
    3. Assessment: Journals will be reviewed periodically for depth of reflection and engagement.
  4. Future Directions Essay
    1. Reflect on the course content and your journal entries.
    2. Write an essay discussing potential future developments in AI related to your field, referring to the rubric for assessment expectations.
    3. Outline a personal plan for staying informed and using AI ethically.

Model Future Directions Essay

The Future of AI in Education: Ethical Considerations and Personal Reflections

Introduction

Throughout this course, I have gained a deeper understanding of the potential and challenges of artificial intelligence (AI) in education. This essay reflects on my learning journey and considers the future implications of AI, focusing on ethical considerations and my personal plan for responsible AI use.

Learning Journey

At the beginning of the course, I had a basic understanding of AI but little awareness of its practical applications and ethical challenges. The assignments and discussions expanded my knowledge, highlighting AI’s transformative potential in personalized learning, intelligent tutoring systems, and educational content creation. I learned how to interact with AI tools, craft effective prompts, and evaluate AI systems critically.

Future Implications of AI in Education

AI will continue to shape the educational landscape, offering increasingly sophisticated tools for personalized learning and administrative efficiency. Potential developments include:

  • Advanced Tutoring Systems: AI-driven tutors will provide real-time feedback and adapt instruction to individual student needs, enhancing learning outcomes.
  • Predictive Analytics: AI will analyze student data to predict academic performance and identify at-risk students, enabling timely interventions.
  • Content Creation: AI will assist in developing interactive and engaging educational content, catering to diverse learning styles.
Ethical Considerations

As AI becomes more integrated into education, ethical considerations must guide its development and use. Key issues include:

  • Data Privacy: Ensuring student data is protected from unauthorized access and misuse.
  • Bias and Fairness: Addressing biases in AI models to promote equitable learning opportunities for all students.
  • Transparency: Making AI algorithms and decision-making processes transparent to educators and students.
Personal Plan for Ethical AI Use

To stay informed and use AI responsibly, I plan to:

  1. Continue Learning: Stay updated on AI developments through professional development courses, conferences, and research publications.
  2. Advocate for Ethical Practices: Promote data privacy, bias mitigation, and transparency in AI applications within my educational institution.
  3. Engage in Dialogue: Participate in discussions with colleagues, students, and the broader community about the ethical implications of AI, encouraging critical thinking and informed decision-making.
Conclusion

AI has the potential to revolutionize education, but its integration must be approached with careful consideration of ethical issues. Reflecting on my learning journey, I am committed to staying informed and advocating for responsible AI use. By prioritizing data privacy, fairness, and transparency, we can harness AI’s power to create more effective and equitable educational environments.
