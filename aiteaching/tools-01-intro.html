AI Tools

Incorporating AI into teaching, research, and creative work begins with knowing what tools are available and what they can do. As educators and scholars, we are increasingly called upon to evaluate, experiment with, and integrate emerging technologies. A practical understanding of current AI tools can help us make informed choices—whether we are designing assignments, streamlining workflows, or supporting student innovation.

This curated collection offers a practical entry point into the world of generative AI, showcasing a diverse range of tools organized by function, from writing and search to image generation and course design. Each tool is listed with a brief description and direct link, making it easy to explore, compare, and choose the right platform for your teaching or research goals.

As you browse the collection, you might encounter tools that:

  • Support writing and editing processes
  • Enhance visual and multimedia design
  • Generate summaries, search results, or translations
  • Help students visualize ideas or prepare presentations

Many tools, like ChatGPT or Midjourney, may already be familiar names. Others, such as Claude, Copilot, or Leonardo.ai, offer alternative capabilities or specialized features that may better align with your course needs or institutional policies. Whether you’re new to generative AI or seeking alternatives with specific strengths, this section is designed to support experimentation and informed adoption.

By familiarizing yourself with the range of available tools, you empower yourself and your students to approach AI critically, creatively, and ethically. This knowledge also equips you to set appropriate expectations, craft meaningful assignments, and foster responsible AI use in the classroom and beyond.
