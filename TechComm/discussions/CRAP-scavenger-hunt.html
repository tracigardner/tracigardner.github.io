Goals

  • Collaborate with classmates in planning, researching, writing, revising, and presenting information. [CLO 5]
  • Apply principles of effective visual design for print and electronic presentation, including hierarchical, chronological, and spatial arrangements. [CLO 6]

The Task

What I Want You to Do

Find an image as you go about your day-to-day life, and then analyze the image to see how it applies (or doesn’t) the CRAP Design Principles.

Why I Want You to Do It

Document design matters to all technical writing projects. Your projects need to use design to make a good first impression and to use design to make your technical writing easy to navigate and read. I want you to apply the CRAP Design Principles to an image that you choose to begin practicing how to apply the principles, which you will use as you work on your Pitch Presentation.

How You Do It

  1. Go on a hunt on campus, wherever you live, or online for a good or bad visual. It can be any kind of visual—a digital sign, a full-page ad in a magazine, a billboard, and so forth.
    • Here’s one way to find a visual for this discussion: Find a bulletin board on campus. Stand across the hall from it, and identify the one piece on the bulletin board that grabs your attention.
  2. Ensure that the visual you find meets the VT Principles of Community standards.
  3. Take a photo of the visual you find, or save the visual if you found it online.
  4. Upload the image to this Discussion, using the Images button (shown below) in the Editing Toolbar:
  5. Add a paragraph that tells us why it is a good visual or a bad one, referring to the CRAP Design Principles.
  6. Comment on or Like posts by your classmates.

Obtaining the Points for Your Work

You track and grade your own work in this course. Each week, be sure to do the following::

  • Track your work in your Weekly Work Log.
  • Claim points for participating in this Discussion when you complete the weekly self-assessment  in Canvas.
