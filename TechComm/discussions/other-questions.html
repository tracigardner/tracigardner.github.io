Goals

  • Collaborate with classmates in planning, researching, writing, revising, and presenting information. [CLO 5]
  • Analyze your draft and find ways to improve your project using strategies related to technical writing [CLO 1–7]

The Task

What I Want You to Do

Participate in this Discussion three times during the next three weeks (from April 12 to April 30). This activity gives you more choice than previous Discussions. You can do of the following:

  • Post a question or comment about the Recommendation Report.
  • Post a meme that relates to what you are working on for the course.
  • Post a link to a resource you have found that could help someone else with the project (with an explanation of what it is and why).
  • Respond to any of those things that have been posted.

Why I Want You to Do It

One of the suggestions to improve feedback was to have a place where you can ask questions that come up that don't require you to post a draft. This forum lets you post those kinds of questions whenever they come up.

How You Do It

  1. Write a post, if you like. As noted above, you can post any of the following:
    • A question or comment.
    • A meme that relates to what you are working on for the course.
    • A link that could help someone else with the project (with an explanation of what it is and why).
    • Anything else that seems relevant.
  2. Read over your message to make sure it’s clear, and make any changes you need to make sure your group understands your message.
  3. Post your message.
  4. Check in by read through the posts at least twice a week (by Wednesday at 11: 59 PM and by Friday at 11: 59 PM).
  5. Comment on or Like posts by your classmates, if you like.
  6. If someone replies to your post, respond to their reply if you like.

Obtaining the Points for Your Work

This Discussion is worth 15 points overall. You can earn the following:

  • 3 points for each of three different participations (posts and/or replies), for a total of 9 points.
  • 2 points for checking in twice a week, for a total of 6 points.

To track and grade your work, be sure to do the following:

  • Track your work in your Weekly Work Log.
  • Claim your points:
    • For checking in twice a week in the weekly self-assessments each week (for the week of 4/12, 4/19, and 4/26).
    • For your three different participations in the weekly self-assessment for the week of 4/26.
