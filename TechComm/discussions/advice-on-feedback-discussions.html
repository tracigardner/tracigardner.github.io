What is the point of the Feedback Discussions?
These Discussions are essentially peer review. You share your draft with others in the class. You give two people feedback, and you get feedback from two people. It’s like asking a coworker to read over your draft and make suggestions.
How do the deadlines work for Feedback Discussions?

Feedback Discussions have a hard and firm due date. To participate, you must post your draft by 11:59 PM on the due date. Feedback Discussions in the course must be completed on time—because of limits in Canvas and because your classmates’ work depends upon it. The system has to set up the people who will read your work.

Are there extensions?
Six of the Feedback Discussions come in two rounds: a first round, and a second-chance round. If you miss the first round, you can submit to the second round (it works as the extension for people who can't make the first round). There are no extensions to the second round (or Second-Chance) discussions. 
Are there makeups?
Sort of. For the feedback discussions, you only need to complete the first round or the second-chance round. I expect you to only complete about 1/2 of the feedback discussions. There is an additional Last-Chance Feedback Discussion you can complete if you want. It can serve as a make-up in the event you miss both rounds of one of the Discussions.
How do I submit my draft?
Click reply in the Feedback Discussion, and then upload the file for your draft by clicking on the Attach link in the lower left corner of the Reply form. If your draft is in Google Docs, paste the share link in the body of your message. If you need step-by-step instructions, use the documentation at How do I attach a file to a discussion reply as a student?
How do I find my peer review partners?

Canvas assigns peer review partners after the due date for the Discussion. You will find an indication of Discussions you need to review in the To Do list on the course Home page and at the top of the Discussion itself. If you need more help, use the documentation at How do I know if I have a peer review discussion to complete?

How do I give someone feedback?

Read through your partner’s draft and cover letter. Next, add a general comment in the right sidebar, and complete the rubric to provide specific suggestions. Click the Show Rubric link at the top right to access the feedback rubric. For more specific directions, use the documentation at How do I submit a peer review to a discussion?

What do I need for my Feedback Submission to be marked Complete?
A Complete on a Feedback Discussion requires three things:
  1. Upload your draft which matches the assignment.
  2. Read your first partner’s draft, add a comment, and fill out the rubric.
  3. Read your second partner’s draft, add a comment, and fill out the rubric.
You must finish all three of these tasks before your Discussion is marked Complete. Because your partners are waiting for your feedback, you must finish these tasks by the listed due date for feedback.
