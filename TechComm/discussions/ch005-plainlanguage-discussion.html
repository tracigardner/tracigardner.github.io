Plain language helps make documents more accessible, especially for public-facing audiences who may not have technical expertise. For this discussion, you’ll apply your understanding of plain language techniques to

  • evaluate the clarity and usability of a piece of writing you choose, and
  • revise it to make it easier for the average reader to understand.

Learning Objectives

By completing this discussion, you will:

  • Practice identifying writing that poses accessibility or clarity challenges for readers.
  • Apply plain language principles to revise confusing or overly complex writing.
  • Reflect on the effectiveness of your own and others’ revisions.
  • Practice giving and receiving constructive, respectful feedback in writing.

Assignment Directions

Part 1. Initial Post (Approx. 250–300 words)

Find a short piece of public-facing writing (around 1-2 short paragraphs) that you believe would be unclear or inaccessible to the average reader. This passage could be a passage from a website, an email, a syllabus, or any document intended for a general audience.

Do not choose private or sensitive content. Likewise do not choose anything that I (Traci) have written, because that gets weird.

In your post, include the following:

  1. Original Text: Paste the original confusing or inaccessible passage.
  2. Context: Briefly explain what the passage is, where it came from, and what its purpose appears to be.
  3. Evaluation (Answer all):
    1. What specifically makes this passage hard to understand?
    2. Who might struggle with it, and why?
    3. What key plain language principles are missing or violated?
  4. Your Plain Language Revision: Revise the passage using plain language techniques.

Part 2. Peer Response (Approx. 100–150 words)

Read a classmate’s post and offer feedback on their revised version (not the original). Your goal is to help them evaluate and improve their use of plain language.

Respond to at least one classmate and address the following questions:

  1. Does their revision improve clarity and accessibility? Why or why not?
  2. Can you suggest any additional changes or refinements?
  3. What do you admire about how they revised the passage?

Be specific and supportive. Aim to help your peer reflect on their choices and feel encouraged to revise further.
