Goals

  • Analyze the rhetorical situation and determine the appropriate audience or users of written communication, considering the needs of global audiences and people with disabilities. [CLO 1]
  • Conduct research appropriate to workplace problem solving, such as literature review, evaluation of online resources, interview, and site inspection. [CLO 2]
  • Use conventions of various workplace genres, such as proposals, instructions, correspondence, reports, and slide decks, with understanding of how the genre conventions can be used as heuristics and as principles of arrangement. [CLO 4]

The Task

What I Want You to Do

Review the webpages that outline the connections among the assignments in this course, and post a reply that tells me what you have questions about.

Why I Want You to Do It

I designed the sequence of assignments in this course to make the activities “busy work free.” The writing projects all relate to the culminating recommendation report. I am asking for feedback this week to make sure you understand the connections among the assignments. I want my goals for you to be transparent. I believe that if you understand why you are doing something you can do a better job reaching your goal.

How You Do It

  1. Review the Detailed Sequence of Projects webpage and the Recommendation Report Criteria webpage, which outline the connections among the assignments in this course.
  2. Click the Reply button below to make a space for your response. Only you and I will be able to read what you write.
  3. Write a message that answers one or more of these questions:
    • What is the muddiest point about the sequence of projects for you?
    • What don't you understand or want clarified about the sequence?
    • What do you need to do your best work on the sequence of projects?
  4. Read over your message to make sure it’s clear, and make any changes you need to make sure I understand your message.
  5. Post your reply.
  6. Allow me time to respond. If a lot of people ask the same question, I will reply in an FAQ for the whole class. For personal questions, I will reply to your Discussion post in Canvas.

Obtaining the Points for Your Work

You track and grade your own work in this course. Be sure to do the following::

  • Track your work in your Weekly Work Log.
  • Claim points for replying when you complete the weekly self-assessment  in Canvas.
