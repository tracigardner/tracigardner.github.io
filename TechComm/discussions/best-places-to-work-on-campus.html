What You Will Learn and Practice

  • Collaborate with classmates in planning, researching, writing, revising, and presenting information. [CLO 5]

What I Want You to Do

Recommend your favorite public place to do course work on campus. Share details about the location that tell the reader how to find it and make it clear to readers why it is a good place to work.

Why I Want You to Do It

By the end of this term, you will write a recommendation report that describes and suggests solutions to a problem on campus, online, in a workplace, or in your community. This discussion serves three purposes:

  1. It asks you to consider what makes a good recommendation.
  2. It works as a discussion starter that helps us get to know one another.
  3. It shares information that may help you find new places to work.

Where You Can Find Help

  • From Markel & Selber, Chapter 8, “Communicating Persuasively” (PDF):
    • Using the Right Kinds of Evidence, pp. 184–186
    • Using Visuals as Persuasive Elements, pp. 186–187
    • Appealing to Emotions Responsibly, pp. 187–188

When to Do It

  • End of Grace Period: If you need more time, take more time. Complete this activity by 11:59 PM on Friday, December 3, 2021.

How You Do It

  1. Review the information on “Communicating Persuasively” from the textbook.
  2. Consider the details that you expect to find in a good recommendation.
  3. Add a reply with your recommendation. Focus on a place that anyone in the class can work (in other words, not your dorm room or apartment).
  4. Be sure that your recommendation shares details about the location that tell the reader how to find it and make it clear to readers why it is a good place to work.
  5. Read through the responses of your classmates, responding to their recommendations and/or liking them.
