Designing for Reading Patterns

The two most common reading patterns are the Z-pattern and the F-pattern. These names come from shape of the way the reader’s eyes move across a page or screen.

Z-Pattern (Visual + Immediate)

The front page should quickly grab attention and guide the reader through the main idea.

How Readers Move

LEFT → RIGHT
     ↘
LEFT → RIGHT

Suggested Layout

Top Left
Logo or organization name		 Top Right
Title or headline
Center
Image, graphic, or key statistic
Bottom Left
Short description		 Bottom Right
Key takeaway or action

Purpose

  • Introduce the topic quickly
  • Highlight the main idea
  • Encourage the reader to continue

F-Pattern (Structured + Scannable)

The back page provides more detailed information. Readers will scan rather than read everything.

How Readers Move

LEFT → RIGHT
LEFT → RIGHT (shorter)
↓
↓

Suggested Layout

Top Section
Main heading + key facts
Middle Section
Subheading + short explanation
Lower Section
Additional details or data

Example Content Structure

  • Key Facts
    • Bullet point
    • Bullet point
  • Why It Matters
    • Short paragraph with important terms
  • Additional Information
    • Extra details or statistics

Purpose

  • Help readers find information quickly
  • Break content into clear sections
  • Make scanning easy
