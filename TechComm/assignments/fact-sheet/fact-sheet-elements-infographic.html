Writing an Effective Fact Sheet

Your Task

Worth 10% of your course grade

Convert content from your Informational Report for Non-Expert Readers to a one-page (front and back) fact sheet that informs or educates an audience of non-expert readers about your topic in a way that is

  • Visually engaging.
  • Easy to understand (using plain language).
  • Accessible to a general audience.
  • Focused on key facts, practical relevance, and clear visuals.

Your fact sheet should help everyday people understand what’s at stake, how the issue may affect them, and what they can do (if anything) in response.

Review Document Samples and the Deliverable Checklist as you work.
Use details from Chapter 15: Fact Sheets & Other Visual Documents.

Assignment Learning Objectives

Hide the Learning Objectives Show the Learning Objectives

By completing this assignment, you will develop your ability to:

  • Practice adapting technical content for a new audience with different needs and expectations.
  • Apply plain language principles to communicate clearly and effectively.
  • Identify and highlight key facts and takeaways from a longer informational report.
  • Use visual design techniques (headings, bullets, charts, icons, etc.) to improve readability and engagement.
  • Create a one-page (front and back) document that informs or educates a general public audience.
  • Reinforce your understanding of how technical writing can support community service, problem-solving, or public good.
  • Gain experience in designing public-facing documents that are both informative and usable.

Your Audience

Design your fact sheet for a new audience of non-experts: the constituents or community members served by the decision-makers you addressed in your report.

Understand the difference:

  • Audience for your informational report: non-expert leaders who need to decide something. They want a thorough, objective document.
    Example: Town of Blacksburg Mayor & Town Council
  • Audience for your fact sheet: non-expert, everyday people who the leaders serve and who want to know more about the topic. They want a shorter, less complex document.
    Example: People who live and work in the Town of Blacksburg

Assignment Requirements

Basic Requirements

  • Length: 1 page (front and back). No longer and no shorter. Take up the full front and back. Make sure you do not include a blank third page.
  • Software Options & Required File Format: Whatever software you are comfortable with (e.g., Canva, Word), as long as you can export your fact sheet as a PDF (required file format).

Fact Sheet Requirements

  • Required Text Components: title; byline; date; introduction or summary; key facts or data points; and conclusion. See Writing an Effective Fact Sheet for help.
  • Headings/Subheadings: Body sections should have headings/subheadings. No heading for the intro. Conclusion use a relevant heading (e.g., Action Steps, or Key Takeaways).

Formatting Requirements

  • Page size: 8.5 x 11 (portrait)
  • Document Layout: Portrait orientation (8.5 X 11) required. Consistent margin on all sides (Minimum of .5"; maximum of 1"). No page numbers.
  • Spacing: Single or 1.15 spacing between lines; double spacing between paragraphs (1 blank line)
  • Paragraphs: Do not indent
  • Font Choices: Arial, Calibri, Verdana, Aptos, or Helvetica—but just one! Don’t mix fonts.
  • Font Sizes: Be consistent. Headings should be larger than the body text. Each heading level should be larger than the next lower heading (e.g., Heading 1 is larger than Heading 2; Heading 2 is larger than Heading 3, and so on).

    TIP: The default heading sizes in Word and Google Docs follow this guideline automatically.
