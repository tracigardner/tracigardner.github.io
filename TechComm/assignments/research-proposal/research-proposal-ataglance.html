Research Proposal at a Glance

Table 1. Research Proposal at a Glance
Category Explanation & Details
Weight 20% of your course grade
Genre Memo. Your proposal should look like a workplace document (not an academic document).
Topic Proposing your subject for the Informational Report of Non-Expert Readers. Not sure about your subject for the report? See the Test Your Informational Report Subject & Audience announcement.
Formatting Memo format. Single-spaced. Skip a line between paragraphs AND between a paragraph and the next heading. Do not indent paragraphs. See the Formatting Requirements section of the assignment for full details.
Length Usually 4–5 pages. I don’t count pages so don’t get hung up here if you’rer slightly short or a little long.
Audience Me (Traci). You’re convincing me to tell you to move on to your Informational Report.
Language Use Plain Language. Short sentences and other plain language techniques. Avoid jargon. Professional tone.
Citation Style Use the style preferred by your discipline (e.g., engineers use IEEE, biologists use CSE). See Citation and style manuals for help.
Outside Sources At least 5 sources. Can be academic journal articles, credible websites, and government reports.
Visuals Gantt Chart required. Can include additional, relevant figures, charts, or tables.
Document Design Headings, lists, paragraph chunking, white space. Contrast between headings and document paragraphs. Attention to CRAP design principles.
Recycling Reuse relevant information from your Subject Selection Message.
Assessment See the rubric at the bottom of the assignment.
