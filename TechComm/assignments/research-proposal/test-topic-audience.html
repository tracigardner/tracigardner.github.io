Testing the Subject & Audience for Your Informational Report

I asked a lot of you to narrow the subject and audience for your Informational Report. You need to focus more BEFORE you submit your Research Proposal. If your subject and audience are not on target, you’ll need to revise your proposal.

Use this process to test your subject and audience. You must be able to answer “Yes” to every question to have a subject that works.

The Major Test

Answer the Test Questions

  • Does your subject specifically relate to your major?
  • Is it a person in your career field knows about?

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the Technical Topic Test.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a subject that does.

Example Response to Test Question

Yes. I’m majoring in Technical & Scientific Communication. I’m focusing on copyright, intellectual property rights, and Creative Commons. Those concepts all relate to my major since the communication I do needs to respect legal ownership laws. Additionally, things I write will be protected by these guidelines and laws.

    It’s an object in my field.

    B.
    It’s a practice in my field.

    C.
    It’s an idea in my field.

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next step.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a subject that does.

Example Response to Test Question

Yes. I’m focusing on copyright, intellectual property rights, and Creative Commons. These concepts are all practices in my field, writing and creation of digital media. Majors in my field apply these ownership and licensing guidelines to their work in order to control the use and reuse of their work.

The Ut Prosim Test

Answer the Test Questions

  • Does your subject have the potential to serve others and contribute to the greater good?

  • Does it have the potential to create a positive social impact?

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next step.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a subject that does.

Example Response to Test Questions

Yes. Helping others understand how copyright, intellectual property rights, and Creative Commons work is a positive service to artists, writers, and digital makers. These concepts give people a way to protect their ownership of things they create. Additionally, violating someone else’s copyrights can result in costly fines (and sometimes embarrassment). By explaining these concepts, I can help others protect their work and avoid costly errors.

The Writing Project Test

Answer the Test Questions

  • Is your subject something you can write a report on with the goal of informing, guiding, or supporting action that contributes to fairness, accessibility, and the well-being of communities?
  • Is it a subject you can write about in a way that allows you to live out Virginia Tech’s Ut Prosim motto?

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next step.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a subject that does.

Example Response to Test Question

Yes. Copyright, intellectual property rights, and Creative Commons are complicated concepts, and people often misunderstand how they work. I can write documents that explain what the concepts are as well as projects that show people how to add copyright or Creative Commons licenses to their work. I can serve others by writing about these concepts in ways that will give them the power to protect their creative work.

The Non-Expert Audience Test

Answer the Test Questions

  • Do most readers lack formal training in this subject?
  • Will most readers need definitions for common jargon or acronyms related to this topic?

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next step.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a subject that does.

Example Response to Test Question

Copyright and licensing impact every artist in Maroon + Orange Digital’s Open Artists’ Studio, where collaboration and sharing are part of daily work. Guidance on intellectual property rights and Creative Commons licensing must be clear and accessible, since artists need to know how to protect their own work while legally using or remixing the work of others. If this information is hard to find, both individual artists and the entire studio community may lose opportunities to create and share confidently.

Creative Commons is a system of free licenses that helps artists in Maroon + Orange Digital’s Open Artists’ Studio share their work with others, while still choosing the rules for how it can be used, credited, or remixed.

--------------------

Yes. Many people have a general idea of what copyright is; however, they may not understand the details, know how to deal with special situations, or be aware of what to do when a copyright violation seems to occur. People make errors about how copyright and intellectual property rights work all the time, and they may not even know about the Creative Commons licensing system. While they may be able to give you a general definition of copyright, they are likely unsure about all the aspects that make up intellectual property rights and may not know what Creative Commons is. It’s likely they will not know how to apply copyright or licensing as well.
