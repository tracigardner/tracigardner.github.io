Narrowing the Subject for Your Informational Report

Follow this process to test your subject and clarify if you need to. You must be able to answer “Yes” to every question to have a subject that works.

  1. The Major Test
    Does your subject specifically relate to your major?

    Compare your answer:
    • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next step.
    • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a subject that does.

  2. The Technical Topic Test
    Does your subject fit in one of these categories?

    It’s an object in my field.

    It’s a practice in my field.

    It’s an idea in my field.

    Compare your answer:
    • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next step.
    • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a subject that does.

  3. The Ut Prosim Test
    Does your subject have the potential to serve others and contribute to the greater good? Does it have the potential to create a positive social impact?
    Compare your answer:
    • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next step.
    • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a subject that does.

  4. The Writing Project Test
    Is your subject something you can write a report on with the goal of informing, guiding, or supporting action that contributes to fairness, accessibility, and the well-being of communities? Is it a subject you can write about in a way that allows you to live out Virginia Tech’s Ut Prosim motto?

    Compare your answer:
    • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next step.
    • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a subject that does.
