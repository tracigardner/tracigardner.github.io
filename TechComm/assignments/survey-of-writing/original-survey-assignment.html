For this assignment, you are tasked with

  • finding out about the types of writing produced by those who work in your field,
  • applying plain language techniques to what you write, and
  • applying document design techniques to your document layout.
    • Note: I will be looking for parts of the memo, appropriately-sized headings, correct spacing, chunking of paragraphs, and more.

You have two choices for finding this information:

  • You can interview someone working in your intended field.
    • This person can be a friend, a family member, or someone you know through past internships or co-ops.
    • This choice will allow you to ask specialized questions to find the information you need for the assignment.
    • You can interview in person, via Zoom, or via email.
      • If you choose this option, read A Short Guide for Conducting Research Interviews (University Writing Center, Appalachian State University).
        • Note: This guide includes sample questions, but yours will be different and based on the goal of this assignment: learning from your interviewee about the four types of writing they do on a regular basis.
  • You can do all of your research online.
    • This choice might require you to do additional digging to ensure that you find the info needed for the assignment.

Your report should include at least FOUR types of writing.

Assignment Specifications

  • Your informal report should be set up as a memo from you (your name) to me (my name).
  • Follow the layout for memos.
  • Follow this structure for the memo content:
    • Intro:
      • Do not use a heading for this section.
      • Explain the purpose of your project and summarize what you discovered.
      • This can be a single sentence or two sentences.
      • You can even start like this: The purpose of this report is to X. Researching for the report, I discovered Y.
    • Body:
      • Your body will consist of these sections:
        • Approach
          • Use this as your heading, appropriately sized.
          • Identify and discuss the approach you took to finding the information.
            • Did you interview someone? If so, who? Where do they work? What's their job title? How do you know them?
            • If you researched online, where did you find the information? Identify your sources in writing. You don't have to list all of the biblio material. Just indicate the source titles.
        • Types of Writing
          • Use this as your main section heading.
          • In separate sections, each with a heading that identifies the type of writing, summarize the various types of writing you learned about. For each, identify these 6 items:
            • genre (what type of writing is it?)
            • purpose of the type of writing (what is it meant to do?)
            • traditional audience for it
            • usual mode of delivery (paper, electronic, etc.)
            • usual length
              • Please write each section in paragraph form, applying the rules of chunking when needed.
        • Conclusions
          • Use this as your heading; note the "S."
          • End the body section by discussing briefly what you learned from the project about the types of writing you might be producing in the workplace.
    • Conclusion: End cordially and provide your contact information.
