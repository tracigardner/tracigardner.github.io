Question 1

Common Error: Using a Vague or General Title

If your title is so general that it would work on anyone’s report, you need to revise it. Use the Effective Report Titles for more help. Consider these examples:

  • Too General: Recommendation Report
  • Specific Examples:
    • Recommendations for Eating Kosher on Campus
    • Reducing Food Waste at Virginia Tech

Common Error: General Table of Contents

The key here is to build a detailed and specific Table of Contents. Your table of contents should show the full headings and subheadings from the report. Check out the Table of Contents in the Sample Recommendation Report on p. 508 of the textbook for an example.

Question 3

Common Error: Using General Headings and/or Subheadings, OR Using No Headings and/or Subheadings

Headings and subheadings help your reader navigate a document. They are like a signposts that tell the reader where to find specific information.

Check the Table of Contents in the Sample Recommendation Report on p. 508 of the textbook to see appropriate headings. See “Titles and Headings” in Chapter 11 of Markel and Selber’s Technical Communication (pp. 275–277) for help writing your headings and subheadings.

I confirm that I have used specific headings and subheadings in my report.

Question 4

Common Error: Not Documenting Outside Sources

You need to be sure of the following in your report:

  • Any words, phrases, and sentences that you have taken from another source are enclosed in quotation marks.
  • Information that is quoted, paraphrased, or summarized from another source is indicated with a citation.

See the Documentation and Citation page for additional help.

I confirm that I have placed all direct quotations in quotation marks and credited my sources in the body of my report.

Question 5

Common Error: Not Including a Bibliography or References Page

All the sources you use in your project should be listed in a bibliography at the end of the project. See the page on Writing Your References section and the Documentation and Citation page for help.

Question 6

Common Error: Typos and Spelling Errors

This error is probably obvious, but you would be surprised how often it trips people up. In particular, be sure that you always check the names of people involved. You want to be sure that you spell the name of the person you send the report to and other colleagues and clients correctly. When you make errors with people’s names, readers may question your attention to accuracy.

I confirm that I have spellchecked my work and double-checked that I have spelled all names correctly.

Common Error: Using the Wrong Name for Virginia Tech

The official name for the university is Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University. The short version of the name is Virginia Tech. If you are using any other name, you have the wrong name. For more information, see https://career.vt.edu/job-search/presenting_yourself/resumes/common-resume-mistakes.html
