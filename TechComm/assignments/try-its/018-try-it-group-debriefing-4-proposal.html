Grace Period Reminder

All Try-Its have a three-day grace period for the rest of the term to ensure that group work goes smoothly.

What You Need to Do

You’ll meet as a group to assess your collaboration on the proposal. Note that you’ll individually assess your work on the proposal in the Proposal Project Wrapper.

Connect with the members of your group and decide how you will divide the tasks for this Try-It. Each group member must agree with the comments you include in your debriefing.

How This Activity Connects to the Course

The rest of the term, you will focus on group projects. In this Try-It, you’ll evaluate how well your group worked together and how you can improve your collaboration. You’ll apply what you’ve learned to the additional group work in the course.

Assessment

I will assess group members individually based on their participation. To earn a Complete, the section a group leader’s organizes must be filled out fully. I will mark your work for this Try-It Complete (or Incomplete) after the end of the Grace Period passes. Allow me several days to read and mark your work.

How to Do It

  1. Connect with the members of your group to arrange a meeting time that fits everyone’s schedules. You can meet using whatever system works best for your group.
    Show/Hide Ways to Meet
    • Share your sites in a Zoom meeting.
    • Post ideas in a Canvas Group Discussion. The following links can help you:
      • How do I view my Canvas groups as a student?
      • How do I create a discussion in a group?
    • Meet in person on campus or another convenient location.
  2. Before you meet, review the Debriefing Questions, choosing one of the options below:
    • Download Microsoft Word file
    • Make a Copy of the Google Doc version (File menu » Make a Copy)
  3. Meet with your group as scheduled to arrange the work on this activity. You should be able to assign sections quickly. You can also collaborate during your meeting to fill out the form together.
  4. Upload your copy of the form to your Project Folder (which you created during Week 7) so that everyone in the group can add information to it as they work.
  5. Choose a leader for each section of the debriefing form:
    1. Division of Work
    2. What Worked
    3. What Could Be Improved
    4. Who Put in Extra Work
    5. Who Did Not Put in Equal Work
    6. What Do You Need
    Everyone in the group needs to collaborate on the debriefing, so there is no need to list helpers. I’ll assume everyone has helped.
  6. Make any other decisions necessary to ensure that your group fills in all sections of the form.
  7. Choose a day and time when group members should have completed their sections so that you can turn your work in.
  8. Submit your completed file here by one of the following methods:
    • Upload a copy of the file
    • Submit the link to your file in your Project Folder.
