Question 1

Find the code of ethics that represents your intended profession. Most disciplines will have several. For example, a quick Google search for “Code of ethics for engineers” presented over 3 million hits. So narrow your search. Instead of engineering, try electrical engineering. Here are some examples:

  • An electrical engineer would probably focus on the IEEE Code of Ethics.
  • A biologist might focus on the Code of Ethics for the Society for Conservation Biology.

Once you identify the principles for your field, do the following:

  1. Read the code, looking in particular for principles explicitly related to writing or communication.
  2. After you read the code for your field, tell your group members about what you have found in your first post in this Discussion, including this information:

    • Identify your field and the relevant ethical code.
    • Provide a link to your ethical code.
    • Talk about what you found about writing, communication, and collaboration in your field’s ethical code.
    • Propose which aspects of your field’s code of ethics the group should adopt and follow for your group projects.
  3. Read the posts from others in your group, noting ways the codes overlap (or don’t).
