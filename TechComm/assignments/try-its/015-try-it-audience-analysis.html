Success Tip

You can use the online texts, any notes that you have, and the available course pages in Canvas for help as you work on these questions. You can also talk to one another.

You’ll collaborate to create an audience profile for the person who can decide whether to implement the recommendations in your report.

Connect with the members of your group and decide how you will divide the tasks for this Try-It. Each group member must lead the work for one section of the form.

How This Activity Connects to the Course

The rest of the term, you will focus on group projects. In this Try-It, you’ll gather background material on the person who can decide whether to implement your recommendations. You will also gather specific details that you will need for portions of your report (such as the name and address to use on your Letter of Transmittal).

How to Do It

  1. Connect with the members of your group to arrange a meeting time that fits everyone’s schedules. You can meet using whatever system works best for your group. Here are some possibilities:
    • Gather in a Zoom or Teams meeting.
    • Post ideas in a Canvas Group Discussion. The following links can help you:
      • How do I view my Canvas groups as a student?
      • How do I create a discussion in a group?
    • Meet in person on campus or another convenient location.
  2. Before you meet, review the Recommendation Report Audience Analysis Form, choosing one of the options below:
    • Download Microsoft Word file
    • Make a Copy of the Google Doc version (File menu » Make a Copy)
  3. Meet with your group as scheduled to arrange the work on this activity. You should be able to assign sections quickly. You can also collaborate during your meeting to fill out the form together.
  4. Download a copy of the Group Project Audience Analysis Form (see #2 above) for your group to fill in, and upload it to your Project Folder (which you created during Week 7). You only need one copy for the entire group.
  5. Choose a group member who will be responsible for each section of the form. Each group member should take the lead for at least one section. If you have six group members, work in pairs on one of the sections. There are five sections:
    1. Basic Information for the Report’s Primary Reader
    2. Primary Reader’s Social Media
    3. Primary Reader’s Professional Background
    4. Primary Reader’s Relationship to the Topic
    5. Apply Your Audience Profile
  6. If group members will collaborate on sections, list additional group members for each section by the Helpers label.
  7. Make any other decisions necessary to ensure that your group fills in all sections of the profile.
  8. Schedule a day and time when group members should have completed their sections so that you can turn your work in. If necessary, update your schedule in the Calendar Pro tab of Teams.
  9. Submit your completed file here by one of the following methods:
    • Upload a copy of the file.
    • Submit the link to your file in your Project Folder.

Assessment

I will assess group members individually based on their participation. To earn a Complete, the section a group member organizes must be filled out fully. I will mark your work for this Try-It Complete (or Incomplete) after the end of the Grace Period passes. Allow me several days to read and mark your work.
