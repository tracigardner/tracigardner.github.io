Setup

In addition to documenting your sources, you need to frame your quotations. In their textbook They Say / I Say, Gerald Graff and Cathy Birkenstein explain the strategy with an analogy to a sandwich:

To adequately frame a quotation, you need to insert it into what we like to call a “quotation sandwich,” with the statement introducing it serving as the top slice of bread and the explanation following it serving as the bottom slice. The introductory or lead-in claims should explain who is speaking and set up what the quotation says; the follow-up statements should explain why you consider the quotation to be important and what you take it to say. (p.80)

The quotation sandwich provides a strong way to integrate your quotations into your report since it ensures that you always tell your reader what you are sharing before the quotation and then explain why you have included it after it is stated.
