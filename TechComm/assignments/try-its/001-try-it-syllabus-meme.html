What You Need to Do

Engage creatively with any of the course documents (the Short Guide, the Course Policies, or the Teamwork & Collaboration Guide) by taking one important concept or idea and turning it into an image meme.

How This Activity Connects to the Course

First, understanding the course documents will help you do well in the course. This activity makes the process more interesting.

Second, we’ll spend a lot of time examining how to use visual and textual elements in this course. This activity asks you to represent and communicate a key aspect of our course in a way that’s engaging, memorable, and thought-provoking.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Review the the course documents (the Short Guide, the Course Policies, or the Teamwork & Collaboration Guide) and select an important concept, idea, or policy that you think is crucial for success in this course. It can be anything from the AI policy, the grace period, or any other key element. Be sure to note the page number and the name of the document where you found the concept.
  2. Choose a meme creation tool. Here are some examples:
    • Canva
    • Imgflip
    • Adobe Free Meme Generator
    • xxx
  3. Create a meme that effectively communicates the concept you’ve chosen, using the meme creation tool of your choice. Your meme can be fun, satirical, or witty. Be sure, however, that it’s in line with the Virginia Tech Principles of Community.
  4. Once your meme is complete, add your first post to this Discussion as a reply. Include the following information:
    • A statement of the concept you selected. You can copy and paste it from the course document.
    • The page number and document name where you found it, so we can find the original mention if we want to know more.
    • Anything else you want to say about the concept or your meme.
  5. Review the memes created by your classmates. Pretend you’re on Instagram.

    Like any that you found particularly interesting, fun, or helpful. Don’t Like every one please. Focus on those that seem the best or most entertaining to you.
  6. Choose at least three memes to comment on. In your comments, discuss:
    • Understanding: Does the meme help you understand the concept better? How so?
    • Engagement: Did the meme catch your attention? What about it made it stand out?
    • Preference: Did you like or dislike the meme? Explain your reasoning.
  7. Read the posts from others in your group, paying attention to how you agree or disagree on their solutions.
  8. Write at least one additional post to discuss the solution your group will recommend and the actions Mateo’s team should take to avoid such dilemmas in the future.

Assessment

I will mark your participation in this Discussion Complete (or Incomplete) after the end of the Grace Period passes and I confirm that you have made at least two posts (required: one response to the original scenario, and a second in response to others in your class). Allow me several days to read and mark all your posts.

Evaluation Criteria:

  • Creativity: How well did you use the meme format to communicate the chosen concept?
  • Relevance: Does the meme effectively represent a key idea from the syllabus?
  • Engagement: Did you thoughtfully engage with your classmates’ memes and provide constructive feedback?
