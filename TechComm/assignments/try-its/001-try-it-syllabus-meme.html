What You Need to Do

Engage creatively with any of the course documents (the Short Guide, the Course Policies, or the Teamwork & Collaboration Guide) by taking one important concept or idea and turning it into an image meme.

How This Activity Connects to the Course

First, understanding the course documents will help you do well in the course. This activity makes the process more interesting.

Second, we’ll spend a lot of time examining how to use visual and textual elements in this course. This activity asks you to represent and communicate a key aspect of our course in a way that’s engaging, memorable, and thought-provoking.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Success Tip

You can use the online texts, any notes that you have, and the available course pages in Canvas for help as you work on these questions. You can also talk to one another.

  1. Choose a Concept:
    Review the syllabus and select an important concept, idea, or policy that you think is crucial for your success in this course. This could be anything from the grading policy, class expectations, the role of technical communication, or any other key element. Be sure to include the page number and the name of the document where you found the concept.
  2. Create a Meme:
    Using any meme creation tool (such as Canva, Imgflip, or a similar platform), create a meme that effectively communicates the concept you chose. Your meme should be both informative and visually appealing. Memes can be fun, satirical, witty, and so on, as long as they are in line with the Virginia Tech Principles of Community.
  3. Post to Discussion Forum:
    Once your meme is complete, post it to the designated discussion forum on Canvas. Make sure to include a brief explanation (1-2 sentences) of the concept you selected, why you chose to represent it in this way, and the page number and document name where you found it.
  4. Engage with Classmates:
    After posting your meme, take some time to review the memes created by your classmates. Choose at least three memes to comment on. In your comments, discuss:
    • Understanding: Does the meme help you understand the concept better? How so?
    • Engagement: Did the meme catch your attention? What about it made it stand out?
    • Preference: Did you like or dislike the meme? Explain your reasoning.
  5. Read the posts from others in your group, paying attention to how you agree or disagree on their solutions.
  6. Write at least one additional post to discuss the solution your group will recommend and the actions Mateo’s team should take to avoid such dilemmas in the future.

Evaluation Criteria:

  • Creativity: How well did you use the meme format to communicate the chosen concept?
  • Relevance: Does the meme effectively represent a key idea from the syllabus?
  • Engagement: Did you thoughtfully engage with your classmates’ memes and provide constructive feedback?

Submission Deadline:
