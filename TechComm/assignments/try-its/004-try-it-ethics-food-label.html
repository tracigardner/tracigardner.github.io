Question 0

What product are you examining the food or beverage label for?

Question 1

Question 2

What information does the label include that relates to social media?

Question 3

Ethical documents “tell the truth” (Markel & Selber, p. 12). What information does the label include that tells you the label is truthful and honest?

Question 4

How does the label use design to highlight important ethical and legal information? If the label does not use design, describe places where its design could be improved to highlight important ethical and legal information.

Question 5

Ethical communication “avoid[s] language [and images] that discriminates against people . . . .” (Markel & Selber, p. 12). What information on the label demonstrates inclusive language and images? If there is any evidence of discrimination, describe what you found.

Question 6

Search online for conduct or ethics statement on the website for the company that produces the food or label. Paste the link to the information for your label’s company below.

Example, from a non-food company: LEGO has a The LEGO Group Communication Letter on Expected Ethical, Social and Environmental Conduct and a page on Responsible Business Principles.

Question 7

Using information on the conducts or ethics page, what details on the label connect to the organization’s values and ethical beliefs?

Question 8

Skip the principle that states, “Take advantage of your employer’s ethics resources” (Markel & Selber, p. 12). There’s no way for you to determine whether the label designers and contributors consulted their company ethics resources.

Question 9

An ethical document avoids “false implications about products, euphemisms, exaggerations about product specifications, and legalistic constructions” (Markel & Selber, p. 12) What information on the label demonstrates that the company does not mislead people? What information is shared in plain and easy-to-understand language?

Question 10

What information on the label helps your reader find the information they need? There won’t be a table of contents or index, of course. What other accessing tools on the label help the company “be clear”?

Question 11

What sources or collaborators are cited on the label? How are they identified? If there are no sources or collaborators for the project, you can state that fact.
