Question 2

  1. What is the title of the presentation, as shown on the first slide? Copy and paste it in here.
  2. Next consider how the title emphasizes important information:
    • Does it clearly state the subject and purpose of the presentation?
    • Is it precise? Does it avoid using general terms?
    • Can it be paraphrased in a clear, meaningful sentence?
    • Is it too long? too short?
  3. Write two or three sentences that evaluate the title’s emphasis of important information.

For help, see pp. 198–199 of Markel & Selber.

Question 3

  1. Skim through the slides and choose four headings (slide titles). Copy and paste them here.
  2. Consider how the headings emphasize important information:

    • Are they informative?
    • Answer any of these questions that apply:
      • Do they use the question form for readers who are not knowledgeable about the subject?
      • Do they use the “how-to” form in instructional materials?

For help, see pp. 199–202 of Markel & Selber.

Question 4

  1. Skim through the slides and choose a list in the body of one of the slides. Copy and paste the list here.
  2. Consider how the lists emphasize important information:
    • Is the list set off by bullets, numbers, or letters?
    • Does the list flow in an obvious sequence?
    • Does the list include a lead-in?
    • Does the list use tight, clear language?
    • Is the list no longer than 9 items?
  3. Write two or three sentences that evaluate the list’s emphasis of important information.

For help, see pp. 203–207 of Markel & Selber.
