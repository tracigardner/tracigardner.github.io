You’ll collaborate to review the document design of your proposal. Note that you’ll review how you’ve meet the content criteria for the proposal when each of you completes the Proposal Self-Check or the Proposal Self-Check with AI.

Connect with the members of your group and decide how you will divide the tasks for this Try-It. Each group member must lead the work for one section of the Try-It.

How This Activity Connects to the Course

The rest of the term, you will focus on group projects. In this Try-It, you’ll evaluate how well your group proposal applies principles of document design to make your proposal easy to navigate and read. You’ll suggest revisions to improve your proposal before you submit it.

How to Do It

  1. Connect with the members of your group to arrange a meeting time that fits everyone’s schedules. You can meet using whatever system works best for your group. Here are some possibilities:
    • Gather in a Zoom or Teams meeting.
    • Post ideas in a Canvas Group Discussion. The following links can help you:
      • How do I view my Canvas groups as a student?
      • How do I create a discussion in a group?
    • Meet in person on campus or another convenient location.
  2. Before you meet, review the Group Proposal Document Design Form, choosing one of the options below:
    • Download Microsoft Word file
    • Make a Copy of the Google Doc version (File menu » Make a Copy)
  3. Meet with your group as scheduled to arrange the work on this activity. You can also collaborate during your meeting to fill out the form together.
  4. Upload a copy of the form to your group’s Shared Folder (which you created during Week 7) so that everyone in the group can add information to it as they work. You only need one copy for the entire group.
  5. Choose a group member who will be responsible for each section of the form. Each group member should take the lead for at least one section. If you have six group members, work in pairs on one of the sections. There are five sections:
    1. Headings
    2. Use of Empty Space (or White Space)
    3. Use of Color
    4. Paragraph Chunking
    5. Use of Lists
  6. If additional group members will collaborate on sections, list additional group members for each section by the Helpers label.
  7. Make any other decisions necessary to ensure that your group fills in all sections of the form.
  8. Schedule a day and time when group members should have completed their sections so that you can turn your work in. If necessary, update your schedule in the Calendar Pro tab of Teams.
  9. Submit your completed file here by one of the following methods:
    • Upload a copy of the file
    • Submit the link to your file in your Project Folder.

Assessment

I will assess group members individually based on their participation. To earn a Complete, the section you are responsible for must be filled out fully. I will mark your work for this Try-It Complete (or Incomplete) after the end of the Grace Period passes. Allow me several days to read and mark your work.
