Grace Period Reminder

All Try-Its have a three-day grace period for the rest of the term to ensure that group work goes smoothly.

What You Need to Do

You’ll collaborate to review the document design of your proposal. Note that you’ll review how you’ve meet the content criteria for the proposal when each of you completes the Proposal Self-Check.

Connect with the members of your group and decide how you will divide the tasks for this Try-It. Each group member must lead the work for one section of the Try-It.

How This Activity Connects to the Course

The rest of the term, you will focus on group projects. In this Try-It, you’ll evaluate how well your group proposal applies principles of document design to make your proposal easy to navigate and read. You’ll suggest revisions to improve your proposal before you submit it.

Assessment

I will assess group members individually based on their participation. To earn a Complete, the section a group leader’s organizes must be filled out fully. I will mark your work for this Try-It Complete (or Incomplete) after the end of the Grace Period passes. Allow me several days to read and mark your work.

How to Do It

  1. Connect with the members of your group to arrange a meeting time that fits everyone’s schedules. You can meet using whatever system works best for your group. Here are some possibilities:
    • Share your sites in a Zoom meeting.
    • Post ideas in a Canvas Group Discussion. The following links can help you:
      • How do I view my Canvas groups as a student?
      • How do I create a discussion in a group?
    • Meet in person on campus or another convenient location.
  2. Before you meet, review the Group Proposal Document Design Form, choosing one of the options below:
    • Download Microsoft Word file
    • Make a Copy of the Google Doc version (File menu » Make a Copy)
  3. Meet with your group as scheduled to arrange the work on this activity. You should be able to assign sections quickly. You can also collaborate during your meeting to fill out the form together.
  4. Upload your copy of the form to your Project Folder (which you created during Week 7) so that everyone in the group can add information to it as they work.
  5. Choose a leader for each section of the form. There are five sections:
    1. Headings
    2. Use of Empty Space (or White Space)
    3. Use of Color
    4. Paragraph Chunking
    5. Use of Lists
  6. If group members will collaborate on sections, list additional group members for each section by the Helpers label.
  7. Make any other decisions necessary to ensure that your group fills in all sections of the profile.
  8. Choose a day and time when group members should have completed their sections so that you can turn your work in.
  9. Submit your completed file here by one of the following methods:
    • Upload a copy of the file
    • Submit the link to your file in your Project Folder.
