Question 1

Announce that you are concluding. Write a sentence that signals your shift to the conclusion.

For example, “At this point, I’d like to conclude my talk with . . .” This statement helps the audience focus on your conclusions.

Question 2

Summarize the main points of your presentation.

Remind listeners of your ideas. You will prepare a slide that lists each of your main points in one short phrase.

Question 3

Look to the future. In two or three sentences, Tell your audience what you project to happen next.

If appropriate, speak briefly about what you think (or hope) will happen next: “If the president accepts our recommendation, you can expect the renovation to begin in late November. After a few hectic weeks, we’ll have the ability to control our environment much more precisely than we can now—and start to reduce our expenses and our carbon footprint.”

Question 4

Invite questions politely. Write a sentence or two that signals you are ready for questions.

You want to invite questions because they help you clarify what you said or communicate information that you did not present in the formal presentation. You want to ask politely to encourage people to speak up.
