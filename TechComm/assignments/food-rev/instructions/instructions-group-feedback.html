Specific Feedback

  • Meet the requirements of the assignment. The Instructions Project Criteria outlines the requirements for an instructions document. Be sure that you meet every one of the requirements in order to earn a Complete on the project.
  • Use these two examples to help you revise. These are drafts from other students in the course, shared anonymously:
    • Instructions Example 1
    • Instructions Example 2
  • Include a specific title. Your title should tell the reader enough to know exactly what the document is about. Check out Effective Report Titles for examples.
  • Use numbered steps and sub-steps. Instructions are usually recognizable at a glance because of their simple and direct phrasing in numbered lists. Use the numbered list tool in your word processor to set your document up easily.
  • Use the imperative mood. Use action-oriented verbs to begin and explain each step of your instructions. Imperatives are commands. They tell the reader what to do. As an example, each bullet item in this Specific Feedback section begins with an imperative: meet, use, include, integrate, and get. Use similar action words to start each step in your instructions.
  • Use document design to help readers navigate your memo. Apply the what you learn about design principles to your own draft as you write. Headings help readers understand the organization of the document, and document design can ensure that the headings are easy to find. Likewise when you are using lists of steps, numbered and/or bulleted lists (depending upon your structure) make the document easier to follow.
  • Integrate images smoothly. Place the screenshot images in your document near the text that mentions them and refer to them in the document. Doing so is following the P (Proximity) in CRAP Design Principles. Be sure to explain how the illustrations connect to actions the reader is to complete.
  • Get help from the Writing Center (optional). Anyone can visit the Writing Center in the library for help with projects for the course. Writing Center tutors can help you strengthen your content as well as work on document design and other issues. You can read more about the Writing Center and make an appointment on the Writing Center’s webpage.
