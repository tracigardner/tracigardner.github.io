What I Want You to Do

You and the members of your group will write a collection of fact sheets that focus on usability, document design, and accessibility principles. Your group will focus on the website identified in your Proposal. Each team member will create one fact sheet using examples from that website. The final collection will include a title page, a table of contents, and a letter of transmittal.

Your audience will be employees responsible for maintaining the chosen website. Your fact sheets should help them recognize and apply improvements to the site’s design and accessibility.

Why I Want You to Do It

You will write longer documents in the workplace, usually with a team of people. This assignment emphasizes collaborative writing, consistent formatting, and professional tone, while also strengthening your ability to analyze and explain digital communication best practices. The Scenario for the Project

You work as a Usability Consultant at a Maroon + Orange Digital, where I (Traci) work as a Founding Owner and the Director of Project Development. Your client is the person responsible for the website your group has chosen.

This client has asked you to assess their website and create educational materials to help their employees improve it. You’ve been asked to prepare a set of short, easy-to-read fact sheets that explain specific principles, show how they relate to the current site, and offer improvement suggestions.

Your collection will be read and used by a team of developers, designers, and content writers who need accessible and actionable advice.

How You Do It

Preparing for the Project

  1. Review the rubric below the assignment to ensure that you understand the requirements for the project.
  2. Ensure that your website backup is complete. If anything on the website has changed, update your backup so that you have the most recent files. Here are some ways to make a backup:
    • Print out the webpage on paper, ensuring all the information on the page prints.
    • Print the webpage and choose to print a PDF, so you’ll have a digital backup.
    • Save a backup to the Wayback Machine by following these instructions.

Arranging Group Work

  1. Assign one principle to each member (e.g., headings, link text, image alt attributes).
  2. Design a shared template or layout that all fact sheets will follow, allowing space for at least three visual elements (see below). You can use Canva, PiktoChart, etc.
  3. Confirm that everyone know the documentation style that your group has agreed upon. See the Documentation and Citations for Your Projects page for help.
  4. Review the schedule that your group included in your Progress Report with one another. Update the schedule as needed.

Writing Your Fact Sheet

  1. Use the shared template or layout that your group was agreed to.
  2. Create a two-page fact sheet using examples from the website. Your fact sheet should:
    • Focus on one principle (e.g., accessible color contrast, alt text, or heading use)
    • Be exactly two pages in length
    • Include clear examples from the chosen website
    • Use consistent formatting and design across the group’s collection
  3. Use in-text citations throughout the report, following the style system your group decides upon. Include the references for your fact sheet at the end of your sheet. There is not a separate bibliography section. You are responsible for your documentation individually.
  4. Add at least three visual elements, following these criteria:
    • Use as many visuals (e.g., photos, illustrations, flowcharts, tables) as needed to make the principle compelling and enhance the message in the content.
    • Do not add decorative illustrations.
    • Include textual references to visuals to integrate them into the text.
    • Wrap text around visuals.

    • Letter of transmittal (Instructions for Your Letter of Transmittal>
