The Non-Expert Audience Test

Answer the Test Questions

  • Do most readers lack formal training in this topic?
  • Will most readers need definitions for common jargon or acronyms related to this topic?

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next test.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a topic that does.

Example Response to Test Question

Yes. My audience is the Board of Directors of Maroon + Orange Digital’s Teen Artists’ Studio. The Board of Directors has a general idea of what copyright is; however, they are primarily financiers and local business owners and executives. They do not work with copyright themselves. While they may be able to give you a general definition of copyright, they are probably unsure about all the aspects that make up intellectual property rights.

The Decisionmaker Audience Test

Answer the Test

  • Do your readers have the authority to approve, adopt, or reject the ideas from your report?
  • Will their decision directly affect the target community, users, or services?

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next test.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a topic that does.

Example Response to Test Question

Yes. The Board of Directors for Maroon + Orange Digital’s Teen Artists’ Studio supports the Artists-in-Residence who are members. Once the Board understands copyright, intellectual property rights, and Creative Commons licensing, they can decide how to help the Studio’s artists protect their work while legally using or remixing the work of others.

Identify a real non-expert group (e.g., first-year majors, community members, patients, town council members, investors, etc.) and briefly explain the following information: what they already know about your subject. what they need to know about it. why they care about it. how you will need to approach them in terms of language and design.
