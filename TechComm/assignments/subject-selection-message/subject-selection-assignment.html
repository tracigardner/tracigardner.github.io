Write a neutral-news email (good-news up front, details in the body, cordial close) that identifies and supports your topic choice for the semester-long project that will involve

  • a research proposal
  • a user document
  • an informational report for non-expert readers
  • a fact sheet

  Review Document Samples and the Deliverable Checklist as you work.

Assignment Learning Objectives

By completing this assignment, you will learn how to plan and draft a professional, neutral-news email that communicates your research intentions clearly and persuasively. You will practice organizing your message using a reader-focused structure (good news up front, details in the body, polite close). You will also develop your ability to:

  1. Identify and articulate a clear and feasible research topic relevant to your field.
  2. Justify the value and scope of your topic, including key questions you intend to explore.
  3. Analyze a real-world non-expert audience and tailor your message to meet their needs in terms of content, tone, and design.
  4. Practice appropriate professional email etiquette, tone, and formatting to build credibility with your reader.

This assignment strengthens your skills in project planning, audience awareness, and workplace communication.

Assignment Requirements

In this email, aim for 4–5 concise paragraphs (roughly 250–350 words total).

  • Organization: Your message must include the following FOUR sections:
    • Introduction (1 short paragraph): Direct statement of purpose (topic you plan to pursue). No heading.
    • Scope & Feasibility (1–2 paragraphs): Use "Scope & Feasibility" (14 pts bold) as your heading. Explain why you have chosen this subject and why you believe it is important enough to research in depth. Sketch the key questions you expect to answer. (Don’t worry; they can change over time as you research!)
    • Audience Analysis (1-2 paragraphs): Use "Audience Analysis" (14 pts bold) as your heading. Identify a real non-expert group (e.g., first-year majors, community members, patients, town council members, investors, etc.) and briefly explain
      • what they already know about your subject,
        what they need to know about it,
      • why they care about it, and
      • how you will need to approach them in terms of language and design..
    • Polite Close (1 paragraph): Ask for approval or guidance, thank the reader, and provide your contact info within a single paragraph. No heading.
    • Polite salutation + cordial close.
  • Plain Language: Use short sentences and other plain language techniques
  • Document Design: Chunk paragraphs; single-space within paragraphs; add one blank line between paragraphs (double space); no internal headings needed; do not indent paragraphs

Remember that you can submit a draft to your preferred generative-AI tool (Copilot, ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude). If you do, you must follow the procedures outlined on the Ethical AI Usage page, which include these rules:

  • Copy the AI comments or revision advice below your original draft.
  • Briefly note which tool you used and what prompt you gave it.
  • Copy and paste your first draft below the AI comments and revise it based on the advice you have received.
