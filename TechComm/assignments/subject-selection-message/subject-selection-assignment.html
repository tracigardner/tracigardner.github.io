Subject Selection Message

Your Task

Worth 10% of your course grade

Write a neutral-news email (good-news up front, details in the body, cordial close) that identifies and supports your topic choice for the semester-long project that will include the following:

  • a research proposal
  • a user document
  • an informational report for non-expert readers
  • a progress report
  • a fact sheet

Review Document Samples and the Deliverable Checklist as you work.

Assignment Learning Objectives

By completing this assignment, you will learn how to plan and draft a professional, neutral-news email that communicates your research intentions clearly and persuasively. You will practice organizing your message using a reader-focused structure (good news up front, details in the body, polite close). You will also develop your ability to:

  1. Identify and articulate a clear and feasible research topic relevant to your field.
  2. Justify the value and scope of your topic, including key questions you intend to explore.
  3. Analyze a real-world non-expert audience and tailor your message to meet their needs in terms of content, tone, and design.
  4. Practice appropriate professional email etiquette, tone, and formatting to build credibility with your reader.

This assignment strengthens your skills in project planning, audience awareness, and workplace communication.

Your Audience

Write your message to Traci, your teacher for the course. She’s the person who decides whether your subject is appropriate for the projects.

Provide her with the basic details on the subject and clear, concrete details on how it fits the Ut Prosim project. This is a neutral-news message, but you still have to persuade her to approve your idea!

Assignment Requirements

In this email, aim for4–5 concise paragraphs (roughly 250–350 words total, 12 pt font).

  • Organization: Your message must include the following FOUR sections:
    • Introduction (1 short paragraph): Direct statement of purpose (the topic you plan to pursue). No heading.
    • Scope & Feasibility (1–2 paragraphs): Use “Scope & Feasibility” (14 pts bold, no quotation marks) as your heading. Explain why you have chosen this subject and why you believe it is important enough to research in depth. Sketch the key questions you expect to answer. (Don’t worry; they can change over time as you research!)
    • Audience Analysis (1-2 paragraphs): Use “Audience Analysis” (14 pts bold, no quotation marks) as your heading. Identify a real non-expert group (e.g., first-year majors, community members, patients, town council members, investors, etc.) and briefly explain the following information:

      • what they already know about your subject
      • what they need to know about it,
      • why they care about it, and
      • how you will need to approach them in terms of language and design.

      Important: Remember that the audience for your message (Traci) is not the same as the audience for your Ut Prosim project.

    • Polite Close (1 paragraph): Ask for approval or guidance, thank the reader, and provide your contact info within a single paragraph. No heading.
    • Polite salutation + cordial close.
  • Plain Language: Use short sentences and other plain language techniques.
  • Document Design: Chunk paragraphs; single-space within paragraphs; add one blank line between paragraphs (double space); no internal headings needed; do not indent paragraphs.

Success Tips

  • Use a clear and objective tone. No need to be a cheerleader!
  • Use the required headings (Scope & Feasibility, and Audience Analysis).
  • Remember there are no headings for the Introduction or Closing.
  • Identify a real non-expert audience with specific needs for your Ut Prosim project.
  • Provide specific details on your topic. Details make your idea clear.
  • Format like a professional email (salutation, paragraphs, closing, signature block).
