Testing Your Semester-Long Topic

Instructions: Use this process to test your topic and informational report audience for the semester-long project. You must be able to answer “Yes” to every question to have a topic that works.

The Major Test

Answer the Test Questions

  • Does your topic specifically relate to your major?
  • Is it a topic that a person in your career field knows about?

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next test.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a topic that does.

Example Response to Test Question

Yes. I’m majoring in Technical & Scientific Communication. I’m focusing on copyright and Creative Commons. Those concepts relate to my major since the communication I do needs to respect ownership laws for intellectual property. Additionally, things I write will be protected by these guidelines and laws.

The Technical Topic Test

Answer the Test Questions

  • Does your topic fit in one of these categories?
    • It’s an object in my field.
    • It’s a practice in my field.
    • It’s an idea in my field.

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next test.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a topic that does.

Example Response to Test Question

Yes. I’m focusing on copyright and Creative Commons. These concepts are practices in my field—writing and creation of digital media. Majors in my field apply these guidelines to their work in order to control the use and reuse of their work.

The Ut Prosim Test

Answer the Test Questions

  • Does writing about your topic have the potential to serve others and contribute to the greater good?
  • Does it have the potential to create a positive impact?

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next test.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a topic that does.

Example Response to Test Questions

Yes. Helping others understand how copyright and Creative Commons work is a positive service. I can serve others by writing about these concepts in ways that will give them the power to protect their creative work. They also need to know how to avoid violating someone else’s intellectual property rights, which can result in costly fines and sometimes embarrassment.

The Informational Project Test

Answer the Test Questions

  • Is your topic something you can write an informational report on?
  • Can you tell readers details such as what the topic is, how it works, what it does (without advocating or arguing for a specific position)?

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next test.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a topic that does.

Example Response to Test Question

Yes. Copyright and Creative Commons are complicated concepts, and people often misunderstand how they work. I can write documents that explain what the concepts are as well as projects that show people how to add copyright or Creative Commons licenses to their work.

The Local Focus Test

Answer the Test Questions

  • Is your topic addressing a community group, a city or town, a county, or a similar local area or group?
  • Can you include a relevant reference or the specific name of the local area or group when you state your topic?

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next test.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a topic that does.

Example Response to Test Question

Yes. I am writing about copyright and Creative Commons as it relates to Maroon + Orange Digital’s Teen Artists’ Studio.

I’d state my topic as copyright and Creative Commons for the community of Maroon + Orange Digital’s Teen Artists’ Studio.

The Non-Expert Audience Test

Answer the Test Questions

  • Do most readers lack formal training in this topic?
  • Will most readers need definitions for common jargon or acronyms related to this topic?

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next test.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a topic that does.

Example Response to Test Question

Yes. My audience is the Board of Directors of Maroon + Orange Digital’s Teen Artists’ Studio. The Board of Directors has a general idea of what copyright is; however, they are primarily financiers and local business owners, and corporate executives. They do not work with copyright themselves. While they may be able to give you a general definition of copyright, they are probably unsure about all the aspects that make up intellectual property rights.

The Decision-Maker Audience Test

Answer the Test Questions

  • Do your readers have the authority to approve, adopt, or reject the ideas from your report?
  • Will their decision directly affect the target community, users, or services?

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” you’re ready to share your topic with Traci.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a topic that does.

Example Response to Test Question

Yes. The Board of Directors for Maroon + Orange Digital’s Teen Artists’ Studio supports the Artists-in-Residence who are members. Once the Board understands copyright and Creative Commons licensing, they can decide how to help the Studio’s artists protect their work while legally using or remixing the work of others.
