What Counts as a Good Topic?

Use this page to browse strong topic ideas by major area. The examples below are technical (not opinion-only) and are workable across multiple documents and audiences.

Once you pick a potential topic, use the Testing Your Topic & Audience page to confirm that your subject and audiences fully fit the project.

Quick Signs You’re on the Right Track

  • It’s technical: an object, process, practice, or system in your field.
  • It has real users: someone needs to understand it, use it, or make decisions about it.
  • You can explain it to non-experts: you can define key terms and avoid heavy jargon.
  • It can stretch across six projects: enough depth to support multiple audiences, purposes, and genres.

Agribusiness

  • Fair-trade cocoa supply (or another supply) chains—trace farmer payments to retail shelves.
  • Carbon markets for smallholders growing coffee or cacao.
  • Low-cost soil-health kits for resource-poor regions.

Animal & Poultry Sciences

  • Antibiotic-free feed additives to curb resistance without harming yield.
  • Welfare-friendly housing systems and productivity trade-offs.
  • Mobile vet clinics serving rural livestock keepers.

Building Construction / Architecture

  • Passive-cooling retrofits for public housing in heat-wave-prone cities.
  • Low-carbon concrete alternatives evaluated for structural safety and cost.
  • Universal-design classrooms that support students with disabilities.
  • Urban green-roof programs to tackle stormwater runoff and urban-heat islands.
  • Community-led design charrettes that give marginalized residents a voice in redevelopment.

Business Information Technology

  • Blockchain for supply-chain transparency (e.g., tracing fair-trade coffee).
  • Data dashboards that let municipalities track equity in service delivery.
  • Cybersecurity guidelines for grassroots human-rights organizations with limited budgets.
  • AI-driven waste-sorting systems that boost recycling rates.
  • Digital-divide analytics: identifying broadband gaps and proposing rollout plans.

Chemical Engineering

  • Low-cost water-purification membranes for rural communities facing heavy-metal contamination.
  • Biodegradable plastics from agricultural waste to reduce ocean micro-plastics.
  • Cleaner fertilizer synthesis routes that cut greenhouse-gas emissions and support sustainable farming in food-insecure regions.
  • Carbon-capture solvents scaled for small factories in developing countries.
  • Equitable distribution of vaccines: designing temperature-stable formulations for clinics without cold-chain capacity.

Clinical Neuroscience

  • Low-cost EEG headsets for stroke rehabilitation in low-resource clinics.
  • Neurodiversity-friendly workspace designs to improve employment outcomes.
  • Smartphone seizure-detection apps enabling rapid response.

Computer Science

  • Bias-testing frameworks for AI hiring tools to reduce discriminatory outcomes.
  • Privacy-preserving contact-tracing apps for future public-health crises.
  • Accessible web design audit bots that flag WCAG violations for small nonprofits.
  • Crowdsourced disaster-response mapping platforms using open data.
  • Energy-efficient data centers: algorithms that shift workloads to renewables-rich hours.

Cybersecurity

  • Privacy-first digital IDs for refugees and stateless persons.
  • Secure telehealth platforms protecting rural mental-health data.
  • Cyber-safety curricula for K–12 students in low-income districts.

Data Science / Analytics

  • Predicting food deserts using census, transit, and grocery-store datasets.
  • Bias audit of facial recognition across diverse skin tones and lighting.
  • Wildfire-risk dashboards delivering real-time alerts to underserved communities.

Environmental Sciences

  • Restoration of urban wetlands to improve flood control and biodiversity.
  • Environmental-justice mapping of toxic-release sites near low-income neighborhoods.
  • Microplastic levels in local waterways and policy recommendations.
  • Indigenous land-management practices as models for wildfire prevention.
  • Life-cycle analysis of fast-fashion and consumer-education strategies.

Finance

  • Social-impact bonds funding affordable housing projects.
  • Climate-risk disclosure frameworks for small publicly traded firms.
  • Micro-lending models that empower women entrepreneurs in emerging economies.
  • ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) scoring: comparing methodologies and real-world impacts.
  • Cryptocurrency carbon footprints and potential carbon-offset mechanisms.

Food Science & Technology

  • Fortified snack bars combating micronutrient deficiencies in food-insecure areas.
  • Upcycling spent grain into high-protein baking flour.
  • Rapid mycotoxin tests for smallholder maize farmers.

General Engineering (Mechanical, Electrical, Civil)

  • Modular, quickly deployable shelters for climate-displaced populations.
  • Solar-powered micro-grids to bring reliable electricity to off-grid schools.
  • Low-cost prosthetic limbs using 3-D-printed components for war survivors.
  • Smart traffic-light systems that cut emissions and improve pedestrian safety in underserved neighborhoods.
  • Bridge retrofits that make 1950s-era infrastructure resilient to stronger storms.

Hard Sciences (Biology, Physics, Chemistry, Earth Science)

  • CRISPR-based disease diagnostics deployable in low-resource clinics.
  • Perovskite solar cells: balancing efficiency gains with toxic-material concerns.
  • Atmospheric micro-sensor networks to monitor air-quality inequalities across a city.
  • Green chemistry routes for synthesizing common pharmaceuticals.
  • Citizen-science radon mapping to reduce lung-cancer risk in older homes.

History

  • Mapping redlining’s legacy—correlate 1930s housing maps with present-day health and wealth gaps.
  • Essential-worker oral histories documenting frontline experiences during the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • Evolution of wartime prosthetics and its impact on disability rights movements.

Humanities

  • Ethical analyses of autonomous-vehicle decision algorithms (applying classic moral frameworks).
  • Archival research on environmental-justice movements and lessons for today’s activists.
  • Plain-language translation of health-care policies for non-native speakers.
  • Narrative impacts of climate fiction on public perception of science.
  • Digital storytelling projects that preserve endangered languages and cultural heritage.

Marketing

  • Social-impact branding for reusable household goods.
  • Vaccine-confidence messaging tailored to hesitant communities.
  • Gamified recycling apps that boost campus participation.
  • Usability testing of public health campaign materials to improve clarity, comprehension, and trust.
  • Plain-language redesign of financial or consumer disclosures to reduce misunderstanding and misuse.

Philosophy / Ethics

  • Allocating scarce medical resources—compare utilitarian, deontological, and care-ethics approaches.
  • AI emotion-detection in schools—assess privacy and autonomy concerns.
  • Right-to-repair debates balancing consumer freedom with sustainability.
