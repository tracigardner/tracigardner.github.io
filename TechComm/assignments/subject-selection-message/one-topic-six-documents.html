How One Topic Becomes Six Documents

This table shows how a single semester-long topic is used to produce six different technical documents.

In every project, the topic stays the same. What changes is who the document is for and what the document needs to do.

Comparison of the Major Projects in the Course

Table 1. Comparison of the Major Projects in the Course
Project & Genre Primary Audience Purpose What Changes
Subject Selection Form Instructor Propose and justify topic Scope, rationale, level of formality
Research Proposal Instructor Plan research and audiences Depth, sources, project planning
User Document Teen artists Help users apply licenses correctly Tone, format, step-by-step structure
Progress Report Instructor Report project status Focus on progress, challenges, next steps
Informational Report Board of Directors Support decisions and policy Evidence, explanation level, organization
Fact Sheet Teens & families Provide quick public guidance Brevity, visuals, layout

Using the Table to Evaluate Your Topic

  1. Consider how your topic fits in each row:
    • Does your topic fit the project & genre?
    • Do you know specific audiences for each project?
    • Can you state a purpose for each project?

  2. Decide if your topic will work for the full semester:
    • If it works across these genres, audiences, and purposes, it is likely a strong choice for the course.
    • If it doesn’t, consider how you can adapt your topic so that it works.
