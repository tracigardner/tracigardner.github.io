How One Topic Becomes Six Documents

This example shows how one semester-long topic can be adapted across six different technical documents. While the semester-long topic stays the same, each project shifts based on audience, purpose, and genre. Seeing this progression helps clarify how a single subject can support multiple kinds of professional writing.

The table below uses a fictional but realistic scenario focused on copyright and Creative Commonsfor Maroon+OrangeDigital’ sTeen Artists’ Studio.

Table 1. Comparison of the Major Projects in the Course
Project Primary Audience Purpose What Changes What Stays the Same
Subject Selection Form Instructor Propose and justify topic Scope, rationale, formality Topic, community focus
Research Proposal Instructor Plan research and audiences Depth, sources, timeline Topic, service goal
User Document Teen artists Help users apply licenses Tone, format, step-by-step structure Accuracy, core concepts
Progress Report Instructor Report project status Focus on progress and challenges Topic, project goals
Informational Report Board of Directors Support decisions and policy Evidence, explanation level Topic, ethical framing
Fact Sheet Teens & families Provide quick public guidance Brevity, visuals, layout Key terms, takeaways

This example illustrates an important pattern in technical communication: the same topic can—and should—be communicated differently depending on who needs the information and why.

As you choose your own topic, use this model to check whether it can stretch across multiple audiences and document types while remaining clear, useful, and accurate. If you can imagine your topic working in each row of the table, you’ re likely on the right track.
