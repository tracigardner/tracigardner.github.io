One of the tricky things about the Proposal is that you’re writing with two different audiences in mind, and it’s easy to get them mixed up. Here’s a breakdown to help you keep them straight and understand what each one expects:

Traci is a Founding Owner and the Director of Project Development at Maroon + Orange Digital. Traci is the the primary audience for your proposal.

More Information about Your Manager, Traci

  • She is the person you are directly addressing in your proposal.
  • Your job is to convince her your proposed project is worthwhile.
  • She decides whether you proceed to research your potential client and write a recommendation report to them.

What Traci Needs to Approve Your Proposal

  • A clear understanding of the usability problem you want to solve.
  • Evidence that your team understands the client and their users.
  • A logical, well-organized plan for improving the website.

üíª Audience #2: Your Potential Client

Your potential client is the person responsible for the website your group has chosen. This person is the subject of your proposal.

More Information about Your Potential Client

  • You are writing about your potiential client’s website, not to your potential client.
  • You will identify this person (or group) in the Project Audience section of your proposal.
  • This person decides whether to implement the suggestions in your Recommendation Report.

What You Need to Know About Your Potential Client

  • Their business goals and target users.
  • Usability issues that could be hurting their effectiveness.
  • Their familiarity with technical or usability language.
  • Whether they need visuals, examples, or simple explanations.

TL;DR: You’re juggling two audiences

Let this breakdown guide both your writing decisions and how you describe your intended audience in the proposal.
