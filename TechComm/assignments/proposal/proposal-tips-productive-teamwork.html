11 Practical Tips for Productive Teamwork

  • Set a Shared Google Doc on Day One: Create a shared document where all team members can contribute ideas, take notes, and track progress.
  • Use a Simple Project Board: Set up a Trello or Notion board with columns like "To Do," "In Progress," and "Completed" to visualize progress.
  • Communicate in One Primary Channel: Avoid confusion by picking one messaging tool (e.g., a Slack channel or a group chat) for team discussions.
  • Agree on a Meeting Schedule Upfront: Set recurring check-ins (e.g., 30 minutes twice a week) to keep everyone accountable.
  • Make Meeting Notes Actionable: After every meeting, document tasks, deadlines, and who's responsible in the shared Google Doc.
  • Use Version Control for Documents: If working on shared files, use Google Docs' "suggesting mode" or Microsoft Word's "track changes" to manage edits.
  • Set Expectations for Response Time: Agree on how quickly team members should respond to messages (e.g., within 24 hours) to keep work moving.
  • Address Problems Privately First: If a team member isn’t contributing, message them directly before escalating the issue to the whole group.
  • Give Specific Feedback: Instead of saying "this section needs work," say "consider adding more evidence in the third paragraph to support our argument."
  • Rotate Who Runs the Meetings: Having a different person lead each session keeps everyone engaged and ensures shared responsibility.
  • Confirm Agreements in Writing: After decisions are made, post a short recap in the shared chat or document so there’s no confusion later.
