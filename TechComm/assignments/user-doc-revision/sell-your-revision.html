Announcement: Sell Your Revision by Showing Your Work

You may have had a teacher who talked about “showing, not telling” in your writing. The idea is pretty straightforward. It’s exactly what it says: Show the reader whatever you are exploring. Don’t just tell them about it. Make it vivid and visual. Leave the reader with a clear understanding of exactly what you are discussing.

You’ve probably been this strategy at work your entire life. Take look at Figure 1, before and after images of a pair of shoes. The first shoe has brown stains, and the second shoe is bright white and clean.

This before and after image is used on the HomeDepot website to sell OxiClean. The marketing strategy shows the reader what the product can do. “Just look at the difference between these two shoes,” it yells. “You could have these results too! Just buy OxiClean!”

Imagine the alternative. Perhaps you’re listening to a commercial on the radio or a podcast.
