The User Documentation Revision and Reflection Memo project focuses on revising a piece of user documentation and composing a reflection memo that explains your revision choices.

Length

Write or compose as much as you need to, but be sure to include all of the required information. There is no specific length requirement, just be sure to cover how to complete the task fully.

  • Revises an existing piece of user documentation by meeting the following requirements:
    • Was originally a webpage or PDF with instructions that someone in your intended career would use to complete a work task.
    • Revises convoluted, overly formal, and confusing language with plain, simple language.
    • Applies design principles to the document, including a title and internal headings, to approve its readability.
    • Addresses all ethical considerations to ensure that the user documentation is honest, inclusive, and accessible.
    • Ensures that legal disclaimers and policies are clear and legible and that citations are used properly.
    • Adapts the content for multiple readers, including international readers.
    • Is submitted as a PDF with your revised layout and design represented accurately.
  • Includes a reflection memo that provides the following information on the user documentation revision:
    • Begins with required memo headers.
    • Uses a clear and specific subject line for the memo.
    • Introduces the user documentation, providing a link to the original version or a reference to an uploaded version of the original, and ending with a purpose statement.
    • Includes a section on each of the following topics, each of which is connected to the course readings to explain the changes that you made:
      • A Plain Language Revision section that evaluates the original document’s usability in terms of its writing and that discusses the language with examples of the original design and your revision.
      • A Redesign section that evaluates the original document’s usability in terms of design and that discusses the design with examples of design principles that are broken in the original document and the revisions that you made to improve the document.
      • An Ethical, Legal, and Cultural Considerations section that evaluates the original document’s ethical, legal, and cultural considerations, indicating any considerations that are excluded and providing examples from the original document and your revisions that improve the document in these areas.
      • A Rhetorical Analysis section that introduces the changes you made to improve the document’s ability to connect with its audience.
    • Ends with a conclusion that reviews your evaluation of the original document and summarizes how your changes have improved the document, as well as provides contact information.
    • Uses standard memo format and document design, with appropriate spacing, headings, and page numbers.
