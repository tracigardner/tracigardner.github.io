This report, Understanding Copyright and Creative Commons for Teen Artists, was developed to support mentors working with artists-in-residence in the Teen Artists’ Studio. It provides an overview of intellectual property concepts—copyright, fair use, public domain, and Creative Commons licenses—to help inform decisions about protecting and sharing creative work.

The information in this report was gathered from reputable public sources, including the U.S. Copyright Office, Creative Commons, and the Artists Rights Society. The report organizes these sources into clear sections that explain key concepts and practical applications, focusing on accessible guidance rather than detailed legal analysis.

The report finds that intellectual property rights offer both economic benefits and protections for attribution, while copyright protection is automatic upon creation but can be difficult to enforce. It also reveals that the complexity of these rights can lead to confusion and may unintentionally limit how creative works are shared and reused.

Overall, the report highlights key aspects of intellectual property rights and their implications for creative work. These findings provide a foundation for understanding how such rights function within the context of supporting teen artists in the Studio.
