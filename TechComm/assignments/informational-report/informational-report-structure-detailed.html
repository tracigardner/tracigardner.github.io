Report Structure

Include these sections and information in this order:

  • Title Page (1 separate page)
    • Title of Your Report: Explanatory Subtitle of Your Report
    • Prepared for xxx
    • Prepared by Your Name
    • Date report submitted/prepared
  • Table of Contents (1 separate page)
    • Heading: Table of Contents
    • Main sections & subsections + related page numbers
    •
  • Executive Summary (1/2 page, on a separate page)
    • Heading: Executive Summary
    • xxx
  • Introduction (1/2 page, begins on a new page)
    • Heading: Introduction.
    • Information to include:
      • Topic Overview: Short explanation of what your report is about.
      • Purpose of the Report: Explain why you wrote the report.
      • Intended Audience: Identify your audience and why this report is useful for them.
      • Scope or Focus: Explain what the report will include and what it won’t include.
  • Discussion (5–7 pages)
    • Heading: Discussion
    • Use informational headings that describe the sub-sections of the Discussion section.

    You should explain the following:

    • what it is.
    • when it was developed (or discovered) and by whom.
    • where it originated and why.
    • what it involves.
    • how it works.
    • how it contributes to fairness, accessibility, and/or community benefit.

    You can even discuss future possibilities for the subject (areas of projected growth, potential for expanding access or impact, etc.)

  • Conclusion (1/2 page, continuing from previous section)
    • Heading: Conclusion
    • Information to include:
      • Restate the purpose.
      • Summarize the main points.
      • Emphasize significance.
      • Look to the future (optional).
  • Works Cited, References, or Bibliography (separate page)
    • Heading: Depends upon documentation style. May be Works Cited, References, or Bibliography.
    • List all of the sources you cited in the proposal itself, in the order required by your documentation style.
  • Appendices
    • Extra materials that are helpful but too detailed to fit in the body of the report, such as the following:
      • Interview transcripts
      • Survey questions & results
      • Full data sets
    • Each starts on a new page.
    • Title each in this pattern: Appendix 1: Name of the appendix
      Example: Appendix 1: Artists’ Interview Questions
