Review these sample scenarios and the related decision-making audiences for the Informational Report for Non-Expert Readers. These examples also apply to your Research Proposal, where you provide a clear, detailed plan for your Informational Report.

To be appropriate for the assignment, you’d update the scenarios and audiences for a specific location or situation. For example, consider the first subject, Improving Food Access in Urban Areas. You’d update that topic to a specific urban area (e.g., Richmond, Virginia). Likewise, you’d update the audience to specific groups or people (e.g., Richmond City Council or Dennis Hatchett, President and CEO of Feed More).

Sample Scenarios & Decision-Making Audiences

  • Improving Food Access in Urban Areas
    • Audience: City Council Members or the Director of a Local Food Bank
    • Scenario: The city is evaluating how to improve access to affordable, healthy food in underserved neighborhoods.
  • Expanding Public Transportation Options
    • Audience: Regional Transit Authority Board
    • Scenario: Transportation planners are reviewing proposals to improve access for low-income communities and individuals with disabilities.
  • Improving Digital Access in Rural Areas
    • Audience: State Broadband Task Force or Local School District Technology Director
    • Scenario: The community is considering investments in broadband infrastructure for underserved rural students.
  • Improving Accessibility in Public Parks
    • Audience: Local Parks and Recreation Department or Municipal Accessibility Coordinator
    • Scenario: A recent review shows several parks lack accessible paths or inclusive playground equipment.
  • Addressing Housing Displacement from Gentrification
    • Audience: Housing and Urban Development Committee or Nonprofit Housing Trust
    • Scenario: Neighborhood redevelopment has led to rising rents and the displacement of longtime residents.
  • Increasing Voter Access for Marginalized Communities
    • Audience: County Election Commission
    • Scenario: Community groups have requested expanded early voting sites and multilingual ballot materials.
  • Providing Mental Health Resources for LGBTQ+ Youth
    • Audience: Local School Board or Director of a Youth Advocacy Organization
    • Scenario: School counselors and community advocates are asking for increased mental health support for LGBTQ+ students.
  • Supporting Formerly Incarcerated Individuals in the Job Market
    • Audience: Workforce Development Board or Chamber of Commerce Diversity Task Force
    • Scenario: Local employers are exploring ways to make hiring practices more inclusive.
  • Improving Language Access in Healthcare Facilities
    • Audience: Hospital Administration or Public Health Board
    • Scenario: A regional health provider wants to better serve patients with limited English proficiency.
  • Addressing Environmental Health Risks in Low-Income Communities
    • Audience: Environmental Justice Committee or State Department of Environmental Quality
    • Scenario: A recent study revealed higher asthma rates in a low-income neighborhood near industrial sites.
  • Expanding Inclusive Curriculum in Public Schools
    • Audience: School Board or Curriculum Review Committee
    • Scenario: Parents and students are asking schools to include more diverse voices and histories in the curriculum.
  • Improving Access to Public Restrooms for Unhoused Individuals
    • Audience: City Council or Downtown Business Improvement District
    • Scenario: Advocates have requested 24-hour restroom access to improve hygiene and dignity for unhoused populations.
