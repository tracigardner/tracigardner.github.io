You’ll identify the audience for your Informational Report for Non-Expert Readers in your Research Proposal. This audience should be a real, concrete, and specific group of people. You should be able not only to name them specifically but also to find a real mailing address for the group. In fact, you’ll need that mailing address for your report.

To choose an audience that works for the Informational Report assignment, you’d update the scenarios and audiences for a specific location or situation.

For example, consider the subject “Food Access in Urban Areas.” You’d update that general focus on “Urban Areas” to a specific urban area (e.g., Richmond, Virginia). Likewise, you’d update the audience to specific groups or people—such as the Richmond City Council or Dennis Hatchett, President and CEO of Feed More.

Review these sample scenarios and the related decision-making audiences for the Informational Report for Non-Expert Readers. These examples also apply to your Research Proposal, where you provide a clear, detailed plan for your Informational Report.

Sample Scenarios & Decision-Making Audiences

  • Food Access in Urban Areas
    • Audience:
      • General: City Council Members or the Director of a Local Food Bank
      • Specific: Feed More, a food bank in Richmond, VA. Contact: Dennis Hatchett, President and CEO
    • Scenario: The city is evaluating how to improve access to affordable, healthy food in underserved neighborhoods.
  • Accessible Public Transportation Options
    • Audience:
      • General: Regional Transit Authority Board
      • Specific: Chairperson Jennifer Liptak of the Pittsburgh Regional Transit Board
    • Scenario: Transportation planners are reviewing proposals to improve access for low-income communities and individuals with disabilities.
  • Digital Access in Rural Areas
    • Audience:
      • General: State Broadband Task Force or Local School District Technology Director
      • Specific: Robert Ratcliffe, Director of Technology, Montgomery County Public Schools (Christiansburg, VA)
    • Scenario: The community is considering investments in broadband infrastructure for underserved rural students.
  • Accessibility in Public Parks
    • Audience:
      • General: Local Parks and Recreation Department or Municipal Accessibility Coordinator
      • Specific: Shay Dunnigan, Director of Parks & Recreation, Pulaski County, VA
    • Scenario: A recent review shows several parks lack accessible paths or inclusive playground equipment.
  • Housing Displacement from Gentrification
    • Audience:
      • General: Housing and Urban Development Committee or Nonprofit Housing Trust
      • Specific: Tom Okuda Fitzpatrick, Executive Director, Housing Opportunities Made Equal of Virginia, Inc. (HOME)
    • Scenario: Neighborhood redevelopment has led to rising rents and the displacement of longtime residents.
  • Voter Access in Marginalized Communities
    • Audience:
      • General: County Election Commission
      • Specific: Board of Directors of the League of Women Voters of Greater Roanoke Valley, Contact: Dianna Wynn, President
    • Scenario: Community groups have requested expanded early voting sites and multilingual ballot materials.
  • Providing Mental Health Resources for Youth
    • Audience:
      • General: Local School Board or Director of a Youth Advocacy Organization
      • Specific: Board of Directors of New River Valley Community Services. Contact: Mary Biggs, Chair
    • Scenario: School counselors and community advocates are asking for increased mental health support for students.
  • Supporting Formerly Incarcerated Individuals in the Job Market
    • Audience:
      • General: Workforce Development Board or Chamber of Commerce Diversity Task Force
      • Specific: New River/Mt. Rogers Workforce Development Board, Michael Miller, Chair
    • Scenario: Local employers are exploring ways to make hiring practices more inclusive.
  • Language Access in Healthcare Facilities
    • Audience:
      • General: Hospital Administration or Public Health Board
      • Specific: Carilion New River Valley Medical Center Board of Directors, J. Thomas (Tom) Ryan, M.D., Chair
    • Scenario: A regional health provider wants to better serve patients with limited English proficiency.
  • Environmental Health in Low-Income Communities
    • Audience:
      • General: Regional Health Board or State Department of Environmental Quality
      • Specific: Blue Ridge Regional Office of the Virginia Department of Environmental Quality. Contact: Regional Director Robert Weld
    • Scenario: A recent study revealed higher asthma rates in a low-income neighborhood near industrial sites.
  • Financial Literacy Curriculum in Public Schools
    • Audience:
      • General: School Board or Curriculum Review Committee
      • Specific: Kathy Burant, Chair of Rockbridge County Public Schools
    • Scenario: Parents and students are asking schools to include more financial literacy in the curriculum.
