Understanding your audience is a key skill in technical writing. Before you begin writing your informational report, you need to understand the audience you’re writing for. A well-informed audience analysis will help you shape a report that not only informs, but also persuades—giving your audience the tools they need to act on an issue that matters.

This activity will help you identify what your audience already knows, what they need to learn, and how best to present your information so that it is clear, useful, and persuasive.

Assignment Overview

Choose the scenario you will focus on for your report. Then, identify your intended audience: a specific group of non-expert decision-makers who have the authority to act on the issue. Example audiences include local government officials, nonprofit leaders, community organization staff, or school administrators.

Answer each of the questions below to analyze your audience for the Informational Report. The answers to all the questions should add up to 500–700 words, but don’t worry if it’s a little long or a little short.

Assignment Objectives

Hide the Learning Objectives Show the Learning Objectives
  • Understand the role of audience in shaping the content, tone, and purpose of an informational report.
  • Identify specific decision-making audiences and analyze their authority, responsibilities, and interests.
  • Assess what an audience already knows—and what they need to know—to fully understand a social justice or equity-related issue.
  • Determine the types of evidence and examples that will be most persuasive to a particular audience.
  • Anticipate audience concerns, questions, or objections, and plan how to address them respectfully.
  • Practice writing clearly and concisely using headings, bullet points, and accessible language.
  • Strengthen research and critical thinking skills by examining real-world stakeholders and their information needs.
  • Build a foundation for writing purpose-driven, reader-centered documents in academic and professional settings.
