Purpose: Your letter of transmittal introduces your report to its audience. It provides context, explains purpose, and sets an objective tone before the report begins. It’s the first thing your reader sees, so it’s important to make a good impression.

Length: Keep it brief—usually one page, with 3–4 short paragraphs.

Letter Content: See the assignment and the details in Section 9e of the course textbook.
