Writing a
Letter of Transmittal

The Task

Part of the Informational Report (Full report worth 25%)

Write a letter of transmittal for your report, which will serve as the first page of your report. In your letter, focus on the big picture so that your audience has all the basic information needed to determine whether to read the full report.

What Is a Letter of Transmittal?

A 1–2 page cover letter that introduces readers to a document that follows, such as a report or proposal. Your letter of transmittal

  • Explains the purpose.
  • Provides context.
  • Sets an appropriate tone.
 

Essential Details

Design for impact. Your letter is the first thing your reader sees, so it’s important to make a good impression.

Make it easy to navigate and understand. Your readers busy, but they know they’re not experts. They want details so they can make the right decision—but they want to make up their own minds!

 

 

What to Include

  • Return Address: List a full mailing address for yourself (It can be fake. I don’t need to know where you live). Alternatively, use your program/university letterhead if permitted.
  • Date: Use a full month, date, and year. Immediately below the return address (no extra line space between them).
  • Inside address: List the full name, title, organization, mailing address of the decision-maker(s).
  • Subject line: Optional but recommended. Add a short, informative subject.
  • Salutation: Add your professional greeting (e.g., “Dear Mayor Collins and Members of the Pulaski Town Council:”). Follow with a colon, since this is a formal letter.
  • Message Body: Include the following paragraphs:
    • Paragraph 1: Introduction: State the purpose of the letter and identify what you’re transmitting.
    • Paragraph 2: Project Overview: Explain the subject the report addresses, and summarize your methods and/or scope.
    • Paragraph 3: Key Findings: Highlight 2–3 key findings/insights.
    • Paragraph 4: Conclusion & Contact Info: Thank the reader, offer availability for questions/presentation, and specify how to reach you.
  • Complimentary Close: Add the closing (e.g., “Sincerely,”).
  • Signature: Skip space for your signature. You can insert a graphic of your signature or use a cursive font.
  • Typed Name: List your name, your title/role, and your department/program, as appropriate.
  • Enclosure Notation: Add details on the attached report (e.g., “Enclosure: Informational Report on ___”).
 

 

Formatting Details

  • Length: Stick to one page if you can.
  • Page size: 8.5 x 11 (portrait)
  • Format: Text running from left to right margin, and 1" margins all around.
  • Spacing:
    • Single or 1.15 spacing between lines.
    • Double space between paragraphs.
  • Paragraphs: Do not indent (Block letter format).
  • Font Choices: Arial, Calibri, Verdana, Aptos, or Helvetica. Sans serif fonts are easier to read online.
  • Visuals: Not needed. It’s okay if letterhead has a logo.
  • Help: See the Letter of Transmittal Example with Formatting Details and the Annotated Letter Format page.
