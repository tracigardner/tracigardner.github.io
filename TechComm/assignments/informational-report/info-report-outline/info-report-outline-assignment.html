Your Task

Review the information required in your Informational Report, and write a topic outline for your draft, focusing on the specific headings and subheadings.

Your topic outline should do the following:

  • plan specific headings and subheadings for your report.
  • sketch out the work you need to do to write the report.

Assignment Learning Objectives

Hide the Learning Objectives Show the Learning Objectives

This assignment will develop your ability to:

  • Write a formal outline as a planning document.
  • Choose strong, information-rich headings and subheadings that will help readers navigate the finished report.
  • Use clear, plain language for headings and subheadings to ensure non-expert decision-makers will understand.
  • Tailor tone and emphasis of headings and subheadings to a real, local audience with concrete needs and constraints.

Your Audience

Your readers are members of a non-expert audience that is responsible for making a decision about the subject covered in your report.

The information in your outline should be the skeleton structure for your report. Your readers won’t see your outline itself, but they WILL see your outline’s headings, subheadings, and related information in your Informational Report.

Assignment Requirements

Basic Requirements

  • Genre: Formal Topic Outline.
  • Length: About 1–2 pages.

Formatting Requirements

  • Page size: 8.5 x 11 (portrait)
  • Format: Text running from left to right margin, and 1" margins all around.
  • Spacing:
    • Single or 1.15 spacing between lines.
    • Double space for clarity as needed.
  • Outline Alignment: Use hierarchical indentation. Find help in “10.1 Creating an Outline” from Technical Writing Essentials.
  • Font Choices: Arial, Calibri, Verdana, Aptos, or Helvetica. Choose one, and use it consistently.

Outline Structure

Include information in this order:

  • Document Title: Use the working title of your Informational Report for Non-Expert Readers.
  • Highest Outline Level:
    • Include all of the sections outlined in the Report Structure section of the Informational Report for Non-Expert Readers Assignment.
    • Adapt the information for your topic, if desired. For instance, rather than “Discussion”, you might use “Discussion of Food Security in Wilmington.”
  • Lower Outline Level(s):
    • Include specific topics and subtopics (as needed) that show what you will cover in each section.
    • Use parallel and consistent phrasing. In other words, use phrasing that matches in structure, capitalization, and punctuation.

Success Tips

  • Remember your outline is a planning document. It’s okay if you need to change your headings when you write your report.
  • Focus on strong phrasing. Each heading and subheading should tell readers exactly what the section will cover.
  • Use plain language, and avoid jargon. If your readers don’t know what the headings and subheadings mean, they won’t know what the section discusses.
