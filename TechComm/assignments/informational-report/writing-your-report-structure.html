Informational Report Document Order

 

Assembling Your Report

Arrange your informational report in a single document file (*.docx or *.pdf) in the following order.

 

1

 

Letter of Transmittal

  • Starts on a new page.
  • No heading.
  • No page number

Instructions: Writing a Letter of Transmittal

 

2

 

Title Page

  • Starts on a new page.
  • Heading: Your report title.
  • No page number.

Instructions: Writing a Title Page

 

3

 

Executive Summary

  • Starts on a new page.
  • Uses heading: Executive Summary
  • Begin page numbers with the number 1, at the bottom center or bottom right of the page.

Instructions: Writing an Executive Summary

 

4

 

Introduction

  • Starts on a new page.
  • Uses heading: Executive Summary
  • Continue page numbers from previous page.

Instructions: Writing an Introduction

 

5

 

Discussion

  • Continue on the same page as the previous section.
  • Uses heading: Executive Summary
  • Continue page numbers from previous page.

Instructions: Writing a Discussion Section

 

6

 

Conclusion

  • Continue on the same page as the previous section.
  • Uses heading: Executive Summary
  • About ½ page long.
  • Continue page numbers from previous page.

Instructions: Writing an Conclusion

 

7

 

Works Cited, References, or Bibliography

  • Starts on a new page.
  • Uses the heading xxx from your field’s style system (for instance, Works Cited).
  • As long as needed.
  • Continue page numbers from previous page.

Instructions: Writing an Conclusion

 

8

 

Appendices

A 1-page overview of a longer document that highlights its main purpose, key points, and primary conclusions or findings.
