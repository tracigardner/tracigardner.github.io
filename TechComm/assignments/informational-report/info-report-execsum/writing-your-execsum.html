What Is an Executive Summary?

A 1-page overview of a longer document that highlights its main purpose, key points, and primary conclusions or findings.

It allows readers to quickly understand the essential information without reading the entire document.

 

Essential Details

Focus on clarity. Your summary must make sense on its own, without requiring readers to consult the full report for basic understanding.

Use an objective, professional tone. Present the information clearly and neutrally, avoiding technical overload, or excessive background.

 

 

What to Include

  • Heading: Executive Summary
     Use Heading 1 in your word processor, and state: Executive Summary
  • Paragraph 1: Context and Purpose
    Briefly introduce the topic, establish the context, and state the report’s focus and scope.
  • Paragraph 2: Research Ovevriew
    Summarize how the information was gathered or organized (e.g., research methods, data sources, criteria, or structure).
  • Paragraph 3: Key Findings
    Present the most important facts, trends, or insights the report reveals.
  • Paragraph 4: Conclusions and Implications Explain what the findings collectively show and why they matter for the intended audience.
 

 

Formatting Details

  • Length: Stick to one page if you can.
  • Page size: 8.5 x 11 (portrait)
  • Format: Text running from left to right margin, and 1" margins all around.
  • Spacing:
    • Single or 1.15 spacing between lines.
    • Double space between paragraphs.
  • Paragraphs: Do not indent (Block letter format).
  • Font Choices: Arial, Calibri, Verdana, Aptos, or Helvetica. Sans serif fonts are easier to read online.
  • Visuals: Not needed. It’s okay if letterhead has a logo.
