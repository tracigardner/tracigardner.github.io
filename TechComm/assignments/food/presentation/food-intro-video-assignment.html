What I Want You to Do

Create an introduction video with audio that tells the class who you are and shares a personal food story. Your video should be about 3–5 minutes long.

You can record a simple video of yourself talking or use a few simple slides. I know some people do not like being on camera, so choose the option you are most comfortable with.

Why I Want You to Do It

You will give many presentations in the workplace, and a lot of them will begin with a self-introduction. You should become comfortable with telling a group who you are and providing enough additional information for listeners to understand why you presenting. Storytelling is one effective way to draw your audience in and make connections with them. This short and informal presentation lets you practice both of those skills.

How You Do It

  1. Review the Presentation Criteria to understand the requirements for the Full Draft.
  2. Skim through the information in Chapter 21 of the textbook.
  3. Decide on the basic personal information that you want to share, being sure to include the following:
    • Your name (what you want us to call you)
    • Your area of study/major
    • What’s next for you: a particular job? further studies?
    • Any additional personal information you want to share (e.g., preferred pronouns, clubs or Greek organizations, military experience).
  4. Choose a food story that you will help the class get to know you:
    • Pick a story your audience (college students at Virginia Tech) will find interesting or entertaining.
    • Choose a story about yourself, your family (however you define it), or your cultural background.
    • Select a story that you can tell in 2 or 3 minutes.
    • Consult the Food Story Ideas page for possible topics.
  5. Combine all your notes from the activities above (in Steps #3 and #4), using the information in the textbook and on the Tip Sheet: Introduction Presentation to create a script of what you will say in your video.
  6. If you decide to use slides, design the slides to display in your video, using advice from the General Guidelines for Powerpoint Presentations to guide your design.
  7. Record your video, using whatever software or app you are comfortable with, using the links below for help:
    • Turn Your PowerPoint Slides into a Video
    • Ways to Record Your Presentation
    • Using Kaltura to Create Your Video
  8. Success Tip
    You should be able to answer “True” to each question before you submit your work to the Full Draft Submission. If you do not meet each of the criteria listed, your draft will be marked Incomplete and you’ll need to revise.
    Review your draft by comparing it to the Presentation Self-Check.
  9. Choose one of these options based on your Self-Check:
    • If you answered “True” to every question, move on to the next step. You’re ready to submit your work.
    • If you did not answer “True” to every question, follow this process:
      • Return to your draft and revise so that you can answer “Yes.”
      • Review your draft with the Presentation Self-Check after you revise.
        • If you answered “True” to every question, move on to the next step. You’re ready to submit your work.
        • If you did not answer “True” to every question, continue revising until you can.
