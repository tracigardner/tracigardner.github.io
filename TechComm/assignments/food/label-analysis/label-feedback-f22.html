If You Have an Incomplete

If you have an incomplete marked for your Label Analysis Assignment, you can still earn a Complete on the project.

  • If the project was marked Incomplete and reassigned
    Revise your project to improve your work, adding whatever is missing or tweaking the design. Resubmit your project, and I will review your work again.
  • If you did not submit your work at all
    It is not too late. Turn in your work and I will give you feedback.

The Label Analysis Assignment has a grace period that ends at 11:59 PM on Friday, April 28. You have until the end of the grace period, but completing the work sooner will help ensure that you have time to work on the other assignments in the course.

Specific Feedback

  • Meet the requirements of the assignment. Be sure that you delete the questions in the form and then fill it out completely. Every cell in the table needs details from the label.
  • Use these examples to help you revise. These are drafts from other students in the course, shared anonymously, per the policy in the Course Policies Manual:
    • Clif Bar Food Label Analysis
    • Oikos Yogurt Food Label Analysis
    • Trail Mix Food Label Analysis
  • Include a specific title and subheadings. Your title and subheadings should tell the reader enough to know exactly what the document or section is about. Remember that you are analyzing the product’s label, not the product itself.
  • Label your photos. In addition to adding images of all the sides of your product’s packaging, include numbers and titles for each image (for instance, Image 1: Front of the package). Use the design principle of proximity by placing the image number and title under each of the images.
  • Link your images to your analysis.  Strengthen your analysis by adding specific connections between the photos and the description of the evidence. Refer directly to the photos by figure number and describe the details in the images that support your analysis.
  • Get help from the Writing Center (optional). Anyone can visit the Writing Center in the library for help with projects for the course. Writing Center tutors can help you strengthen your content as well as work on document design and other issues. You can read more about the Writing Center and make an appointment on the Writing Center’s webpage.
