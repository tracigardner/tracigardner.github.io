Here are answers to questions I have received related to the optional activities in the course.

Where are the optional points explained?
The points system is explained in the Course Manual. See the information on “Additional, Optional Points in the Course.” You can find more recent details on the Optional Activities Overview page.
Where do I find the optional reading activities?
Optional readings have been posted every class day (excluding Mondays and Spring Break Days) since February 2. Links to these readings appear under the “Canvas Announcements & Course Advice” heading on the Course Home page. These posts are also referred to in the weekly Updates Announcements that are posted every Monday and in the Module Instructions for each week.
Are there additional details on how the Optional Reading Activities work?
Yes, read the Optional Reading Activities Instructions.
I missed some of the optional readings for February? Is every activity required to earn an A?
No, you can do some basic math to figure out what is required. The weekly modules guarantee you a B, or 830 points, if you do all the work. An A requires 930 points or more, and there are 170 optional points in total. Math: 930 -830 = 100 optional points needed. Since there are 170 optional points available, there are 70 more points than you need for an A.
What optional activities will be available?
These are the activities that will be available and the total number of points for each category:
  • Reading optional posts, which frequently include watching LinkedIn Learning videos.
    Each post is worth 1 point, and the optional reading activities are worth up to 50 points total.
  • Writing additional short documents.
    Each of eight, short documents is worth up to 10 points each. These optional writing activities are worth up to 80 points total.
  • Completing the final exam.
    The optional final exam focuses on writing a performance review. The final exam is worth up to 40 points total.

You can complete any combination of optional activities to earn additional points. A point is worth the same amount no matter how you earn it.
