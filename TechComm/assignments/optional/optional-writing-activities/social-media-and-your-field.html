How You Do It

  1. Decide how you will define social media. It can include many tools. Choose one in particular or several. Be sure you have a specific social media in mind.
  2. Consider the following possible issues to get started:
    • Social media and the way people  work in your field. 
    • Social media and interaction with clients or customers.
    • Social media and interaction with the public generally.
    You can add another issue if you like or combine issues if appropriate to what you want to discuss.
  3. Once you have an issue in mind, take a stance, using the following positions to get started:
    • Social media has improved the issue.
    • Social media has changed the issue.
    • Social media has weakened or undermined the issue.
    You can add or change positions, as you like. Do not feel limited to the three listed.
  4. Combine all the decisions you have made into a single statement of your position. For example, you might state your position as “Social media like Instagram has complicated interactions with clients in the field of finance.”
  5. In your response, state your position, and then skip a line and explain why have you taken your stance. Include specific examples or details to support your stance. Remember to include facts and details. Your opinion alone is not enough.
  6. Once you have posted your stance, review the positions of others in the course and add on to the conversation.
