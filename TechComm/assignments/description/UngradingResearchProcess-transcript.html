Details on the research process I followed to learn more about how to use #ungrading in the college classroom.

Secondary Research: Finding books, journal articles, websites, & webinars

  1. Search the VT Libraries databases for resources using the keywords ungrading, grading, writing assessment, & writing feedback.
  2. Skim/Read identified sources for details on history of grading, ungrading approaches, and feedback strategies. Harvest additional sources from source bibliographies.
  3. Search Twitter, Facebook, & other online sources for the keywords ungrading, grading, writing assessment, & writing feedback. Search for social media by authors of sources found in Steps 1 & 2.
  4. Sort & narrow collection of sources to those most inclusive and relevant to the topic. Ensure foundational texts included in narrowed collection.
  5. Review sources for coverage of history & background on grading practices, development of ungrading, and strategies for implementing ungrading. Find additional sources if any details missing.

Noteworthy

Ungrading: Why Rating Students Undermines Learning (and What to Do Instead)

This collection gathers teacher perspectives on ungrading. Edited by Susan Blum, the book is a key resource for learning why ungrading is adopted & how to use the strategy.
