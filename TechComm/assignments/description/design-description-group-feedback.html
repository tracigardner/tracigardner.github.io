Specific Feedback

  • Meet the requirements of the assignment. The assignment called for a technical description of a design principle. Unfortunately many students instead described flaws and potential improvements for the websites they were analyzing. These documents on the wrong topic need to be revised to be marked Complete. To begin that process, reread the assignment and follow the step-by-step instructions to describe a design principle. Be sure to use the resources in the "Where You Can Find Help" section on that page.
  • Use these two examples to help you revise. These are drafts from other students in the course, shared anonymously:
    • Design Description Example 1
    • Design Description Example 2
  • Include a specific title. Your title should tell the reader enough to know exactly what the document is about.
  • Use document design to help readers navigate your memo. Apply the what you learn about design principles to your own draft as you write. For example, if you discuss the design principle of contrast, be sure that you have used contrast effectively in your own draft.
  • Integrate images smoothly. Place the screenshot images in your document near the text that mentions them and refer to them in the document. Be sure to explain how the illustrations connect to design principle you are describing. Show the reader exactly how the images demonstrate the concept.
  • Get help from the Writing Center (optional). Anyone can visit the Writing Center in the library for help with projects for the course. Writing Center tutors can help you strengthen your content as well as work on document design and other issues. You can read more about the Writing Center and make an appointment on the Writing Center’s webpage.
