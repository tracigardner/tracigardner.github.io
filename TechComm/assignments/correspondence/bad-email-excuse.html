Why to Do It

Okay, I admit that you may consider this task busywork, but it’s fun busywork.

This assignment asks you to compose a rough draft that you can use to practice the Feedback Discussions process.

Instructions

Writing Scenario

This week you have the chance to do your very worst work. Yes, you read that correctly. I want your rough draft to be awful.

Imagine that you have been offline for a few days. As a result, you have fallen behind on your work for the class. Write an email message to me about your situation, but make it a terrible message, following these guidelines:

  • Make up any additional details you need.
  • Feel free to ignore the advice from the How to Email Your Professor page of the Short Guide.
  • Even though you are writing a bad message, make sure your email  draft is appropriate for the class. 

Everyone in the class will eventually be able to read your message. The underlying goal of this activity is to create a draft that your classmates can provide formative feedback on, so you will also post your draft in Piazza.

Composing Your Rough Draft

  1. Review the readings on correspondence and email messages, since you will violate the advice they provide:
    • Chapter 14 of Markel & Selber, “Corresponding in Print and Online”
    • Email Etiquette, from the Purdue OWL
    • Strong Subject Lines
    • How to Email Your Professor
  2. Create a rough draft of your project that responds to the writing scenario above. Keep the following expectations in mind:
    • Technology Requirement: Use your word processor or Google Docs.
    • Fake the email headings (To, From, and Subject).
    • Make your message humorously incorrect if you want. Have fun with it.
  3. Share your rough draft in this week’s Feedback Discussion (in Piazza), following the instructions on the Feedback Discussions for Your Bad Email Excuse page.
  4. Submit your rough draft:
    • Submit your rough draft here by 11:59 PM on Friday, September 4.
    • If you need more time, submit your rough draft by 11:59 PM on Tuesday, September 8. You have an additional day in the grace period this week because of the Labor Day holiday.

Obtaining the Points for Your Work

You track and grade your own work in this course. Be sure to complete the following tasks:

  • Track your work in your Weekly Work Log.
  • Post your rough draft in Piazza with a specific question, for this week’s Feedback Discussion.
  • Claim points for submitting your rough draft when you complete the Week of 08/31 Self-Assessment in Canvas.
