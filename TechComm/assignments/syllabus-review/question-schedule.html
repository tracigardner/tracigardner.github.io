Because this is an online course, your time management matters a lot—especially since this is an asynchronous course. We have no planned meeting times, so you have to plan specific times to get your work done.

Suggested Work Flow

The work flow below suggests one way to spread your work across the week. Most weeks, plan on working 8 to 12 hours on the course. That said, everyone works differently, and we all have other obligations. Adjust this schedule to it work for you.

Day of the Week What to Do
Monday
  • Check the Module Instructions in Canvas for the specific activities for the week.
  • Complete Peer Feedback Discussions for your classmates. .
Tuesday
  • Read the textbook and other readings, looking for details that will help you complete the activities.
Wednesday
  • Complete writing activities that will help you write your draft.
  • Use feedback from your classmates to revise your draft from the previous week.
Thursday
  • Compose a rough draft of your project.
  • Continue work on any other readings and activities for the week.
Friday
  • Compose a cover letter for your draft.
  • Post your cover letter and draft in Discussions by 11:59 PM. Canvas automatically assigns peer feedback partners at 12:00 AM Saturday.
Weekend
  • Take a break. No one should work all the time!

More information in “Typical Weekly Schedule,” from A Short Guide to English 3764: Technical Writing.
