Choosing a Task
for Your User Document QUIZ

Instructions: Use this process to find and then test the task and audience for your User Document. You must be able to answer “Yes” to every test question to have a task that works for your User Document.

Gather Your Ideas

Instructions

  1. Begin with your approved semester-long topic.
  2. Brainstorm specific tasks someone interested in the topic might complete.
  3. Eliminate anything your list that are not interested in.
  4. Choose a task for your step-by-step instructions from what’s left.

Example Brainstorming

  • choosing a Creative Commons license.
  • adding a copyright to your work.
  • adding a Creative Commons license to your work.
  • determining how you can reuse someone else’s work that is protected by a specific Creative Commons license.
  • testing whether something falls under fair use.

The Semester-Long Topic Test

Answer the Test Questions

  • Is your the task for your User Document specifically related to your Semester-Long Topic?
  • Is it a task that someone learning about your Semester-Long Topic would want to learn about?

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next test.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a topic that does.

Example Response to Test Question

Yes. My semester-long topic is copyright and Creative Commons. I will write my User Document about how to choose a Creative Commons license for your work. This task directly relates to my semester-long topic since it’s one of the things someone can do once they understand what Creative Commons is and how it works.

The User Need Test

Answer the Test Questions

  • Does your task address a real user need?
  • Will readers be able to use a User Document on the task to better understand, use, decide about, or troubleshoot something related to your Semester-Long Topic?

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next test.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a topic that does.

Example Response to Test Question

Yes. Writers and artists need to know how to protect the ownership of their work. One way people can do this is to apply a Creative Commons license to their work. My User Document will provide step-by-step instructions for choosing a license to protect a writer’s or artist’s work.

The Ut Prosim Test

Answer the Test Questions

  • Does writing about the task have the potential to serve others and contribute to the greater good?
  • Does it have the potential to create a positive impact?

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next test.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a topic that does.

Example Response to Test Question

Yes. Helping writers and artists learn how to choose a Creative Commons license is a positive service because it will give them the power to protect their creative work and give them the ability to share their work without fear they will lose ownership. Writing about this task should have a definite positive impact on the Maroon + Orange Digital’s Teen Artists’ Studio.

The Non-Expert Audience Test

Answer the Test Questions

  • Is the audience for your User Document made up of non-expert users—such as new users, community members, caregivers, students, or volunteers?
  • Do most readers lack formal training in this topic?
  • Will most readers need definitions for common jargon or acronyms related to this topic?

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next test.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a topic that does.

Example Response to Test Question

Yes. The details of Creative Commons licensing are not common knowledge. People may see the license in use on sites like Wikipedia, but they not experts on the system or how it works.

My audience for the User Document is the writers and artists who work in Maroon + Orange Digital’s Teen Artists’ Studio. Because they are teens still learning and exploring their art, they are particularly unlikely to know about the Creative Commons licensing system. They will need full explanations and definitions for the system.

The Specific, Local Audience Test

Answer the Test Questions

  • Is your audience specific people from a community group, a city or town, a county, or a similar local area or group?
  • Can you state the specific name of the local area or group?
  • Can you identify the members of the local area or group by their name, position, or role?

Evaluate Your Answer

  • If you answered, “Yes,” move to the next test.
  • If you answered, “No,” stop and choose a topic that does.

Example Response to Test Question

Yes. My audience is made up of community members of Maroon + Orange Digital’s Teen Artists’ Studio. They are teen writers and artists who are in residence at the Studio, where they create and share their art.
