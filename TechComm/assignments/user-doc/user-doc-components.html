Components of User Documents

1
Title
Clear, concise; use “how to” phrasing or a gerund (-ing verb).
2
Date
Indicates currency and version context.
3
Table of Contents (optional)
for multi-task, multi-section, or manual-length documents
4
Introduction
State task and scope, audience/background needs, purpose/outcome, when to use, and an overview.
5
General Notices
Notes, Cautions, Warnings, Dangers to highlight risks or key exceptions.
6
Technical Background or Theory
(Optional): Concepts/definitions needed to understand the procedure.
7
List of Equipment and Supplies
Tools, equipment, and consumables (add specs like brand, size, amount as needed).
8
Directions (Steps)
Step-by-step instructions with appropriate formatting and visuals:
  • Fixed-order steps: Numbered list.
  • Variable-order steps: Bulleted list.
  • Alternate steps: Bulleted options with “OR.”
  • Nested steps: Indented substeps (a, b, c...).
  • “Stepless” instructions: General guidance when steps don’t fit.
9
Conclusion
Wrap-up, troubleshooting guidance, and contact information.
10
Back Matter
References, glossary/appendix, index, technical specifications.
