Components of User Documents

1
Title
Identifies the topic of the user document clearly and concisely. Uses “How toâ€¦” phrasing or a gerund phrase (-ing verb).
2
Date
Indicates currency and version context.
3
Table of Contents (optional)
Is included when the document has multiple tasks, sections, or is manual-length.
4
Introduction
  • States the task and scope.
  • Describes audience knowledge or background needs.
  • Explains the purpose or outcome.
  • Indicates when to use the instructions.
  • Provides an overview.
5
General Notices (optional)
  • Uses Danger to show risk of death or severe injury.
  • Uses Warning to show risk of harm or major damage.
  • Uses Caution to show possible risk.
  • Adds Note to provide details or troubleshooting tips.
6
Technical Background or Theory (optional)
Provides definitions or explains concepts needed to understand the procedure.
7
List of Equipment and Supplies
  • Lists tools, equipment, and consumables.
  • Includes specifications such as brand, size, amount, or type.
8
Directions (Steps)
  • Uses step-by-step lists, following this guidance:
    • If the steps must be done in order, use a numbered list.
    • If the steps can be done in any order, use a bulleted list.
    • If there is more than one option for the steps, use bulleted options with “or.”
    • If there are nested steps, use indented substeps (a, b, c...).
    • If the steps offer general guidance or steps don’t fit, use stepless instructions.
  • Adds visuals to support the steps.
9
Conclusion
  • Summarizes the process.
  • Provides troubleshooting guidance.
  • Lists contact information.
10
Back Matter (optional)
  • Includes references.
  • Adds glossary, appendix, or index.
  • Provides technical specifications.
