AI Workflow + Reflection Tracker

What I Want You to Do

Each time you use AI in a writing assignment, I want you to complete an AI Workflow + Reflection Tracker entry. This short activity helps you document how you used the AI tool, what it produced, and what you thought about that process. You’ll attach your tracker as an appendix when you turn in your assignment.

Why I Want You to Do It

This assignment helps you think critically about how AI tools fit into your writing process. It’s not just about what the AI gives you—it’s about what you decide to keep, change, or ignore. This kind of reflection builds your confidence, strengthens your decision-making, and helps you use AI ethically and responsibly in both academic and professional writing.

How to Do It

Follow the steps below and submit your tracker along with your final draft:

  1. Write your responses in a document or slide deck (or record audio/video if you prefer)
  2. Label the file or section clearly: AI Workflow + Reflection Tracker for [Assignment Name or Activity]. Here are some examples:
    • AI Workflow + Reflection Tracker for Proposal Memo Brainstorming
    • AI Workflow + Reflection Tracker for Proposal Self-Check
  3. Include these details in your tracker:
    • Tool(s) Used + Purpose: In 2–3 sentences, explain which AI tool(s) you used and what you were trying to do (e.g., brainstorming ideas, revising tone, generating headings).
    • Example AI Output: Paste a short excerpt or screenshot of what the AI produced. Highlight something you used, changed, or discarded.
    • Your Reflection: Choose one of the reflection prompts below and respond thoughtfully. If none fit your experience, you can write your own reflection prompt.
    • Your AI Chat PDF: Attach the saved AI output PDF as an appendix.
    • Optional: You can submit your tracker in a different format—like an audio note, short video, or visual map—if that helps you explain your thinking.
  4. Make sure your example output and reflection are specific to your experience

Example Reflection Prompts

To guide your reflection, choose one of these prompts:

  • What was the muddiest point for AI in this use? In other words, what did the AI not understand about your writing goals or audience?
  • How did using AI change your approach to revision?
  • Compare your voice to the AI’s. What did you keep or change to make it more "you"?
  • What surprised or challenged you in the AI’s output? Include quotation(s) from your Chat PDF to show me.
  • How would you explain your AI use to a future employer or colleague?
  • What ethical questions came up while using the tool?
  • Imagine designing a better AI for this task. What would it do differently?
  • How did this AI interaction shape what you learned from the assignment?
  • Would your writing have been better or worse without AI? Why?
  • If you and the AI were co-authors, how would you divide credit?
