What I Want You to Do

Create a draft of what your audience will see while you are making your pitch. This assignment focuses on composing a draft of the presentation slides you will show in your video. The slides should be keyed to the information in your script.

Why I Want You to Do It

Your pitch is three minutes or less. You can jumpstart the opening portions of your report by working on a clear, concise statement of your topic and how you will address the situation you are examining. Additionally, you will get practice in persuasive oral presentations, which are a highly useful skill in the workplace.

How You Do It

Work on Slides

  1. Review the resources in the “Where You Can Find Help” section above, particularly the design information in the LinkedIn Learning video.
  2. Determine the number of slides you will need for your presentation. You should include about one slide for each 30 seconds (for a total of about six slides maximum).
  3. Using your pitch script (created last week), determine the general outline of your slide show, keeping the Pitch Criteria in mind.
  4. Create your slides using your presentation slide show (e.g., PowerPoint or Google Slides).
  5. Add the text of your script to the Speaker Notes on your slides.

Improve Your Slides

The following activities will all contribute to your Pitch. You can complete all of these activities, or you can pick and choose—just be sure that you put in your best work and record what you do in your work log.

  1. Share your slide presentation (or as much of a presentation as you have) in the Pitch Slides Feedback Discussion in Canvas. Next week, use the feedback you receive to revise your work.
  2. If you missed the original chance for feedback, you can try the Second-Chance Pitch Slides Feedback Discussion in Canvas.
  3. Next week, move on to finalize and record the video of your presentation.
