What I Want You to Do

Create a draft of what you will say in your pitch. Your pitch presentation will tell your decisionmaker about what you’ve found on the website, why it should be updated, and the next actions to take. Your pitch will take the format of a video recording with audio that outlines the basic details you plan for your recommendation report and persuades listeners to support your plan.

The transcript for your audio can be used as part of the first draft of your report introduction and front matter. Graphics that you create can be used as illustrations and visuals in your report.

Why I Want You to Do It

Your pitch is three minutes or less. You can jumpstart the opening portions of your report by working on a clear, concise statement of your topic and how you will address the situation you are examining. Additionally, you will get practice in persuasive oral presentations, which are a highly useful skill in the workplace.

How You Do It

  1. Review the resources in the “Where You Can Find Help” section above.
  2. Gather ideas for what you will say by completing the following:
    1. Introducing Your Presentation
    2. Using Memorable Language
    3. Concluding Your Presentation
  3. Include each of the six points explained in “The Elevator Pitch” video as you work on the body of your script.
  4. Combine all your notes into a working draft of what you will say, and revise as appropriate.
  5. Share your current draft in the Pitch Script Feedback Discussion.
  6. If you missed the original chance for feedback, try the Second-Chance Pitch Script Feedback Discussion.
  7. Use the feedback you receive from your classmates to revise your work before you submit your Best Draft.
  8. Next week, move on to work on the slides that you will show as you read your script.
