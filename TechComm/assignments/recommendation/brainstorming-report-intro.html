What I Want You to Do

Begin gathering material for the introduction to your report by answering the questions included in the Markel and Selber’s Technical Communication on page 493. Once you answer the questions, you can begin connecting and revising the information into your introduction section.

Why I Want You to Do It

You are working on a long report that includes many sections. Working on the sections one-by-one is a good way to avoid being overwhelmed. I am asking you to begin with brainstorming facts for the introduction because it includes a lot of the basic information that you will expand upon later in the report.

How You Do It

  1. Download the Introduction document (a word processor file).
    Note: you must download the file. Everyone in the class uses the same file, so you cannot edit the file at the link.
  2. Open the file in a word processor and add your answers, following the instructions in the document.
    • Add more lines to the answer spaces as you need. You can write as much as you want to.
    • Write informally. You are gathering notes, not writing the final version for your report.
    • Skip a question if you are unsure of the answer or have not conducted enough research to know. Add a note to skipped questions that explains why you skipped it.
  3. Save the finished document once you add all your notes, and submit it here in Canvas.
