What You Will Learn and Practice

  • How to analyze your audience and purpose so that you can choose research that will persuade readers. [CLO 1]
  • How to find and evaluate original, ethical evidence that supports your position through primary and secondary research. [CLO 2 & 3]

What I Want You to Do

List all of the sources you have found so far for your report, telling me what kind of source each is and how you expect to use the source.

Why I Want You to Do It

As one of the culminating activities after three weeks of research, I want you to tell me about the sources that you have found so far. I want to check whether you have found adequate, reliable sources at this point so that you will know if you need to complete more research before you get any further in the process of writing your report.

Where You Can Find Help

  • From Markel and Selber, Chapter 6 in the “Conducting Secondary Research” section (pp. 126–135).
  • From the University Libraries, consult the Research Guide for your discipline to familiarize yourself with sources in your field. You can also talk to the librarian for your subject area.

How You Do It

NOTE:
This is a working list. You can add or remove resources as you continue work on your report. You do NOT need formal citations at this point, but you can submit them if you have them already.

  1. Compose an informal list of the secondary resources that you have found for your report. For each resource, tell me the following:
    • The author and title
    • The kind of source (for instance, a book, a website, a journal article)
    • How you think you will use the resource (for instance, as background, to support a recommendation)
  2. Add any notes or comments you have. This is an informal list, so you can include questions about the sources that you have found so far.
  3. Submit your work by uploading your file or sharing your Google Drive link.
