What I Want You to Do

Get started on the recommendation report by choosing the topic that you will focus on for the term. After you make your choice, tell me about it by answering a few questions.

Why I Want You to Do It

Your recommendation report relates to every other project in the class. Review the Sequence of Technical Writing Projects from the Short Guide to remind yourself how the projects connect. This activity is the first step of the sequence, “Choose Your Topic.”

We’re choosing topics now so that I can review your ideas and approve the topic. I want to make sure your topic will work right away, while it’s still easy to change or clarify your topic. If you start out with a good topic, everything else will be smoother.

How You Do It

  1. Read the Recommendation Report Topic Requirements, which explains the recommendation report assignment and the requirements that will influence your choice.
  2. Choose a topic for your recommendation report, keeping the Requirements in mind.
  3. Jot out a short explanation of your topic:
    • Aim for about the length of a Tweet, which is 280 characters. There’s no problem if you run over.
    • Make your topic specific. For example, don’t say “an intersection.” Tell me exactly what intersection in what town.
  4. Answer the questions below to tell me about your topic. Short responses are fine. I am not expecting long paragraphs (though if you want to write more, you always can).
