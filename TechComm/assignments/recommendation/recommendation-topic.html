What I Want You to Do

Get started on your recommendation report by choosing the topic that you will focus on for the term. After you make your choice, tell me about it by answering a few questions.

This activity counts as a Best Draft Submission in the Effort Expectations for Each Grade Level.

Why I Want You to Do It

Your recommendation report relates to every other project in the class. Review the Sequence of Technical Writing Projects from the Short Guide to remind yourself how the projects connect. This activity is the first step of the sequence, “Choose Your Target Website.”

You are proposing websites now so that I can review your ideas and approve the choice. I want to make sure your topic will work right away, while it’s still easy to change or clarify your choice. If you start out with a good website, everything else will be smoother.

How You Do It

  1. Read the Recommendation Report Topic Requirements, which explains the recommendation report assignment and the requirements that will influence your choice.
  2. Choose a topic for your recommendation report, keeping the Requirements in mind.
  3. Download the Recommendation Topic Proposal Form (Alternative Download).
  4. Open the form in your word processor, and answer the questions.
  5. Save your form once you finish, and submit your work by uploading the file.

How to Assess & Track Your Work

You track and grade your own work in this course. Be sure to complete the following tasks:

  • Track your work in your Weekly Work Log.
  • Watch for feedback comments from me—find feedback in annotations and comments on your submissions in Canvas.
  • Watch for this assignment to reappear in your to-do list if you need to revise or choose a new topic.

Work is always marked as either 1 for Complete or 0 for Incomplete:

  • I will mark this activity Complete in Canvas Grades, usually within 24–48 hours after the end of the grace period.
  • I will mark this activity Incomplete in Canvas Grades if your topic needs revision OR if you do not submit your topic by the end of the grace period.
