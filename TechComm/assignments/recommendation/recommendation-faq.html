As we finish the Recommendation Report, I want to provide you some tips that you should follow to ensure that your report is marked complete. If you do not follow these guidelines, you will need to revise. If you have to resubmit your Recommendation Report, be sure to submit by 11:59 PM on Monday, May 3. The assignment will close at that point.

  • Include all of the required sections listed on the Recommendation Report Criteria page. Do not combine or omit any of the required sections.
  • Make sure your sections are in the required order listed on the Recommendation Report Criteria page.
  • Address your letter of transmittal to a specific person, committee, or group.
  • Choose a specific title for your report. If your title is so general that it would work on anyone’s report, you need to revise it. Consider these examples:
    • Too General:
      • Recommendation Report
    • Specific Examples:
      • Revising The Way Our Organization Writes Project Proposals: A Recomendation Report
      • Building a Clear and Concise Software Project Proposal
  • Include a detailed and specific Table of Contents. It should show the full headings and sub-headings from the report. Check out the Table of Contents in the Sample Recommendation Report in the textbook for help.
  • Use specific headings and sub-headings in your report. Again, check the Table of Contents in the Sample Recommendation Report in the textbook to see appropriate headings.
  • Include both your primary and secondary research. Both are required.
  • Be sure that you place all quotations in quotation marks, and credit your sources in the body of your report. See the Documentation and Citation page for help.
  • Make sure all of your sources are listed in your References section. See the Documentation and Citation page for help.
  • Mention your Instructions Project in your report and include it in your Appendix.
  • Be sure you spell check your work, and double check that you have all names spelled correctly.
  • If mentioned, use the correct name of our university. For more information, see https://career.vt.edu/job-search/presenting_yourself/resumes/common-resume-mistakes.html
