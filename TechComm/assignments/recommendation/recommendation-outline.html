Goals

  • Analyze the rhetorical situation and determine the appropriate audience or users of written communication, considering the needs of global audiences and people with disabilities. [CLO 1]
  • Use conventions of various workplace genres, such as proposals, instructions, correspondence, reports, and slide decks, with understanding of how the genre conventions can be used as heuristics and as principles of arrangement. [CLO 4]
  • Apply principles of effective visual design for print and electronic presentation, including hierarchical, chronological, and spatial arrangements. [CLO 6]
  • Identify and apply the principles of effective style in the composing of usable, reader-centered written communications. [CLO 7]

The Task

What I Want You to Do

Review the information required in your Recommendation Report, and write an outline for your draft.

Why I Want You to Do It

Think of this outline as a working plan for your recommendation report. By creating an outline, you are sketching out the work that you need to do. Longer documents frequently require specific sections and information. I want you to start with an outline so that you have a plan from the beginning to include everything you need in the report.

How You Do It

  1. Review the specific list of information that your report should include (in the Scenario) and the list of required sections (in the Project Requirements) on the Recommendation Report: Assignment page.
  2. Examine the Sample Report, Figure 18.8 on pages 490–513 of Markel and Selber’s Technical Communication,  to review the document’s structure.
  3. Review Table 18.1: Elements of a Typical Report, on page 478 of Technical Communication for additional details on report structure.
  4. Create a rough outline of the required information for your Recommendation Report, relying on the information from the assignment criteria and the textbook.
    • Begin your outline with your report title, using the advice on the Effective Report Titles page to compose a strong title.
    • Indicate in your outline where you will cover the list of requested information (from the Recommendation Report Criteria page).

    • Use whatever outlining system you prefer for now, as long as it shows major sections and their subsections.
    • Start your outline by copying the list of required sections from the Recommendation Report Criteria page. Copying that information into your outline or first draft is the best way to ensure you have all the information you need.
  5. Submit your document:
    • Submit your project here by 11:59 PM on Friday, April 9.
    • If you need more time, submit your document by 11:59 PM on Monday, April 5.
