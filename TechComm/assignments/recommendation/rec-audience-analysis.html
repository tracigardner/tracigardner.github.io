What You Will Learn and Practice

  • Analyze your audience and purpose so that you can choose the best way to present information clearly and effectively. [CLO 1]

What I Want You to Do

Identify the decisionmaker who can act on your recommendation topic, gather information about that person or group, and complete an audience analysis form that identifies relevant details.

Why I Want You to Do It

To write a successful recommendation report (or any technical writing), you must be aware of who your reader is and how they will react to your document. In this activity, you gather information on the person who will decide on whether to follow your recommendation so that you can adjust your report to meet the decisionmaker’s expectations.

Where You Can Find Help

  • From Markel and Selber’s Technical Communication:
    • Chapter 5, “Analyzing Your Audience and Purpose”
    • Determining the Important Characteristics of Your Audience
  • From LinkedIn Learning, “Technical Writing & Audience Analysis Playlist”

How You Do It

  1. Look back over details on audience analysis in Chapter 5, “Analyzing Your Audience and Purpose,” in Technical Communication.
  2. Review the Determining the Important Characteristics of Your Audience page.
  3. Determine who is the decisionmaker for the recommendation you will make. You need to find the name of the person or group who can decide to implement your recommendation.
  4. Search online for biographical information on the decisionmaker for your report, and learn everything you can about the person.
  5. Download a copy of the Audience Analysis Profile Sheet. 
  6. Complete the Audience Profile Sheet in your word processor (adding as much space between the questions as you need).
  7. Save your form once you finish, and submit your work by uploading the file.
