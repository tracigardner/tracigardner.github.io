The Progress Report project focuses on providing your reader with an honest assessment of your work on the Recommendation Report and your plans to complete the Recommendation Report.

Length

Write or compose as much as you need to, but be sure to include all of the required information. Generally progress reports for this assignment are 2 to 3 pages long. There is no specific length requirement.

  • Is written in standard memo format and document design, with appropriate spacing, headings, and page numbers, including the following:
    • Complete memo headers and label.
    • Strong subject line.
    • Single space within section paragraphs.
    • Double space between sections and between paragraphs.
  • Includes the following sections:
    • An Introduction that
      • reminds the reader of the project and summarizes the work that has been accomplished so far in the first paragraph.
      • provides the purpose statement, which identifies the purpose of the document, in the second paragraph.
    • A Work Completed Section that
      • tells the reader what you’ve been able to accomplish so far.
      • uses specific, concrete details.
      • organizes the information with subheadings, using these guidelines:
        • Include a brief sentence or two of introduction between your main heading and the first subheading. This sentence can be an advance organizer.
        • Ensure that your subheadings are visibly different from your heading. Typically they use a slightly smaller font than the heading (but still bigger than the font for the paragraphs). They can also be a different color or size.
    • A Work Scheduled Section that
      • identifies work you still have left to do.
      • introduces the schedule with a simple explanation of the information to follow.
      • includes a Gantt Chart with an updated schedule for the group.
      • concludes with a brief summary of the work that still needs to be done.
    • A Conclusion that
      • wraps up the report.
      • provides contact information should your reader have additional questions.
  • Uses informative headings to help your readers navigate the report.
  • Uses graphics and design elements that will make the subject and the document itself more visually enticing to the reader and hold their attention. These can include:
    • bullet lists
    • headings and subheadings
    • color
    • font type, weight, and size
    • horizontal rules
    • boxes
