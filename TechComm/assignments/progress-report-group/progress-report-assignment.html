You will explain the work that the group has completed and the information that you have collected so far. You will include a Gantt Chart that updates your group schedule for the rest of the term.

Why I Want You to Do It

Progress reports, status reports, and completion reports are related informational documents that update readers on the work being done, warn about any challenges or delays, and forecast the tasks scheduled for the future. They are a common workplace document that provides managers, administrators, and other colleagues with details they need to ensure the work is continuing as expected and to provide any additional support or resources. The ethical challenge is to provide an honest assessment of the work-to-date, even if it means communicating bad news.

How You Do It

Follow the instructions in the four sections below to

  1. assess your project as a group.
  2. plan the work and schedule for your progress report.
  3. write the progress report collaboratively.
  4. review the progress report draft and revise/proofread as necessary.

Assessing Your Project as a Group

  1. Review the current draft for your Recommendation Report and compare what you have accomplished to the requirements of the assignment.
  2. As a group, determine what you have completed and what you still need to do for your Recommendation Report. Consider any challenges you have encountered and how you can address them. Compare your work to your schedule and determine whether any adjustments are necessary.
  3. Review the Progress Report Criteria and the example progress reports linked in the previous section to understand the requirements for the project.

Planning the Work and Schedule for Your Progress Report

  1. Use the information in the readings and Progress Report Criteria to divide the work for your group’s Progress Report, considering the two basic ways to work.
  2. Plan your group schedule—including draft due dates, meetings, and status updates—with one another. Here are some tips:
    • Create your schedule to finish by the due date. If your group gets down to the last minute and needs to use the grace period, that’s fine; but plan to be on time.
    • Pay attention to the way that sections of the report rely upon one another. For instance, you’ll need to understand all the work that has been completed before you create a schedule for the work that needs to be finished.
    • Consider the tips on how to Plan a strategy for scheduling from UNC’s Writing Center.
  3. Set up a Google Doc or shared Word doc for your group’s progress report. Share the document with everyone in your group, so that you can all edit. Also share the Doc with me (tengrrl@vt.edu). Find additional ways to share your document and related information in this UNC Writing Center Tip Sheet.
  4. Add headings in the document for each of the required sections (listed in the next section) of the progress report. Use the styles in the word processor your group has chosen to format all of the headings consistently, following the relevant documentation:
    • Make a title or heading in Google Docs
    • Add a heading in Microsoft Word

Collaborating to Write Your Progress Report

  1. Write a group progress report that proposes your topic and plan for the Recommendation Report. Your progress report should include the following information:
    • An Introduction that
      • reminds the reader of the project and summarizes the work that has been accomplished so far in the first paragraph.
      • provides the purpose statement, which identifies the purpose of the document, in the second paragraph.
    • A Work Completed Section that
      • tells the reader what you've been able to accomplish so far.
      • uses specific, concrete details.
      • organizes the information with subheadings, using these guidelines:
        • Include a brief sentence or two of introduction between your main heading and the first subheading. This sentence can be an advance organizer.
        • Ensure that your subheadings are visibly different from your heading. Typically that are a slightly smaller font than the heading (but still bigger than the font for the paragraphs). They can also be a different color or size.
    • A Work Scheduled Section that
      • identifies work you still have left to do.
      • introduces the schedule with a simple explanation of the information to follow.
      • includes a Gantt Chart with an updated schedule for the group.
      • concludes with a brief summary of the work that still needs to be done.
    • A Conclusion that
      • wraps up the report.
      • provides contact information should your reader have additional questions.

Checking and Revising Your Progress Report

  1. Assemble all parts of the proposal if you have worked separately so that you have one document.
  2. Review the document to ensure that the text is clear and complete, using these Revising, Editing, and Proofreading Tips
  3. Check and update your group’s progress report with the review tools in your word processor, using the following links if you need instructions:
    • Check spelling and grammar in Office
    • Check grammar, spelling, and more in Word for the web (for Microsoft 365)
    • Check your spelling & grammar in Google Docs
    • Check spelling in Pages on Mac
  4. Check and update your progress report for accessibility, using the following links if you need instructions:
    • Improve accessibility with the Accessibility Checker (for Office and for Microsoft 365)
    • Accessibility for Google Docs, Sheets, Slides, & Drawings
    • Create accessible documents, spreadsheets, or presentations with Pages, Numbers, or Keynote
  5. Success Tip
    You should be able to answer “True” to each question in the Self-Check before you submit your project. If you do not meet each of the criteria listed, your draft will be marked Incomplete and you’ll need to revise.
    Review your Progress Report by comparing it to the criteria in the Progress Report Self-Check.
  6. Choose one of these options based on your Self-Check:
    • If you answered “True” to every question, move on to the next step. You’re ready to submit your group’s work.
    • If you did not answer “True” to every question, follow this process:
      • Return to your draft as a group, and revise so that you can answer “True.”
      • Review your draft with the Progress Report Self-Check after you revise.
        • If you answered “True” to every question, move on to the next step. You’re ready to submit your work.
        • If you did not answer “True” to every question, continue revising until you can.
  7. Submit your group‘s progress report here once you are ready. See How do I submit an online assignment? if you need help with Canvas.
