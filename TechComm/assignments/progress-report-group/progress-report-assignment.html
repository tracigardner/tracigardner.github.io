What I Want You to Do

You and your group will

  • Evaluate your progress to date on the Recommendation Report or Fact Sheet Collection.
  • Summarize the work completed so far.
  • Outline your schedule for the remaining work in a Gantt Chart.

Why I Want You to Do It

Progress, status, and completion reports are common workplace documents that update readers on current work, note any challenges or delays, and outline upcoming tasks. They help managers and colleagues monitor progress and offer support if needed. The key ethical responsibility is to provide an honest assessment, even if it means communicating bad news.

The Scenario for the Project

You work as a Usability Consultant at a Maroon + Orange Digital, where I (Traci) work as a Founding Owner and the Director of Project Development. I’ve approved your proposal to recommend improvements to usability of an existing website, and your work is underway.

Your job now is to update me (Traci) on what your group has accomplished and whether your work is on track. You should also tell me if there are any challenges or concerns that your group has encountered. You’ll follow the instructions below in the “How to Do It” section.

How You Do It

Allowed Use of AI

You can use Copilot or another Generative AI tool of your choice on this project in any of the ways listed under the question “What can I use AI for in this course?” in the Course Policies Manual.

Be sure to save the responses AI gives you. You need the information for your Submission Wrapper.

Prohibited Use of AI

You cannot ask AI to write or revise your drafts or final submissions for you. Doing so directly violates the Cheating and Plagiarism sections of the Virginia Tech Honor Code.

Use the instructions in the three sections below to do the following:

  1. Assess your project as a group.
  2. Plan the work and schedule for your progress report.
  3. Write and revise the progress report collaboratively.

Section 1: Assessing Your Project as a Group

  1. Review the current draft for your Recommendation Report or your Fact Sheet Collection and compare what you have accomplished to the rubric for the assignment.
  2. As a group, do the following:
    • Determine what you have completed and what you still need to do for your Recommendation Report or your Fact Sheet Collection.
    • Consider any challenges you have encountered and how you can address them.

Section 2: Planning the Work and Schedule for Your Progress Report

  1. Use the information in the assignment to divide the work for your group’s Progress Report, considering the two basic ways to work.
  2. Update your group schedule—including draft due dates, meetings, and status updates—with one another. Here are some tips:
    • Create your schedule to finish by the due date. If your group gets down to the last minute and needs to use the grace period, that’s fine; but plan to be on time.
    • Pay attention to the way that sections of the report rely upon one another. For instance, you’ll need to understand all the work that has been completed before you create a schedule for the work that needs to be finished.
    • Consider the tips on how to Plan a strategy for scheduling from UNC’s Writing Center.
  3. Set up a Google Doc or shared Word doc for your group’s progress report. Share the document with everyone in your group, so that you can all edit. Also share the Doc with me (tengrrl@vt.edu). Find additional ways to share your document and related information in this UNC Writing Center Tip Sheet.
  4. Add headings in the document for each of the required sections (listed in the next section) of the progress report. Use the styles in the word processor your group has chosen to format all of the headings consistently, following the relevant documentation:
    • Make a title or heading in Google Docs
    • Add a heading in Microsoft Word

Section 3: Collaborating to Write & Revise Your Progress Report

  1. Write a group progress report, following the instructions in the Annotated Progress Report Template. Note that the template is the same whether you are working on a Recommendation Report or a Fact Sheet Collection. Include these required sections:
    • Section 1: Memo Headers
    • Section 2: Introduction
    • Section 3: Work Completed
    • Section 4: Work Scheduled
    • Section 5: Conclusion
    You can also use the Progress Report Template (Format Only) as you work to see an example of the layout and formatting.
  2. Assemble all parts of the progress report if you have worked separately so that you have one document.
  3. Review the document to ensure that the text is clear and complete, using these Revising, Editing, and Proofreading Tips
  4. Check and update your group’s progress report with the review tools in your word processor, using the following links if you need instructions:
    • Check spelling and grammar in Office
    • Check grammar, spelling, and more in Word for the web (for Microsoft 365)
    • Check your spelling & grammar in Google Docs
    • Check spelling in Pages on Mac
  5. Check and update your progress report for accessibility, using the following links if you need instructions:
    • Improve accessibility with the Accessibility Checker (for Office and for Microsoft 365)
    • Accessibility for Google Docs, Sheets, Slides, & Drawings
    • Create accessible documents, spreadsheets, or presentations with Pages, Numbers, or Keynote
  6. Success Tip
    You should be able to answer “True” to each question in the Self-Check before you submit your project. If you do not meet each of the criteria listed, your draft will be marked Incomplete and you’ll need to revise.
    Complete the Progress Report Self-Check, and decide with your group whether to submit the assignment:
    • If you answered “True” to every question, move on to the next step (#14). You’re ready to submit your work.
    • If you did not answer “True” to every question, follow this process:
      • Return to your draft and revise it to meet all of the criteria.
      • Review your draft with the Progress Report Self-Check after you revise.
        • If you answered “True” to every question, move on to the next step (#8). You’re ready to submit your work.
        • If you did not answer “True” to every question OR AI said you did not meet all the criteria, continue revising until you can.
  7. Submit your group‘s progress report here once your group is ready. Only ONE member of the group needs to submit the progress report. See How do I submit an online assignment? if you need help with Canvas.
