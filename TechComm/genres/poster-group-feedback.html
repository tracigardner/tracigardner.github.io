Group Feedback on Ethics Poster Drafts

I have marked all of your Ethics Poster rough drafts complete (or incomplete if they are missing or didn’t meet the requirements of the assignment). In this Announcement, I’ll comment on how you all did and what you can do to improve your projects.

Why Group Feedback?

As you may remember from the Course Manual, I use antiracist assessment strategies, adapted from Asao Inoue’s work on contract grading, labor-based grading, and anti-racist assessment strategies.

The usual grading process of comparing student work to an ideal text means your success in a course depends upon perfect grammar and style. But whose grammar and style count? And who decides? That’s the problem. The ideal is systemically racist (and classist as well!) because it ignores the many ways that people communicate.

In what we’ll call “real life,” no one speaks or writes according to a single “correct” grammar and style. In fact, there is no one “correct” way of communicating. There are many ways of communicating, and as long as your audience understands the message, those ways are “correct.” To be honest, I don’t like to talk about what is correct or incorrect. Your writing is either going to be effective or ineffective at accomplishing your goals.

Too often, writers look at comments on their draft as a to-do list. They assume that a teacher marks out everything wrong with a draft [1]. In response, all they have to do is address all those little marks and they will “perfect” their submissions.

You, as a writer, have to determine the best strategies to use to communicate effectively. You need to look at advice and expectations, choose those that will help your writing, and apply them to your work.

General Feedback

Everyone receives one of the two comments listed in the bulleted list below. These comments simply state policies related to the assignment. There’s no hidden meaning. I just want you to be aware of these policies:

  • The grade on your draft indicates only that you turned in a draft. It does not reflect how well you have met the requirements of the assignment.
  • This document was turned in after the deadline for feedback, so the grade only indicates completion of the activity. It is not a statement of how well the draft meets the requirements of the assignment.
