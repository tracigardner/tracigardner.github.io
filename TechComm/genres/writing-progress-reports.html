Progress reports are a constant part of workplace communication. Frequently you will update coworkers and managers verbally, in meetings or quick email messages. More formal written progress reports are shared with upper management and clients.

The audience for each kind of progress report will help you determine the kinds of details to include. The most important guideline is to be honest about any setbacks. No one wants to be surprised by a project that has fallen behind. Further by sharing potential challenges, you can then work with your readers to find the best solution to whatever has come up.
