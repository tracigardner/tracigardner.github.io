What I Want You to Do

Use the guidelines for use of images in this activity to evaluate (and if necessary, revise) the use of images in your project.

Why I Want You to Do It

Images can add a great deal to technical writing, but they are most effective when they

  • follow guidelines that ensure they fit the topic,
  • are well-edited, and
  • are integrated smoothly into the project.

This activity asks you to look at every image in your project, examining each one to see if it follows a specific guidelines. By working through the project one guideline at a time, you have the chance to ensure every image contributes positively to your project.

How You Do It

  1. Open the Use of Images Self-Review page (or visit the slides in Google Docs) to review your project.

  2. Open your project in the appropriate software. The software depends upon the project format you are working with. For example, if it’s a text document, you would use a word processor. If it’s a slide presentation, you’d choose the slideshow program you used.
  3. Move through the slides, and check your project:
    1. STOP on each slide.
    2. Read the advice on the slide.
    3. Apply the advice to your draft, if necessary.
    4. Move to the next slide.
  4. Repeat Step 3 until you apply all the slides to your draft.
