  • Review how to improve projects in the course by watching video feedback and applying the ideas to your own drafts.

What I Want You to Do

Watch feedback videos that provide quick advice for improving drafts that have been written for the course. After watching the videos, use the advice to revise your own drafts.

Why I Want You to Do It

Improving your writing can be difficult without examples of how to revise your work. These feedback videos will provide those examples. Applying this advice to your own work will help you take your projects to the next level.

How It Works

  1. I will choose a particular assignment or a specific writing strategy that I will provide feedback on.
  2. I will hide the names of the people who have submitted the files, following the Anonymous Use of Student Text policy (from the Course Manual and repeated below).
  3. I will then record videos that give fast feedback on the draft, with closed captions.
  4. I will upload the videos to the class files and post an Announcement to tell you when they are available.

Anonymous Use of Student Texts Policy

The work that you and your classmates create in this course may be used anonymously to demonstrate revision strategies.

In the case where student texts are used to show a weakness or error, the presentation will also include examples of how to revise and strengthen the text. As the author of such a text, you will gain direct examples of how to change your draft.

When student work is used in this course, it will be used according to these guidelines:

  • Student work is always used anonymously.
  • Anonymous texts are typically analyzed in one of two ways:
    • To demonstrate a weakness and then show how to revise.
    • To model a strength and show how to use similar strategies.
  • Anonymous work will be changed as necessary to remove any personal or potentially identifying information.
  • Several examples of student work may be combined to create one composite example when appropriate.
