MEMORANDUM

Document Title

  • Include “Memo” or “Memorandum” as the document title.
  • Do not include any other title.
  • Do not include the assignment name as the document title.
To: All Office Staff
From: Guy Warwick, HR Manager
Date: March 5, 2026
Subject: Required Procedures Before Leaving for Spring Break

Memo Headers

  • Every memo begins with memo headers: To, From, Date, and Subject.
  • Follow each header with the pertinent information.
  • Align the memo headers vertically.
  • Use the same size and style for all the memo headers.
  • Double space (skip 1 blank line) between headers.

This memo outlines the required procedures to complete before leaving for spring break to ensure office security, continuity of service, and a smooth return to work.

Important Dates

  • Last day of regular operations: Friday, March 7
  • Office closed: March 7–15
  • Office reopens: Monday, March 16 at 8:30 a.m.

Complete all closing procedures before you leave on your final workday.

Client and Communication Responsibilities

  • Respond to all pending emails and voicemail messages.
  • Set an out-of-office message on email and voicemail that includes:
    • Dates of absence
    • Expected return date
    • Alternative contact person for urgent matters
  • Forward time-sensitive requests to your supervisor or designated colleague.
  • Update shared project trackers with current task status.

Clear communication prevents delays and reduces confusion while the office is closed.

Memo Body

  • Do not indent paragraphs
  • Use hierarchical headings, in bold. Your docment title should be the largest and then work down from there.
  • Begin all main sections with a First-level Heading (using the same size and style).
  • Include your section content in paragraphs under the heading.
  • Add another main section with a First-level Heading (using the same size and style).
  • Add footnotes with bibliographic details on the key information you plan to use in your Recommendation Report.
  • Find help on writing about the methods you propose using in Tech Writing.

Office and Equipment Checklist

  • Shut down your computer and monitor.
  • Turn off printers, space heaters, coffee makers, and other personal appliances.
  • Remove perishable food from refrigerators.
  • Clean and clear your workspace.
  • Ensure windows are closed and blinds adjusted.

Supervisors will complete a final walkthrough at 4:30 p.m. on March 7.

Project Audience Section

  • Add another main section with a First-level Heading (using the same size and style).
  • Find help on the audience section in Tech Writing.
  • Find help for analyzing your audience in Technical Writing Essentials and adapting your writing for your audience in Technical Writing.

Questions

If you have questions about these procedures, please contact me before March 7.

Project Personnel Section

  • Add another main section with a First-level Heading (using the same size and style).
  • Return to your group’s Professional Bios in the Introduce Yourself Discussion to get started. Focus on the professional details in the bios.

Thank you for your attention to these details. Completing these steps helps ensure a safe and organized break for everyone.

Conclusion Section

  • Add another main section with a First-level Heading (using the same size and style).
  • Keep it simple.
  • Do not sign your name at the end or add a signature block.
  • Find help on writing about the Conclusion in Tech Writing.
