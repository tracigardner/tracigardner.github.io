The example in the table below shows the general layout and formatting instructions for memos. Note these important details:

  • The layout in the table includes more vertical space between the sections to allow for the full explanation.
  • If your memo is more than one page long, add page numbers (either at the top of each page or the bottom of each page). If you choose the top of each page, do not number the first page.

MEMORANDUM

Document Title

  • Include “Memo” or “Memorandum” as the document title.
  • Do not include any other title.
  • Do not include the assignment name as the document title.
To: All Office Staff
From: Guy Warwick, HR Manager
Date: March 2, 2026
Subject: Required Procedures Before Leaving for Spring Break

Memo Headers

  • Every memo begins with memo headers: To, From, Date, and Subject.
  • Follow each header with the pertinent information.
  • Align the memo headers vertically.
  • Use the same size and style for all the memo headers.
  • Double space (skip 1 blank line) between headers.

Please complete the required procedures below before leaving for spring break to ensure office security, continuity of service, and a smooth return to work.

Important Dates

  • Last day of regular operations: Friday, March 6.
  • Office closed: March 7–15.
  • Office reopens: Monday, March 16 at 8:30 a.m.

Complete all these closing procedures before you leave on your final workday.

Client and Communication Responsibilities

  • Respond to all pending emails and voicemail messages.
  • Set an out-of-office message on email and voicemail that includes:
    • Dates of absence.
    • Expected return date.
    • Alternative contact person for urgent matters.
  • Update shared project trackers with current task status.

Office and Equipment Checklist

  • Shut down your computer, printer, and monitor.
  • Turn off space heaters, coffee makers, and other personal appliances.
  • Ensure windows are closed and blinds adjusted.

Managers will complete a final walkthrough at 4:30 p.m. on March 6.

Memo Body

  • Do not indent paragraphs
  • Single space your content. Double space between paragraphs.
  • Use hierarchical headings, in bold. Your document title should be the largest and then work down from there.
  • Include your section content in paragraphs under the heading.
  • Use lists, subheadings, and other document design elements to increase readability.

Thanks for helping us wrap things up before spring break. If you have questions about these procedures, please contact your manager or me at gwarwick@business.com before March 6.

Memo Conclusion

  • No heading needed.
  • Include contact information.
  • Do not sign your name at the end or add a signature block.
