In Your Messages, Who Wins?

When you write a salutation in a letter or email (like “Dear Champ”), you must follow your greeting with one punctuation mark—and the one you choose matters! The matchup is simple:

Colon = Formal correspondence Comma = Informal correspondence

Think about your audience and the situation:

  • If the context is official, evaluative, or you’re addressing a role or office, the colon takes the championship.
  • If the tone is friendly or familiar, the comma gets the nod.

Highlight Reel: How It’s Done

Formal Messages (Use a colon)

  • Dear Hiring Committee: (Job application)
  • Dear Professor Nguyen: (Academic inquiry)
  • Dear Dr. Patel: (Medical or research-related correspondence)
  • Dear Members of the Board: (Business or organizational matters)
  • Dear Admissions Office: (College or university application)
 

Informal Messages (Use a comma)

  • Hi, Sam, (Text or email to a friend)
  • Hello, everyone, (Group message to classmates)
  • Hey, Coach, (Casual note or email)
  • Dear Mom, (Personal letter)
  • Hi, team, (Informal workplace message)

Note: In these informal examples, notice the comma after Hi, Hello, and Hey—and the comma after the name/group, too.
