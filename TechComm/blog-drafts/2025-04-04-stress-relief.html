Quick Ways to Reset: Stress Relief for College Life

College life comes with a constant balancing act—deadlines, group work, jobs, and everything else you’re juggling. If you’re feeling overloaded, you’re not alone—and you’re not stuck. Virginia Tech offers plenty of simple, low-stress ways to get back on track, no appointment required.

1. Try a Fast-Acting Stress Strategy

If you’ve got five minutes between classes or before a team meeting, you can still reset. The UCC’s Relieve Stress Now page has quick strategies that actually work—from breathing exercises to muscle relaxation techniques. You don’t need gear, apps, or a quiet room. Just pick one and try it out.

2. Hang Out with Therapy Dogs

Sometimes, the best relief is a tail wag and a nuzzle. The Virginia Tech Therapy Dogs program brings trained dogs (and their friendly handlers) to campus spaces for drop-in comfort. If you haven’t met them yet, it’s worth stopping by—you’ll walk away smiling.

3. Meet Growley II, the Corps of Cadets' Comfort Dog

Growley II is not just a beloved member of the Corps—he’s also an unofficial ambassador of calm. You’ll often see him around campus events and outreach efforts. Keep an eye out: getting a high-five from Growley might be the best moment of your week.

4. Tap into Campus Well-Being Resources

For deeper support or longer-term wellness strategies, the Virginia Tech Well-Being Hub has what you need—from mental health tools to counseling options to workshops on managing stress and building resilience. Everything is tailored to student life, and many services are free.

Reminder: Stress is normal. What matters is building small, repeatable ways to handle it. Use the resources that work for you—and share them with friends who might need them too.
