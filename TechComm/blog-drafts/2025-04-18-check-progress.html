How to Check Your Progress in This Course

Canvas calculates your grades based only on the work you’ve submitted so far. That means it ignores anything not yet turned in—even if it’s overdue. As a result, the summary at the bottom of the Grades page might show an overly optimistic estimate of your course grade.

Why I’m Marking Remaining Work as Incomplete

To give you a more realistic view of your current standing, I will mark all remaining work in the course as Incomplete in Canvas during the weekend.  This doesn’t mean you’ve failed those assignments—it just allows Canvas to factor in what’s still missing when it calculates your overall progress.

This adjustment makes the category percentages and summary at the bottom of your Grades page more accurate, so you can see your estimated course grade based on everything expected of you—not just what you’ve already submitted.

Releasing Your Current Grade Estimate

I will calculate your current course grade by the middle of next week. I plan to add feedback on the work that meets the Target Due Date of April 18 before calculating the estimates.

This estimate will tell you where you stand as of the moment it is released. That estimate is locked in. If you stop working in the course, it will be your course grade.

How to Check Your Current Grade

  1. Go to the Grades page in Canvas.
  2. Scroll to the bottom to see a breakdown like this:
Category Your Score
Major Projects 80% (4.00/5.00)
Check-In Surveys 85% (12.00/14.00)
Weekly Activities 75% (27.00/36.00)
  1. Compare your percentages to the Effort Expectations Contract:
  • A: 5 Major Projects, 95% Surveys, 95% Activities
  • B: 4 Major Projects, 85% Surveys, 85% Activities
  • C: 3 Major Projects, 75% Surveys, 75% Activities
  • D: 2 Major Projects, 65% Surveys, 65% Activities
  • F: Less than 2 Projects or below 65% in other categories
  1. Figure out your estimated course grade based on where your percentages line up. Remember:
  • Major Projects carry the most weight.
  • You must earn a Complete on at least 2 Major Projects to pass the course.
  • Grades are not averaged; they are based on meeting the specific expectations in each category.
