So if I just turn everything in, will I earn a Complete?
That’s not quite how it works. You have to finish all the work and submit it. You also have to make sure that the work you do meets the requirements for the task so that you earn a Complete (or a 1 in Canvas). Here are some example scenarios to help you understand: 
  • If you answer all the questions in a Check-In Survey, you'll have a Complete.

  • If you answer all the questions for a Try-It but don't only partially answer the questions, you will end up with an Incomplete. The process is that Canvas will automatically give you a Complete; however, I will check your work and change the grade back to Incomplete because you didn't meet the requirements. As long as there is time left in the grace period for the Try-It, you can revise it.

  • If you submit a full draft (like the Food Label Analysis), I will give you feedback on the project with the rubric attached to the assignment (which matches the criteria page for the project).
    • If you have met all of the criteria, I will mark your work Complete.
    • If you have not met all the criteria, I will mark your work Incomplete.
What if my work doesn’t earn a Complete?
As long as there is time left in the grace period, you can revise. It's possible to need to revise more than once, though it doesn’t happen often.

How will I know how I’m doing? I’m used to checking my course grade.
You can always tell how you are doing by looking at the details on the Grades page in Canvas. Follow the instructions on page 11 of the Short Guide to understand what you see.
How can I remain motivated without grades?
Focus on what you are learning and what you are accomplishing. Are you getting all the work in? Are you earning Completes? Are you keeping in touch with your Accountability Group? If so, you’re doing well.
Is there time for revision before the grace period ends?
Generally, yes, you will be able to revise. The one exception is at the end of the open Grace Period, at 11:59 PM on Friday, December 2, 2022. That is the very last moment to turn in the work of the course. You cannot revise any further after that point. That’s why waiting until the end of the grace period to turn in your work is a bad idea.
