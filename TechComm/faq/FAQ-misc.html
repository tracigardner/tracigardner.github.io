How can I fight procrastination? How can I avoid overestimating my ability to get to it later?
Try some of the strategies in the LinkedIn Learning audio course The Procrastination Cure (Blinkist Summary) (22M) or the One-minute habit to stop procrastinating (LinkedIn Learning, 2m 40s). You can also find suggestions on the Procrastination page from the Writing Center at UNC at Chapel Hill.
What can I do to stay ahead?
Set a schedule for yourself and then keep to it. The Time Management Strategies page from the Cook Counseling Center has advice on how to create a schedule and adjust it as needed.

Will we ever meet in person?
No, the class will not meet in person. It is a 100% online asynchronous course.
Can we do the work in a different order?
You can but be aware that some activities in later modules rely on information from earlier modules. Additionally, know that I review work in the order on the schedule.
Can we get feedback on personal research and work for other classes?
No, with over 80 students in the course, the documents that you write for this class take up all my time. You can make an appointment at the Writing Center for help on your other projects.
How often will you be in contact about work and submissions?
In addition to the reminders in the Module Instructions, I usually send out reminders before the grace period ends to anyone who has not yet submitted their work. Ideally, however, you should set up your own reminders. See the next question for details.
Will there be reminders for all the due dates?
You can set up your own reminders. First add your Canvas Calendar to your Google Calendar, and then add notifications on your Google Calendar to remind yourself of Suggested Due Dates and the end of Grace Periods. Since some students want frequent reminders and some want none at all, setting up your own ensures that everyone gets what they want. Further, setting up your own reminders makes you responsible for your time and workflow (skills that are critical in the workplace).
