Why food?
Focusing on food gives us a topic that everyone can relate to, lets us examine a range of ethical issues that everyone will understand, and allows us to talk about technical writing that takes place both in the workplace and in other locations. Read more on Food and Technical Writing.
Is every assignment food-related?
Yes, you’ll write about food in some way in every assignment.
Must the work all focus on the same food?
No, you can talk about some of the same food or issues, but you don’t have to.
Can the food be something personal?
Absolutely. Writing about something you care about is a great idea.
 
Will anything be related to programming or development?
Yes and no. First the no: we have quite a few majors represented in this course. Many people don’t have any connection to programming or development. That’s why we are talking about food, something everyone can relate to.

Now the yes: You can think of a food-related situation that relates to programming or development for your recommendation report.
I’d rather talk about accessibility on campus than food. What can I do?
Great. Just talk about accessibility in a way that intersects with food. For instance, you might talk about physical accessibility in dining halls for students or for people working there. Think about ways that you can connect accessibility to food, and you should be able to come up with something.
