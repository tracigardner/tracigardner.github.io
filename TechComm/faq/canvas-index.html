Are there examples for assignments?

Usually. Most of the samples are in the Document Samples page in Canvas. Some examples are also included in the assignments themselves.

Do we need to cite sources? What format should we use?

If you borrow words, phrases, images, audio, video or ideas, then yes, you ALWAYS need to cite your sources—and that is in any class, not just technical writing. Otherwise, you are plagiarizing.

  • For your major projects, use the style that is appropriate for your career field. For instance, electrical engineers use IEEE, and biologists often use CSE.
  • For homework, you can use the format that makes sense. For a borrowed image, for instance, you can include a credit line in the image caption or at the end of the document. As long as I can tell what you borrowed and where it came from, you’re fine.

If you’re not sure what style your field uses, consult the Citation and style manuals guide from the library and the Documentation and Citations page for help.

What do I do if I miss an assignment?

If the grace period for the assignment is still open, go ahead and complete and then submit the assignment. As long as you submit the assignment before the grace period ends, there is no problem.

If the grace period has passed, it is too late to submit the assignment.

Are any of the assignments group projects?

There are no group writing assignments. Because it is so challenging to find common times for people to work in an asynchronous course, I do not include them.

There are Discussions that require group interaction; however, the grades are all individual.

What happens if I miss a Target Due Date?

Most weeks, nothing significant will happen. You still have the grace period so that you can turn in your work later.

Technically however, Canvas will grade the work as a Zero and label the activity as Late.

Do I need to tell you if I’m using the grace period?

Nope. If you don’t turn your work in, I assume you’re using the grace period.

Is work submitted during the grace period graded the same way as “on time” work?

Yes, it is graded as if it was turned in by the Target Due Date. There is no penalty for using the grace period.

How can I avoid falling behind?

Schedule this class as if it were an on-campus class that meets in a regular classroom. Choose a time slot that works for you (like 11 AM MWF), put it in your calendar so that you get reminders, and do work during that time period. Don’t leave it for “when you have time.” That typically causes the most trouble if you get busy.

All of the assignments are due on the same day every week, so I’m struggling to find time.

Staggered deadlines—quizzes on Tuesday, discussions on Wednesday, projects on Thursday—rarely fit everyone’s schedules. Instead, each week has one Target Due Date at the end of the week. The idea is to give you the freedom to manage your own time each week. Plan your own milestones, and complete all work by that Target Due Date. If you need more time any week, use the Grace Period.

I'm still a little confused about the grace period deadline.

Basically, the grace period is a automatic extension on all course assignments (except the final exam). You don’t have to ask in advance or let me know you’ve decided to use it.

The Target Due Date for each week’s work is usually Friday. The grace period gives you THREE extra class days to turn your work in. It ends on the Wednesday following the Target Due Date.

The only exception is the week of Fall break, when the Target Due Date is Thursday (since there are no classes on Friday). In that case, you get a bonus day, with the grace period still ending on the following Wednesday.

 

Feedback & Assessment

Back to the Top

How quick assignments will normally be graded after submitting?

For major projects, allow a minimum of 3–4 days after the Target Due Date for me to add feedback. For longer projects, it may take a week.

For homework that is not marked automatically by Canvas, it depends upon the activity. I do not mark Discussions until at least the Target Due Date passes and often I wait until the Grace Period closes since the posts require interaction. It’s better to wait until everyone has turned in their work.

Will there be clear rubrics that detail what is expected for grading?

I provide rubrics for the Major Projects, but not the homework and other activities. Think of the homework that is not graded automatically like the kind of work your teacher marks with a checkmark. I look at your work, but I do not provide extensive comments unless something wows me or something is amiss. Either way, as long as you’ve put in the effort, there’s nothing to worry about.
