Are there examples for assignments?

Usually. Most of the samples are in the Document Samples page in Canvas. Some examples are also included in the assignments themselves.

Do we need to cite sources? What format should we use?

If you borrow words, phrases, images, audio, video or ideas, then yes, you ALWAYS need to cite your sources—and that is in any class, not just technical writing. Otherwise, you are plagiarizing.

  • For your major projects, use the style that is appropriate for your career field. For instance, electrical engineers use IEEE, and biologists often use CSE.
  • For homework, you can use the format that makes sense. For a borrowed image, for instance, you can include a credit line in the image caption or at the end of the document. As long as I can tell what you borrowed and where it came from, you’re fine.

If you’re not sure what style your field uses, consult the Citation and style manuals guide from the library and the Documentation and Citations page for help.

What do I do if I miss an assignment?

If the grace period for the assignment is still open, go ahead and complete and then submit the assignment. As long as you submit the assignment before the grace period ends, there is no problem.

If the grace period has passed, it is too late to submit the assignment.

Are any of the assignments group projects?

There are no group writing assignments. Because it is so challenging to find common times for people to work in an asynchronous course, I do not include them.

There are Discussions that require group interaction; however, the grades are all individual.

Due Dates & the Grace Period

Back to the Top

What happens if I miss a Target Due Date?

Most weeks, nothing significant will happen. You still have the grace period so that you can turn in your work later.

Technically however, Canvas will grade the work as a Zero and label the activity as Late.

Do I need to tell you if I’m using the grace period?

Nope. If you don’t turn your work in, I assume you’re using the grace period.

Is work submitted during the grace period graded the same way as “on time” work?

Yes, it is graded as if it was turned in by the Target Due Date. There is no penalty for using the grace period.

Is there a list of deadlines?

There are several places to check for Target Due Dates:

  • In the Module for each week.
  • On the bottom of the Syllabus page.
  • In the Canvas Calendar. Visit “How do I use the Calendar?’ for help.
Why is everything due on the same day each week?

You have the freedom to manage your own time each week:

  • Consider your obligations for the week (e.g., your job, other classes, extra-curricular activities, and so on).
  • Plan your own schedule, working around your obligations.
  • Complete your work according to your plan and these options:
    • Turn it in early if you want.
    • Submit it by the Target Due Date.
    • Finish it by the end of the Grace Period if you need more time. There is never a penalty for using the Grace Period.

I'm still a little confused about the grace period deadline.

Basically, the grace period is a automatic extension on all course assignments (except the final exam). You don’t have to ask in advance or let me know you’ve decided to use it.

The Target Due Date for each week’s work is usually Friday. The grace period gives you THREE extra class days to turn your work in. It ends on the Wednesday following the Target Due Date.

The only exception is the week of Fall break, when the Target Due Date is Thursday (since there are no classes on Friday). In that case, you get a bonus day, with the grace period still ending on the following Wednesday.

How can I fight procrastination?

Try some of the strategies in the LinkedIn Learning audio course The Procrastination Cure (Blinkist Summary) (22M) or the One-minute habit to stop procrastinating (LinkedIn Learning, 2m 40s).

You can also find suggestions on the Procrastination page from the Writing Center at UNC at Chapel Hill.

How can I avoid falling behind?

Schedule this class as if it were an on-campus class that meets in a regular classroom. Choose a time slot that works for you (like 11 AM MWF), put it in your calendar so that you get reminders, and do work during that time period. Don’t leave it for “when you have time.” That typically causes the most trouble if you get busy.

How can I remember all the assignments in this course?

I have two tips for you:

  1. Rely on the Module for each week. Everything you need to submit each week is listed on the module page, and they’re also listed on the Module Introduction page.

  2. Use the To Do list in the right sidebar in Canvas to see a list of tasks that are due and upcoming. Check the Canvas documentation to learn more about the To Do list.

Artificial Intelligence Use

Back to the Top

It’s hard for me to realize how to use AI and what are the limitations. Please give some examples of assignments for better understanding.

The first place to look for information on AI is in the Short Guide (pages 9–10), where I outline all the ways you can use Copilot or another tool.

Here are some example activities that incorporate AI:

  • Subject Selection Message Self-Check: Look for the link to prompts and instructions.
  • Choosing Your Project Subject: Ut Prosim in Your Hometown?: Look at the “Finding Your Topic Using AI” section.
I found it a little challenging to have AI generate a response that didn’t provide any suggestions even after explicitly telling it not to.

AI rarely does exactly what we want it to do. Further, it takes some practice writing prompts to get to a point where you get consistent answers. You’re not responsible for AI misbehaving. As long as you have the prompt that you used and the reply that AI gave you, you’re covered.

Feedback & Assessment

Back to the Top

How quickly are assignments graded?

For major projects, allow a minimum of 3–4 days after the Target Due Date for me to add feedback. For longer projects, it may take a week.

For homework that is not marked automatically by Canvas, it depends upon the activity. I do not mark Discussions until at least the Target Due Date passes and often I wait until the Grace Period closes since the posts require interaction. It’s more efficient to wait until everyone has turned in their work.

Are there rubrics for all the assignments?

There are rubrics for the Major Projects and for some of the homework (such as the Discussions). Think of the homework that is not graded automatically like the kind of work your teacher marks with a checkmark. I look at your work, but I do not provide extensive comments unless something wows me, amuses me, or has an error.

Why don’t all the links open in a new tab?

Because opening in new tabs is not accessible according to the ADA Title II Digital Accessibility guidelines that all Virginia Tech sites have to meet now.

There are two exceptions:

  • Resources on the site that use iframes open in an new tab because they do not work properly otherwise.
  • Canvas also opens external links (i.e., links to other sites) in a new tab.
