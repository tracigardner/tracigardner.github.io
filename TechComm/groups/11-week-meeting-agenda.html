1. Set-up Your Meeting Minutes.
    Choose a format for your meeting minutes and a person to record the minutes, using the information on the Meeting Minutes Format page.
  2. Add Items to Your Agenda if Desired.
    You might set a meeting for the week of April 14 so that you’re ready to collaborate next week.
  3. Ask Everyone for a Status Update.
    Have each group member share details on the work they’ve completed and what they still need to do. Make sure everyone discusses the status of their research. Take time to offer support to one another and address any issues that come up.
  4. Choose your Final Major Project
    Your group can choose to write either the Recommendation Report or the Fact Sheet Collection for the final major project. See the Important: Course Changes Announcement for details. Once your group has made a choice, the group leader (or their designee) should fill out and submit the Final Major Project Choice Quiz.
  5. Plan Your Progress Report.
    1. Set up a shared word processor document for your Progress Report.
    2. Follow the instructions in the Progress Report Assignment to plan your document, dividing the work among group members.
    3. Schedule a day and time when group members should have submitted their contributions to the Progress Report.
    4. Choose a group member to review the draft for consistency, spelling, and grammar after the scheduled date and then to submit the finished Progress Report.
  6. Finish Your Meeting.
    Once you have met and finished all the work on this agenda, finalize and edit your Meeting Minutes and adjourn your meeting. The Meeting Minutes will be posted in an Announcement.
