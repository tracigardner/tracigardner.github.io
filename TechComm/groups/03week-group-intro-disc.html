What You Need to Do

For this first Discussion with your Project Group, introduce yourself and get to know one another by writing a short professional biography (bio). You’ll be collaborating for the rest of the term so knowing one another is important!

How This Activity Connects to the Course

You will collaborate on three Major Projects as well as many Weekly Activities in this course. Connecting with your group is crucial to doing well on these projects.

Instructions

Consult the documentation links in the Teamwork & Collaboration Guide or contact 4Help if you need additional support for the tool.

  1. Find the name of your group on the Spring 2025 Project Groups PDF (not available until Week 3).
  2. Go to the Groups page in Canvas for your Project Group, using these instructions: How do I view my Canvas groups as a student?
  3. Click on the Discussions link in the left sidebar (Figure 1).
  4. Click the Start a Post button (shown by the orange arrow in the image below), and add a greeting message to the rest of your group for your first post. Share you’d like your group to know.

    NOTE: If another group member has already posted, you can also click on the Reply button (marked by the blue arrow in the image above) to respond to their comments for an alternative first post.
  5. Connect with other group members by replying to their posts for additional posts.
  6. Add your contact information to the Contact-Info-Form by following these steps:
    1. Click on the Files tab, above the channel messages (shown by the maroon arrow in the image above).
    2. Click on the Contact-Info-Form.docx file in the file list (marked by the maroon arrow in the image below).
    3. Follow the instructions in the contact info form to add your details to the group list.
      NOTE: If your information changes during the term, be sure to return to this form and update it.
    4. Close the form tab when you are done adding your information. The form saves automatically.
  7. Explore Teams more if you’d like. Otherwise, close the Teams tab as well.
  8. Fill in the true/false quiz below to claim a Complete for this activity in Canvas.

Assessment

Canvas automatically marks your work when you answer the questions below. There are three required tasks for this activity:

  • A greeting post in your private channel in Teams.
  • A reply to at least one other group member in your private channel in Teams.
  • Contact information added to the contact-info-form.docx file in your private channel in Teams.

If I find that you did not complete all three of these tasks, I will remove the Complete mark. If the grace period has not ended, you can revise.

In your first post, be sure to mention these things:

  • Provide a bit of info about yourself (name, major, hometown, hobbies, anything that makes you YOU).
  • Talk about your schedule for the term and how you like to work so that you can begin to coordinate your workflows.

In your second (and additional) posts, connect with other group members and negotiate how you will work together.

Optionally, you can talk about how to inform one another if you’ll be offline for several days as well as how to connect if someone isn’t responding. Make whatever communication plans you want as a team, and use the Teamwork & Collaboration Guide as a resource.
