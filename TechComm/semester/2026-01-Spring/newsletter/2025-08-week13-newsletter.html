üóÇÔ∏è From Report to Fact Sheet

Your informational report covered everything about your topic. Now it’s time to compress it into a single page (front and back) that anyone can understand.

Think of your fact sheet as the highlight reel of your report. Keep only what a reader truly needs to know:

  • The most essential facts or definitions.
  • Visuals that clarify your point.
  • A short introduction and a clear structure of sections.

Each fact sheet should stand on its own, giving the reader all the information to understand your topic.

üìä Pick the Right Chart, Not the Flashy One

When you choose a chart, think about what you want readers to understand in the first five seconds. A clean, well-labeled visual beats a fancy one every time.

  • Bar charts: Best for comparing categories.
  • Line graphs: Show trends or change over time.
  • Pie charts: Use sparingly for simple parts of a whole.
  • Scatter plots: Reveal relationships between variables.
‚úÖ Tip: If the takeaway isn’t obvious without reading the text, the chart needs a clearer title, labels, or scale.

üë• Know Your New Audience

Fact sheets reach non-experts, not specialists. Your readers might be community members, volunteers, or policymakers with limited time.

Use plain, direct language and visuals that speak for themselves. Replace insider terms with everyday ones, and define anything technical in simple phrasing. The goal isn’t to simplify your subject—it’s to make it accessible.

üí° Try this test: Ask a roommate or friend outside your major to read your draft. If they can summarize it in one sentence, you’ve hit the mark.

ü™∂ Writing with Plain Language

Plain style doesn’t mean simple-minded; it means reader-friendly. The key techniques to plain language are the following:

  • Choose short, direct sentences and common words.
  • Replace jargon with everyday terms unless precision requires otherwise.
  • Break up long paragraphs.
  • Use topic sentences.
  • Rely on active verbs.
  • Read aloud—if a sentence feels heavy or abstract, simplify it.
