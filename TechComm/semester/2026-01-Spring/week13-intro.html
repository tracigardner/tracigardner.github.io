Topic Overview

In this module, you’ll shift your focus from writing to decision-makers to writing for the public. You’ve already researched an Ut Prosim project related to your major and written a detailed report aimed at leaders or policymakers. Now, your task is to take that same topic and translate it into a one-page (front and back) fact sheet designed for the people those leaders serve, like local community members, constituents, or everyday users.

To do this effectively, you’ll need to simplify complex information, highlight what matters most, and use visuals to help communicate key facts.

Two essential concepts about which you will be learning this module are data visualizations and fact sheets. Understanding them will help you create a document that is not only clear and usable, but also helpful to your readers.

Data Visualizations

Data visualizations are charts, graphs, or other visuals that make numbers and data easier to understand. Good data visualizations can highlight trends, show comparisons, or make abstract data feel more real. In technical writing, they help readers grasp information quickly and clearly. For social justice efforts, they make important facts more visible and understandable to broader audiences.

Fact Sheets

A fact sheet is a short, one-page document that presents key information about a topic in a clear, organized way. It often includes headings, bullet points, visuals, and plain language. In technical writing, fact sheets help simplify complex topics for non-experts. They also support social justice by making information more accessible to those who may not have the time, resources, or background to read full reports.

 

Module Objectives for this Unit

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

  • Translate complex information into a visually engaging, easy-to-read fact sheet for a general audience.
  • Use plain language and clear design strategies to improve accessibility and readability.
  • Identify the most relevant data from your research and select data visualizations that clearly support your message.
  • Apply basic principles of ethical communication to ensure your fact sheet is accurate, fair, and inclusive.
  • Explain how well-designed public-facing documents can increase understanding, equity, and access to information.
