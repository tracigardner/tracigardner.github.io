Tengrrl’s Do’s & Don’ts
for Workplace Writing

Successful documents are ready for the workplace. Don’t include the name of the assignment, the class, or other course information.
01
Ready for the Workplace
02
Break Up Large Paragraphs
Long paragraphs confront the reader with a difficult-to-read wall of text. Chunk long paragraphs into smaller elements to increase readability.
Left-aligned, ragged right paragraphs and headings are the easiest to read. Centered and justified paragraphs are more difficult to scan and follow, especially in longer passages.
03
Left Align for Readability
04
All Visuals Need Captions
Include clear, consistent captions so readers understand what they’re seeing and why it matters. Follow a two-part format: a label and number (e.g., Figure 1:) followed by a brief, descriptive title (e.g., How a Dog’s Teeth Are Arranged).
Letters and memos have distinct structures and conventions, so don’t mix elements or leave out key parts. Use the appropriate format to ensure your document is clear, complete, and professional.
05
Use the Correct Format
06
Links Are Not a Bibliography
Use bibliographical citations on your references page, no matter what citation system your field uses. Links are NOT citations. They lack the details needed to identify and retrieve a source reliably.
Place connected information together on the same page. Keep headings with their opening paragraphs and pair visuals with their captions and references. Make the most of the design principle of proximity.
07
Keep Related Content Together
08
Limit All Caps
Avoid all caps in body text and headings, since it’s harder to read. Save all caps for purposeful uses—like acronyms—where they can add emphasis without “yelling” at the reader.
Text should be easy to read at a glance. Use at least 10–12 pt font in your document, and keep text in visuals the same size or larger so readers don’t have to strain to see it.
09
Use Readable Font Size
10
Use Title Case
Use title case for document titles, headings, subheadings, and subject lines to create a professional look. Capitalize the first and last words and all major words. Leave short articles, conjunctions, and prepositions lowercase unless they begin or end the line.
