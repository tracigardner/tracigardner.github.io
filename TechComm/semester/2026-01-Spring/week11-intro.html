It’s Week 11 of the course. You’ll continue work on your Informational Report for Non-Expert Readers, and you’ll compose your Letter of Transmittal for the report.

A letter of transmittal is essentially a cover letter for something you are sending to a reader. In our case, the letter of transmittal introduces your informational report to your readers. It’s the first thing your readers will see, so aim to make a strong first impression with it.

Module Objectives for this Unit

By the end of this unit, you will be able to:

  • Research and explain a technical or complex subject clearly for a non-expert audience.
  • Describe how a topic addresses or relates to fairness, accessibility, and the well-being of communities.
  • Write a professional letter of transmittal that introduces and contextualizes your final report.
  • Apply core technical writing skills—such as clarity, organization, and audience awareness—in support of socially meaningful communication.
