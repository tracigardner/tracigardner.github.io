I will also comment on your work generally, and suggest what you can do to improve your project if I asked you to revise.

If You Have an Incomplete

If you have an incomplete marked for your User Document Assignment, you can still earn a Complete on the project.

  • Read the feedback in annotations on your submission, in the rubric, and in the summary comment.
  • Revise your project to improve your work, adding whatever is missing or revising the information to include more (or accurate) details.

Resubmit your project by 11:59 PM on Wednesday, November 12, and I will review your work again. No more revisions on this project after 11:59 PM on Wednesday, November 12.

Specific Feedback

  • Include the memo title and all memo headers. See 9c. Memos & Their Components in the textbook.
  • Do not add a salutation (greeting) or closing and signature in your memo. See 9c. Memos & Their Components in the textbook.
  • Place your cover memo at the beginning of the document (not the end).
  • Include a 2-part caption below your visuals. See the assignment and 7c. Integrating Visuals into Your Documents in the textbook.
  • Explain every visual fully and include a specific reference to the visual by Figure number, both before the visual appears in the text of the document. See 7c. Integrating Visuals into Your Documents in the textbook.
  • Make sure that the text in your visuals is large enough to be read easily. As a guideline, the font size in the visuals needs to be at least as big as the font size of the text in the document. 
  • If you have step-by-step directions in your User Document, ensure that each step uses an action verb and includes only one action in each step or substep. See 11c. Guidelines for Setting Up Your Directions in the textbook.
  • Proofread. Use the grammar and spell checker in your word processor. No workplace wants to send out documents that are full of typos. Review your work to make sure you catch these errors.
  • Get help from the Writing Center (optional). Anyone can visit the Writing Center in the library for help with projects for the course. Writing Center tutors can help you strengthen your content as well as work on document design and other issues. You can read more about the Writing Center and make an appointment on the Writing Center’s webpage.
