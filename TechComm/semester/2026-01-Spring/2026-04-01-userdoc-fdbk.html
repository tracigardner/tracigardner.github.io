I have added feedback on all of the User Documents. In this post, I’ll share details on your grades and how to use the feedback to improve your writing in the course.

Feedback on Your User Document

Find comments on your User Documents in three possible places:

  • Annotations on your submission. Look for comments on or to the left of your document.

  • Comments in the rubric. Read the description listed in the rubric as well as any custom comments added for specific characteristics.

  • Summary comments that show when you look at the assignment.

Revision for the User Document

If you earned an 89 or less on your User Document, you can revise and resubmit your document. The grade for your revision will replace your current grade. There is no penalty for revision. If you decide not to revise, your grade will stand as is.

User Document Revision Dates

  • The deadline for revision of your User Document is Thursday, April 9 at 11:59 PM.

  • The grace period for User Document revisions is Tuesday, April 14 at 11:59 PM.

How to Resubmit

If you eligible to submit a revision, go to the User Document: Creating Step-by-Step Instructions, and upload your new attempt.

Six Resources to Help You Revise

  1. Review the list of tips on the SSM Feedback Announcement.

  2. Check your memo format against the Memo Formatting page.

  3. See the Choosing a Task for Your User Document page for help narrowing your topic and audience.

  4. Use the example instructions for examples of the information that belongs in each section.

  5. Check previous weekly newsletter Announcements for advice.

  6. Get help from the Writing Center. Anyone can visit the Writing Center in the library for help with projects for the course. Writing Center tutors can help you strengthen your content as well as work on document design and other issues. You can read more about the Writing Center and make an appointment on the Writing Center’s webpage.
