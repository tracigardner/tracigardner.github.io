Grace Period by Assignment Type

  • Check-Ins: Short, 3-day grace period. Their Target Due Date each week is on Friday, and the Grace Period ends on the following Wednesday. Why? Because Check-Ins tell me how things are going at a particular point in time. They lose their usefulness if the response is delayed.
  • Try-It Discussions: Short, 3-day grace period. Their Target Due Date each week is on Friday, and the Grace Period ends on the following Wednesday. Why? Because Discussions need to take place in a timely manner. You can’t carry on a conversation unless everyone participates at the same time.
  • Try-Its from Weeks 2 to 6 (other than Try-It Discussions): Open grace period that ends on December 1. Why? Because these are individual activities that do not depend upon anyone else in the class.
  • Try-Its from Week 7 to the end of the term: Short, 3-day grace period. Their Target Due Date each week is on Friday, and the Grace Period ends on the following Wednesday. Why? Because of the impact these Try-Its have on group writing projects. Group work has to keep on track so that everyone in the group can participate and finish the projects on time.
  • The first two Major Projects (the Survey and the User Documentation Revision and Memo): Open grace period that ends on December 1. Why? Because these are individual activities that do not depend upon anyone else in the class.
  • The remaining three Major Projects (the Proposal, the Progress Report, and the Recommendation Report): Writing groups agree on the end of the grace period. Everyone in the group must have their work turned in by the dates that the groups choose. Why? Because these are group writing projects, everyone has to participate and finish the projects on time.
  • The final exam: No grace period. If you choose to submit the optional final, you must submit it by the end of the exam week. Why? The university expects the end of exam week to be the last moment that exams can be submitted.
