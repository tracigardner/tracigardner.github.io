From Sherri:

When this happens I ask for the AWOL person to meet with me. If they show up to the meeting, I give them a chance to get their act together within the next week. If they fail to do so or fail to show up, the next step is to put them into a group of other AWOL students. At least that way they all struggle together and they see the impact they had on other students. Sometimes in online async classes there's no saving the student. If I put them on the project as a single then the highest they can earn is an 80% because they had not completed a core component of the project, which is collaboration. I explain all this in writing through the initial email.

I also use scary language like "You are at risk of failure" in my correspondence. In addition to group contracts, there is a three strikes process I introduce with the project: 1) Warning, 2) Meeting with me, 3) Removal from group and 20% project grade reduction.

Nothing helps some students but the groups feel better knowing I take the lack of participation seriously.
