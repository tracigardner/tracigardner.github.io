Start with Wikipedia

It may seem like an odd thing for a teacher to suggest, but you might start your research process by finding relevant Wikipedia entries. Believe it or not, Wikipedia is a great place to start research. Just don‚Äôt end your research there.

  1. Go to a Wikipedia page matching the topic you‚Äôre exploring in your Recommendation Report.
  2. Skim through the page for background information and for a quick overview of other details on the topic.
  3. Check links under the See Also heading, following any that seem relevant to find related information.
  4. Skim through the References or Works Cited section to find further sources you can use.
  5. Visit the sites listed in the External Links section, which can yield more details for your report.
