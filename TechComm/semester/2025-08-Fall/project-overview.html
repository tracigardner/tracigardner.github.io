Virginia Tech’s motto, Ut Prosim (That I May Serve) is not just part of the university logo; it is a guiding principle that shapes how Hokies approach learning, research, and professional life. This semester-long writing project is designed in that spirit.

Over the course of this term, you will research, write, and design a series of interconnected documents about a subject rooted in your major that serves others and contributes to the greater good. Each assignment builds toward your ability to explain a complex topic clearly and accessibly to different real-world audiences, always with the goal of informing, guiding, or supporting action that contributes to fairness, accessibility, and the well-being of communities.

This page explains the focus of the project and introduces each of the five major projects.

Project Focus: Writing that Serves Others

You get to choose the subject on which you will focus your work this semester. The caveat is that it must be a technical object, practice, or idea within your field that has the potential to create a positive social impact. In the spirit of Ut Prosim, your topic should be:

  • Grounded in your major or area of study.
  • Clearly connected to community service, problem-solving, or public good.
  • Feasible to research and explain to multiple non-expert audiences.

Examples include the following:

  • Accessible design standards for public websites
  • Water purification systems for underserved areas
  • Renewable energy solutions for communities with limited resources
  • Assistive technologies that promote independence
