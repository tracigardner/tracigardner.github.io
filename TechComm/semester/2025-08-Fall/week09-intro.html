Unit Overview: Informational Report

This week, we begin writing the major informational report for non-expert readers, the longest document in the course. Your report defines and explains a subject rooted in your major that serves others and contributes to the greater good (an Ut Prosim project). You’ll research the topic thoroughly and write about it clearly, with attention to how it addresses a real-world need or challenge.

The Genres We’ll Cover in this Unit

During the coming weeks, we will explore three document types—informational reports, progress reports, and letters of transmittal . These document genres are all important tools in technical and professional writing. Understanding how and why they’re used will help you produce strong, ethical documents that inform and empower readers, especially in contexts where access to good information supports fairness and informed decision-making.

Week 9 Focus: Informational Reports

This week, our focus is on the informational report for non-expert readers itself. This genre explains a topic or issue in clear, objective detail for a specific audience.

Why it matters: Informational reports help non-experts understand complex topics. In technical writing, they support informed decisions, promote understanding, and—when focused on equity or justice—can help spread knowledge that supports positive social change.

Module Objectives for this Unit

By the end of this unit, you will be able to:

  • Research and explain a technical or complex subject clearly for a non-expert audience.
  • Describe how a topic addresses or relates to fairness, accessibility, and the well-being of communities.
  • Track and reflect on your progress using a well-structured progress report.
  • Write a professional letter of transmittal that introduces and contextualizes your final report.
  • Apply core technical writing skills—such as clarity, organization, and audience awareness—in support of socially meaningful communication.
