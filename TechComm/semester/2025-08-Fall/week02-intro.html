Topic Overview

This module continues exploration of the basic underlying principles of technical writing: rhetoric, ethics, and audience. This week, we’ll focus on ethics, audience, and organization.

Ethics

Technical writing is never 100% neutral. Writers make ethical choices about accuracy, fairness, confidentiality, and professionalism, all of which impact how information is presented. As a result, technical writers need to guard against bias, present information clearly, and prevent harm by communicating responsibly.

Audience & Accessibility

Technical communication is always audience-centered. Writers must consider who their readers are, what barriers they face, and how to make information usable and inclusive. By analyzing expert and non-expert audiences, as well as primary, secondary, and tertiary readers, you’ll explore how accessibility is both a practical strategy and an ethical responsibility in technical writing.

Neutral-News Organization

The neutral news message is one of the simplest and most common formats in technical writing. Because it delivers good or neutral information, it follows a clear three-step structure: state the news upfront, provide the necessary details, and close cordially. Mastering this straightforward pattern will help you communicate routine updates, approvals, or requests with clarity and efficiency.

Module Learning Outcomes

Once you complete this module, you should be able to

  • Demonstrate how rhetoric, ethics, and audience awareness work together in technical writing decisions
  • Critique existing technical documents using these three frameworks
  • Design user-centered documents that are rhetorically effective, ethically sound, and audience-appropriate
  • Justify writing choices by referencing principles of rhetoric, ethics, and audience awareness
