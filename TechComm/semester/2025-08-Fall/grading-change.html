Why It Matters

Our program increased the number of major projects and set them up as weighted categories this semester, and my Complete/Incomplete system isn’t working fairly under the new program. The weighted assignments can put you at a disadvantage. Here’s how:

Earn an Incomplete on the Information Report



Run Out of Time and Can’t Revise



End Up with a Zero for 20% of the Course Grade





Even worse, the same thing can happen with the Fact Sheet, and another 10% is lost! A Zero is not a fair representation of anyone’s work on these projects—so I changed the system.

How the System Is Changing

What’s Effected

  • The Informational Report for Non-Expert Readers
  • The Fact Sheet

NOTE: Everything is working fine for the Homework, Quizzes, Check-Ins, and Self-Checks. No changes needed there.

What Changes

  • The rubrics for the projects include partial credit for each of the Ratings levels.
  • These two projects are worth 100 points (see more details in the “Converting from 1-Point to 100-Point Scale” section below).
  • Now it’s harder to earn a Zero on these projects. The only way to earn a Zero is not to submit the project OR to submit something that has nothing to do with the assignment.

Converting from 1-Point to 100-Point Scale

If you want more details, read on. Originally, I tried using the 0/1 system, simply adding partial scores across the levels in the rubric, shown in the second column of Table 1 below:

Table 1. Rubric Scale Comparison by Level
Level 1-Point Scale 100-Point Scale
Exceeds Expectations 0.12 12
Meets Expectations 0.10 10
Almost There 0.07   7
Needs Work 0.05   5
Missing 0.00   0

That adjustment worked, but it seemed silly to mess with decimal points. I switched to 100 points and adjusted the levels in the rubric, as shown in the third column of Table 1. The result will be scores that align with the VT Grade Scale with +/- (see Table 2 below).
