Proposing a Project (M 9.8-Su 9.28)

In this module, you'll learn how to write a clear, well-organized research proposal on the topic you chose in Module 1. Writing a proposal helps you plan a project and explain why it matters. To do this well, you’ll need to understand how to present your ideas using plain language and how to support your plan with research. These concepts are key in technical writing, where your audience often needs to understand complex ideas quickly and easily.

During this module, you will work with the following genres and concepts.

Research Proposal

A research proposal is a plan for a project. It explains what you want to research, why it’s important, how you’ll do it, and what your timeline or steps will be. In technical writing, proposals are used to request funding, approval, or support for projects. A strong proposal shows your ability to plan, organize, and communicate clearly.

Plain Language

Plain language means writing that is clear, direct, and easy to understand. It avoids jargon and long, complicated sentences. In technical writing, plain language helps readers—especially non-experts—quickly grasp important information and make decisions based on what they’ve read.

 

  Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

  1. Identify the purpose and key components of a research proposal.
  2. Choose and define a focused research topic that relates to your major and promotes community benefit, equity, or social relevance.
  3. Write clearly and concisely using plain language techniques.
  4. Support your proposal with credible background research.
  5. Organize and format your proposal to meet professional standards.
