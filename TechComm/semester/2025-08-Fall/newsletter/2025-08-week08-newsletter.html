The right visuals clarify tasks; the wrong ones confuse.

Use Images When They…

  • Show a process or part that’s hard to describe in words.
  • Reinforce—not replace—written instructions.
  • Are simple, cropped, and clearly labeled.

Always Add Clear Captions

A caption tells readers what they’re seeing and why it matters.

üî¢ Numbered vs. Bulleted Lists

Lists improve readability—but only if you choose the right kind.

Use Numbered Lists When Order Matters

  1. Plug in the device.
  2. Press the power button.
  3. Wait for the indicator light to turn green.

Use Bulleted Lists When Order Doesn’t Matter

  • Phillips-head screwdriver
  • Two AA batteries
  • Replacement filter

Quick rule: If users must follow steps, number them. If they just need to check items, use bullets.

Use Strong Verbs

Readers will use your User Document to do something, so your verbs should help them act. Clear, direct language helps readers move confidently from one step to the next.

What Are Imperative Verbs?

The imperative mood gives a command or instruction. It tells the reader exactly what to do. In user documents, imperative verbs make directions clear and actionable:

  • Click the Start button.
  • Turn the knob clockwise until it clicks.
  • Save your file before closing the app.

These sentences are short, direct, and easy to follow because the verb comes first and gives the user a clear action.

What Is Passive Voice?

Passive voice hides the doer of the action or shifts focus away from the user. In passive constructions, the object comes before the verb and the person acting is added at the end (often with by):

  • The Start button should be clicked by the user.
  • The file will be saved by the system.

These versions sound indirect and wordy. They make actions seem less urgent and can confuse readers who need quick, confident steps.

Bottom Line: Write with Active, Directive Energy

Prefer active voice and imperative verbs in your instructions. They give readers momentum and make your writing sound professional and efficient.
