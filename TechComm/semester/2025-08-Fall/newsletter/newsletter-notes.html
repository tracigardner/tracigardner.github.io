Use “You” for Actions

A simple way to make your proposal clearer is to use you-attitude—writing directly to your reader with “you” instead of abstract terms like “the user” or “the applicant.” Extend you-attitude to your pronouns as well. Use “your community” rather than “the community.”

This approach shifts the focus from the writer or the process to the person who must act, making steps in methods, timelines, and deliverables easier to follow.

You-attitude helps your audience see themselves in the document. It also cuts wordiness and turns vague requirements into specific actions. In short: it respects your reader’s time and makes your proposal feel practical and usable.
