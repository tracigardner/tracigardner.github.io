Know Your Audience

For your Informational Report for Non-Expert Readers, your readers aren’t specialists. They do not know about your topic—and they need to!

You readers will use your information to make a decision. That means your success depends on how clearly you write for non-experts with authority.

Advice for Your Audience Analysis

This week, you’ll complete your Informational Report Audience Analysis. Before you begin the activity, make sure you can answer these two questions with a strong “Yes!”:

  • Do your readers have limited knowledge about your topic?
  • Do you the decision(s) your readers need to make?

What They Need

  • Definitions: Explain key terms the first time you use them.
  • Plain language: Use short sentences and familiar words.
  • Balanced facts: Present multiple expert viewpoints fairly.
  • Visuals that teach: Use charts, maps, or photos that answer real questions.

Keep It Objective

These readers must trust your neutrality. Avoid opinion statements, loaded words, and advocacy. Let evidence and expert sources speak for themselves so your readers can make their own informed choice.

Quick Tip

Before drafting, write one sentence that answers this question: What decision will my audience make after reading this report? Use that answer to guide what you explain—and what you leave out.
