Course Description

English 3764: Technical Writing will enable you to gain an understanding of the theories, issues, and practices of technical communications you might encounter in the workplace, such as general correspondence, proposals, reports, and presentations.

From a foundation based on the principles of rhetoric, you will learn to analyze writing situations, consider the needs of your audience, assemble evidence, and design final documents that are user-friendly and visually persuasive. By the time this course is over, you should be able to understand these documents and produce them with ease and confidence.

Course Objectives

Having successfully completed this course, students will be able to perform the technical writing tasks defined by the learning objectives for the course:

  • Analyze the rhetorical situation and determine the appropriate audience or users of written communication, considering the needs of global audiences and people with disabilities.
  • Conduct research appropriate to workplace problem solving, such as literature review, evaluation of online resources, interview, and site inspection.
  • Interpret research findings with understanding of ethical and human implications.
  • Use conventions of various workplace genres, such as proposals, instructions, correspondence, reports, and slide decks, with understanding of how the genre conventions can be used as heuristics and as principles of arrangement.
  • Collaborate with classmates in planning, researching, writing, revising, and presenting information.
  • Apply principles of effective visual design for print and electronic presentation, including hierarchical, chronological, and spatial arrangements.
  • Identify and apply the principles of effective style in the composing of usable, reader-centered written communications.

Course Expectations

This course is 100% virtual; that is, we will never meet in the classroom. As a result, it is important that you understand how the course will be conducted and what will be expected of you as a student enrolled in it.

As your teacher, it is my responsibility to (among other things)

  • Answer any questions you might have that are not covered by the textbook or another reading,
  • Provide feedback to you about your work,
  • Grade your work as promptly as possible,
  • Use a grading rubric to help explain your grades,
  • Update your grades on Canvas, and
  • Inform you promptly of any alterations to the schedule, such as due date changes.

That said, in order to do well in this course, you must follow these policies:

Read All Announcements

      • Announcements are the primary way I keep in touch with you, so it's imperative that you read every single one in its entirety. Not reading announcements will not be an excuse for not submitting work on time should a due date change.

Be Aware of Due Dates/Times

      • Most (if not all) assignments are set to be due by 11:59 pm. You can, of course, upload your work earlier. All work should be uploaded in the appropriate manner to the appropriate page on the Canvas site.

Read All Materials and Review Examples

      • The textbook for this course was created especially for ENGL 3764: Technical Writing. You will be quizzed over most chapters.
      • You will also find on the Sample Documents page examples of the major assignments. Review them. Pay attention to them.
      • If a checklist is provided for an assignment (Checklists page), please use it.

Apply What You are Learning

      • Do your best to apply what you have read and the lessons you have learned from reviewing examples to producing and improving your own work.

Follow Assignment Instructions

      • Each assignment provides you with a description of the deliverable and the specifications, including page length, formatting, and other requirements. Assignments other than discussions include checklists that are designed to help you cover all requirements.

Conduct Yourself Professionally

    • For this course, just as for one that meets in a classroom, you are expected to be civil to other participants and to the instructor, to apply what you are learning about professional correspondence to any messages or discussion posts, and to contribute conscientiously to any discussions and group assignments.

Course Policies

Absences

      • Since we do not meet in the classroom, there is no formal attendance policy.
      • There is one overriding policy: Don't disappear and expect to make up missed work (daily work or major assignments). This won't be possible. If you find yourself in this situation, either drop the course or withdraw from it.
      • In the end, you will get the grade you get.
      • If circumstances prevent participation or timely completion of assignments, you must contact me in advance of due dates.

AI, Working with

      • You are not required to use AI in this course. If you choose to do so, you will need to follow the procedure outlined on the Using AI Ethically in This Course page.
      • Any assistance you receive from AI should be cited, just like any other source. Get in the habit of indicating when AI has been consulted.
      • For this class, do this with every assignment for which you used AI:
        1. Insert a footnote in ONE of two places:
          • The document title (within the document itself) or
          • At the end of your name in the From line (if a memo).
        2. Include in your actual footnote the ways you consulted AI for the assignment.
          • You can write something as simple as "For this assignment, I used AI in the brainstorming process, to create a stronger outline, and to review my draft and final version."
          • Be comprehensive!

Incompletes

      • Incompletes are given only in provable emergency situations -- and only if you have completed at least 75% of the coursework. Incompletes will not be given in situations where you might have disappeared early in the class and then reappeared later. Be aware of drop and withdrawal dates.

Participation

      • Participants in this course should expect to spend about 8-10 hours per week involved in the activities and completion of assignments over the semester. This is similar to the time one would invest in a course in a traditional classroom setting.
      • This time invested is an average, with some weeks requiring more and some less time to complete all assignments and activities. Participation will be evaluated as the completion of all activities within the unit or module period.

Assessment

  • Every assignment in this course is worth 100 points.
  • Unless otherwise noted on the assignment page, all major assignments, homework, discussion posts, and quizzes will be due by 11:59 on their designated days. (See individual assignments.)
  • Plus and minus grades are used.
  • Your assignments will be graded as soon as reasonably possible.
  • If you have a questions/concerns/issues with a grade(s), please write or see me as soon as you can.
  • Final grades are rounded up. Individual assignment grades are not.
  • Please do not write at the end of the semester to tell me you need a grade of X in this course. I will not raise your grade beyond what is merited by your final average.

Submission Guidelines

  • Unless otherwise noted, all major and homework assignments will be uploaded directly to the specific assignment page. That page will identify the type of file to be uploaded (.doc, .docx, .pdf). 
    • Read the assignment carefully, as sometimes the assignment requirements will note the form your submission should take.
  • Naming convention for all assignments: YourLastName_Assignment_Date or YourLastName-Assignment-Date. (EX: Smith_Proposal_Sp25 or Smith-Proposal-Sp25).

Due Dates & Late Assignments

    • Extension Requests:
      • Major Assignments: You can request a 24-hour extension on ONE major assignment, but you must email me with the request 24 hours or more ahead of the due date. You'll get that extension -- no questions asked. Once you have used your one extension, any late work will be reduced one letter grade per day after the due date.
      • Drafts & Peer Evaluations: No extensions. This is the closest you will have to get to "group work" in this course, so your participation is required.
      • Homework: No extensions for homework assignments. You get to drop your lowest homework grade, so if you miss one, it won't count against you.
      • Quizzes: No extensions for quizzes. You get to drop your lowest quiz grade as well.
      • Discussions: No extensions for discussions because that totally defeats the point of having a discussion.
