In last week’s Check-In Survey, I asked this question:

Which of these concepts for the Survey of Website Writing in your Field Informal Report would you like me to explain in more detail?

Here’s how you responded:

◼ 26% Genre (what type of writing is it?)

◼ 26% Purpose of the type of writing (what is it meant to do?)

◼   9% Traditional audience for it

◼   2% Usual mode of delivery (paper, electronic, etc.)

◼ 37% Usual length

Here are more details:

  • Genre
    The word genre means the type or kind of something. If we were talking about music, different genres would be rock, pop, rap, jazz, and so forth. Find more information on different genres of writing on the Resources for Identifying Types of Writing page.
  • Purpose
    The purpose of a piece of writing is its goal(s), whatever it is trying to accomplish. For instance, the purpose of a thank you note is to tell someone you appreciate something that they did. You can read more about purpose in the resources on Rhetorical Situation from the Week 2 Readings.
  • Audience
    The audience for a document is the person or people who it is meant for, the readers or viewers. There can be more than one audience for a document. For instance, a company’s annual report has a primary audience of company stakeholders, but it also is written for the public, for the media, and for company employees. You can read more about audience in the resources on Rhetorical Situation from the Week 2 Readings.
  • Mode of Delivery
    The mode of delivery for a text is the format that it’s produced in. It might be printed out on paper so that it can be mailed out. It could be delivered digitally, sent as an attachment to an email message.
  • Usual Length
    The length is straightforward: how long the text is. Usually there’s a range expressed as a number of pages, a number of minutes, or even a number of words or characters. For example, the usual length of a social media post on X is 280 characters or less.
