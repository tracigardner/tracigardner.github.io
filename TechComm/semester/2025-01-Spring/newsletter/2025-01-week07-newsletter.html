üîë Keys to Effective Collaboration

Strong teamwork is key to a successful group project, and successful collaboration takes planning and effort. The tips below address common concerns and provide strategies to help your team work efficiently and fairly. For more detailed guidance, be sure to check the Teamwork & Collaboration Guide.

 
 
 

‚öñÔ∏è Distributing Work Fairly

  • Set clear expectations early. Agree on individual responsibilities and document them in your group contract.
  • Use a shared tracking system. Google Docs, Trello, or a shared spreadsheet can help track contributions.
  • Rotate responsibilities. If someone is always proofreading or researching, switch roles for balance.
  • Communicate concerns proactively. If workload feels unbalanced, bring it up respectfully and adjust as needed.

üìÖ Scheduling Meetings & Work

  • Use scheduling tools. Find meeting times with When2Meet, Doodle, or Google Calendar.
  • Connect early in the week. You’ll have weekly meetings. Connect early so you’re not scrambling at the last minute.
  • Set internal deadlines. Break your work into smaller tasks and agree on draft due dates before the final deadline.
  • Be flexible but reliable. Respect everyone’s time by showing up prepared, keeping meetings focused, and communicating if plans change.

üìù Maintaining Quality

  • Establish a style guide. Agree on formatting, citation style, and terminology from the start.
  • Regularly review progress. Assign one or two members to check for consistency throughout the project.
  • Merge and revise together. Before submission, work as a group to integrate sections smoothly.
  • Give feedback constructively. Use formative feedback—suggest improvements rather than just pointing out mistakes.
