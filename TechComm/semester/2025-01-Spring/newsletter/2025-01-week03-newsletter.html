Opinions on Strong Writing

In a recent Check-In, you told me what makes a strong document. Your response valued concise writing the most—and I have to agree that it’s very important. After all, who wants to read a long, rambling text?

Here’s the percentage of the class that chose each criteria as most important to composing a strong piece of writing:

Percentage of the Class What Makes Writing Strong?
39% Strong writing is short and to the point.
35% Strong writing has a professional appearance
23% Strong writing has perfect spelling and grammar
3% Strong writing includes images and graphics

While concise writing and visual appeal matter, strong grammar and spelling lay the foundation for clarity and credibility. A well-written document with errors can undermine professionalism, while a visually polished piece that lacks clarity can still frustrate readers.

üì© Annoying Writing Mistakes

You also told me the writing issues that annoy you the most. The overwhelming majority—71%—said the worst offense is an email that makes no sense, even if the words are familiar.

Other pet peeves included:

  • 11% get frustrated when their name is misspelled.
  • 9% dislike emails with no subject line.
  • 6% feel annoyed by messages that seem too rushed or lazy.
  • 3% are bothered by tiny, unreadable fonts.

These results highlight the importance of clear, thoughtful communication. Writing that is hard to understand or appears careless can leave a lasting negative impression.

Takeaways for Your Writing

Based on these insights, here are some key takeaways to improve your technical writing:

  • Clarity is key‚Äîif your audience can’t understand your message, nothing else matters.
  • Grammar and spelling should be a priority to maintain professionalism.
  • Put effort into your writing‚Äîreaders can tell when something was rushed or careless.
  • Personal details, like correct names, matter in professional communication.

Keep these points in mind as you work on your assignments. Your writing isn’t just about delivering information‚Äîit’s about making sure your audience receives it clearly and effectively.
