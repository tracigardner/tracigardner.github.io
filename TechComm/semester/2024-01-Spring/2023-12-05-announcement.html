About the Rubric

The rubric for the Current Course Grade Estimate is based on the Effort Expectations chart. The rubric percentages come from the bottom of the Canvas Grades page (see an example in the Checking Your Progress section).

I marked the percentage on your rubric according to the number listed. For instance, if you had 85.71% for Weekly Activities, I marked the B rating for that category in the rubric. I added the ratings according to the percentage cut-offs. There is no rounding or bumping up.

I do have several strict guidelines, listed below and in the Short Guide under the Additional Grade Calculation Guidelines heading:

  • I do not average the percentages or make any other calculation with them to determine your grade.
  • The different categories listed on the Effort Expectations Contract are not equivalent. Major Projects is the most important category. It becomes your base grade, and then I move your estimate up or down based on the other categories (Project Wrappers, Weekly Activities, and Check-In Surveys).
  • You cannot pass the course without earning a Complete on at least TWO Major Projects.
  • If you have an F for the Major Projects category, you have an F as your course grade.
  • To earn an A or A- in the course, you must earn a Complete on ALL FIVE of the Major Projects.
  • To earn a Complete for the group projects, you must participate fully in the projects as well as submit documents that meet the basic criteria.
