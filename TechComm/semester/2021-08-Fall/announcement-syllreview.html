How can I address worries about time management, falling behind, etc.?
Schedule this class as if it were synchronous. Choose a time slot that works for you (like 11 AM MWF), put it in your calendar so that you get reminders, and do work during that time period. Don't leave it for "when you have time." That typically causes the most trouble if you forget or get busy.

How does the Late Policy work when there are no grades?
It doesn't do anything like drop your grade. Instead, you either do the work or you don't, so it's marked complete or incomplete. If you don't try, I'm going to assume you aren't putting in your best effort (unless I know about some problematic situation that came up, like being sick).

How do the two rounds of peer feedback work? Do I have to do both of them?
The idea of two rounds is more to help people who are working more slowly. Nearly all students I've had either choose the first round or the second-chance round. I don't remember anyone trying both, but you can. You do provide feedback to 2 other people whenever you participate (but you get feedback from 2 people too). The activity is essentially the same. It's just the time frame that changes.
Can you share some example topics for the Recommendation Report?
Sure. There is a huge range. Some examples are the following:
  • Making a place accessible (like where there is no wheelchair access)
  • Improving recycling systems
  • Implementing a small-scale solar system to a small cabin
  • Improving website functionality or accessibility
  • Adding sidewalks where there are none to improve safety
  • Choosing sustainable materials for construction
  • Changing a process to make it safer.
  • Conserving wildlife or a specific habitat
Note that whatever you choose, you need to make it more specific than those listed. For instance, not conserving all the fish in the world, but a specific species in a specific location.
