Progress Reports: Multimedia Examples

Progress and status reports have traditionally been created to look like paper-based documents, which may be distributed in print or by electronic means. Multimedia tools, however, are sometimes a better choice, especially in the case of multimedia projects. Rather than describing progress on a project, a multimedia format for a progress or status report allows the writer to demonstrate changes in ways that make the work to date clear.

At the simplest level, this kind of progress can often be shown in side-by-side photos that show a before and an after view. In more sophisticated reports, this progress is explained in video format.
