To avoid being accused of spreading untrue information, be a fact checker. When you write a document in the workplace, your first task is to compose the document; but before you send that project out to your readers, you need to do some fact checking to verify the ideas and data.

You know all about fact checking from the news. Fact checking isn’t just for political speeches however. In the same way that you will double check your calculations in a budget, you need to confirm the facts and sources that you include in your report.
