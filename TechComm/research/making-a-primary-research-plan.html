Goals

  • Analyze the rhetorical situation and determine the appropriate audience or users of written communication, considering the needs of global audiences and people with disabilities. [CLO 1]
  • Conduct research appropriate to workplace problem solving, such as literature review, evaluation of online resources, interview, and site inspection. [CLO 2]

The Task

What I Want You to Do

Identify the primary research strategies that will help you gather the information you need to establish the situation you are examining and to support the recommendation you are proposing.

Why I Want You to Do It

Primary research is a critical piece of your recommendation report. You need to research the situation completely to determine what is going on and how you can improve it. You need to decide on the best way to gather information about the situation and use that information to write your next project, the Instructions.

How You Do It

  1. Review the information in Markel and Selber, Chapter 6 in the “Conducting Primary Research” section (beginning on p. 131).
  2. Answer the questions below to explore how to use different primary research strategies and then to choose the best strategy (or strategies) to use for your report.

Obtaining the Points for Your Work

  1. Be sure to track your work in your Weekly Work Log.
  2. When you submit your work, the points will be recorded in Canvas Grades automatically. I will review your answers. If I find that you did not put in appropriate effort or did not do the expected work, I will adjust your grade accordingly.
